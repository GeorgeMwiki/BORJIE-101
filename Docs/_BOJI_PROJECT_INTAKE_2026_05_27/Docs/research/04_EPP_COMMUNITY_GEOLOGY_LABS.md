## 1. The Environmental Protection Plan (EPP) for PML holders

In Tanzania, every Primary Mining Licence (PML) — the small-scale, 7-year renewable tenure designed for artisanal and small-scale miners ([Bowmans](https://bowmanslaw.com/insights/tanzania-mining-technical-support-for-primary-mining-licence-holders-regulations-published/)) — must be accompanied by an **Environmental Protection Plan (EPP)** rather than a full EIA. The EPP is an abridged, NEMC-supervised instrument issued under the umbrella of the Environmental Management Act, 2004 ([FAOLEX EMA 2004](https://faolex.fao.org/docs/pdf/tan61491.pdf)) and the Environmental Management (EIA and Audit) Regulations, 2005 as amended in 2018 ([ELAW Tanzania](https://elaw.org/elm/tanzania)).

The EPP form, downloadable from the Mining Commission (Tume ya Madini) and NEMC offices ([Tume ya Madini guidelines](https://www.tumemadini.go.tz/pages/applicationprocedure/)), typically requires: applicant identity and PML number; coordinates of the licence polygon; project description (target mineral, expected tonnage, processing route — gravity, sluice, cyanide, mercury, etc.); site map and access road; baseline of soil, water, vegetation and air; identified impacts (vegetation loss, dust, noise, dewatering, mercury contamination, AMD risk); a mitigation matrix; a rehabilitation plan with cost estimate; an emergency response plan; and signatures.

**Sign-off chain.** The EPP must be prepared by a NEMC-registered EIA expert (Environmental Expert, individual or firm), reviewed and counter-signed by the **District Environmental Management Officer (DEMO)**, and lodged with NEMC's Zonal Office for issue of an **Environmental Protection Certificate** (the small-scale equivalent of an EIA Certificate). Certified private practitioners on NEMC's expert register can act as the principal author. Government Notice No. 260 of 2025 — the new Mining (Technical Support for Primary Mining Licence Holders) Regulations — formalised a *facilitator* role: a credentialled professional who works alongside the PML holder to produce the mining plan within 90 days and the EIA-equivalent plan within 120 days ([Bowmans 2025](https://bowmanslaw.com/insights/tanzania-mining-technical-support-for-primary-mining-licence-holders-regulations-published/)).

**Timeline & cost.** A typical EPP cycle runs 6–12 weeks (baseline field visit, drafting, DEMO review, NEMC zonal sign-off). Professional fees in 2025–2026 range from TZS 1.5M–6M depending on site complexity, plus statutory NEMC charges. Common rejection reasons documented by FADev and NEMC zonal reviews ([FADev policy analysis](https://www.fadev.or.tz/policy-analysis-report-final-english-version/)): missing baseline data, inadequate rehabilitation cost provision, no mercury-handling protocol where amalgamation is implied, overlap with a protected area, no community consultation record, no signed DEMO sheet, and coordinates that do not match the cadastre.

**Why EPP must precede any clearing.** EMA s.81 prohibits commencement of a "project" (including excavation, road clearing, camp construction) without environmental clearance ([Lexology snapshot](https://www.lexology.com/library/detail.aspx?g=6bc13cfe-92e6-4592-a8e5-b5c4e87824d0)). Clearing before EPP approval voids the licence-holder's standing in any future bond release and exposes the holder to a NEMC stop-order. There is a recognised regulatory tension — NEMC's enforcement teeth sit in EMA while EPP lives outside it ([Lexology](https://www.lexology.com/library/detail.aspx?g=6bc13cfe-92e6-4592-a8e5-b5c4e87824d0)) — but in operational practice, NEMC's regional inspectors do treat the EPP as gating.

## 2. The full EIA threshold — ML and SML

For a **Mining Licence (ML)** or **Special Mining Licence (SML)**, the abridged EPP is *not* enough. Under EMA 2004 and the EIA & Audit Regulations 2005/2018, mining is a **Type A project** — a full Environmental and Social Impact Assessment is mandatory ([Clyde & Co ESG](https://www.clydeco.com/en/insights/2024/10/esg-and-its-regulatory-framework-in-tanzania), [Lexology snapshot](https://www.lexology.com/library/detail.aspx?g=6bc13cfe-92e6-4592-a8e5-b5c4e87824d0)). The Mining Act explicitly bars issuance of an ML/SML until an EIA Certificate has been granted.

The cycle: **(a) registration of project** with NEMC; **(b) scoping report and Terms of Reference** approved by NEMC; **(c) baseline studies** (hydro, ecology, social, archaeology, air, noise); **(d) draft EIS** (Environmental Impact Statement); **(e) public hearing** with affected villages, district authorities and CSOs — minutes are part of the dossier; **(f) NEMC technical review**; **(g) Minister of State (VPO) issues the EIA Certificate**; **(h) post-decision conditions** including the Environmental Management Plan (EMP); **(i) Environmental Performance Bond / Rehabilitation Bond**; **(j) annual environmental audit** (s.31 EMA); **(k) decommissioning notice and final closure plan**.

**Bonds.** Tanzania currently runs a controversial **dual-bond** regime — a Rehabilitation Bond under the Mining Act administered by the Mining Commission, and a Decommissioning Bond under the Environmental Management (Environmental Performance Bond) Regulations 2024 administered by NEMC ([Mwebesa Law](https://mwebesalaw.co.tz/a-legal-quagmire-of-competing-bonds-prioritize-environmental-security-or-regulatory-efficiency/), [Clyde & Co 2024](https://www.clydeco.com/en/insights/2024/07/environment-management-regulations)). Bond release requires NEMC inspection within 90 days of a decommissioning notice. Closure planning follows the Tanzania Mine Closure Guidelines 2019 issued under s.19 of the Mining Act ([OGEL](https://www.ogel.org/legal-and-regulatory-detail.asp?key=23534)).

**Audit cycle.** Annual environmental audits with surprise inspections; s.22(h) of the Mining Act empowers the Commission to verify rehabilitation budgets and progressive closure provisioning.

## 3. Land, surface rights & compensation

Tanzanian land is held in three legal categories under the **Land Act 1999 (Cap.113)** and **Village Land Act 1999 (Cap.114)**: **General Land, Reserved Land, and Village Land** ([FAOLEX Village Land Act](https://faolex.fao.org/docs/pdf/tan53306.pdf), [Land Act 1999](https://procedures.tiseza.go.tz/media/The%20Land%20Act%201999.%20Cap%20113.pdf)). The village is the legal occupier of Village Land, sub-categorised into **communal, individual (granted CCRO), and reserved-for-future-use**. The **Village Council** administers day-to-day; the **Village Assembly** (all adults >18) is the supreme organ for material decisions ([DFID village governance study](https://assets.publishing.service.gov.uk/media/57a08d42ed915d3cfd001906/R7973_ANNEXC.pdf)).

**Negotiating entry.** A PL/PML holder needing to occupy Village Land must (i) advise the landowner, (ii) consult the Village Council and the District authority, (iii) submit a compensation/relocation plan under s.97 of the Mining Act ([Lexology](https://www.lexology.com/library/detail.aspx?g=b14bbf0b-16e4-491d-9172-0ed5750a343f)). For acquisitions above 250 ha, the Village Assembly's role becomes advisory rather than determinative ([Land Portal Tanzania](https://landportal.org/book/narratives/2022/tanzania)). The Commissioner for Lands must approve type, amount, method and timing of compensation.

**Compensation components** are defined by the **Land (Assessment of the Value of Land for Compensation) Regulations, L.N. No. 78 of 2001** ([ECOLEX](https://www.ecolex.org/details/legislation/land-assessment-of-the-value-of-land-for-compensation-regulations-ln-no-78-of-2001-lex-faoc028341/)), as amended through 2017 land-law amendments:

- **Market value of the land** (set by a Registered Valuer, ratified by the Chief Valuer);
- **Value of unexhausted improvements** (crops, trees, structures — schedule rates per crop and tree maturity);
- **Disturbance allowance** — calculated as the prevailing commercial-bank fixed-deposit interest rate applied to the land value for 12 months ([DanVast](https://danvastproperty.com/news/resettlement-compensation-laws-tanzania));
- **Transport allowance** — 12 tonnes × 20 km × prevailing freight rate;
- **Accommodation allowance** — market rent for the relocation property for 36 months;
- **Loss-of-profit allowance** — net monthly profit × 36 (where the land is producing income).

**Village government meeting protocol.** Quorum is set in the village constitution but commonly half plus one of registered adults. Minutes must record the agenda, attendance roll, motions, voting, and dissent. Authorisation by illiterate signatories is by **thumb-print witnessed by the Village Executive Officer (VEO)**. Common conflict drivers documented in IPIS/IIED fieldwork ([IPIS NW Tanzania](https://ipisresearch.be/wp-content/uploads/2019/01/1901-ASM-Tanzania_web%C2%AE.pdf), [IIED Tanzania ASM](https://www.iied.org/sites/default/files/pdfs/migrate/16641IIED.pdf)): family graves on the licence polygon, standing maize/cassava farms, water-source contamination fears, school proximity, sacred groves/shrines, footpaths and grazing routes. Best practice: walk the polygon with the Village Chairperson, VEO, Mwenyekiti wa Kitongoji and elders before any pegging.

## 4. CSR Plans under Mining Act s.105

**Section 105 of the Mining Act Cap.123** obliges every mineral right holder to prepare a **credible annual CSR Plan** jointly agreed with the relevant Local Government Authority (LGA), with concurrence from the Minister responsible for LGAs and the Minister responsible for Finance ([Bowmans CSR 2023](https://bowmanslaw.com/insights/tanzania-the-mining-corporate-social-responsibility-regulations-2023/), [FB Attorneys](https://fbattorneys.co.tz/compulsory-community-social-responsibility/)). The **Mining (Corporate Social Responsibility) Regulations 2023** (GN 409/2023) operationalised this, with 2026 amendments issued by Government Notice ([Clyde & Co Jan 2026](https://www.clydeco.com/en/insights/2026/01/legal-update-tanzania-amendments-to-the-mining-cor)). Note that Tanzania's High Court invalidated the original allocation framework — practitioners should track the latest GN ([African Mining Market](https://africanminingmarket.com/tanzania-high-court-invalidates-corporate-social-responsibility-allocation-framework-under-mining-regulations/25116/)).

**Process.** The Plan is first submitted to a CSR Committee that reviews within **14 days** and forwards to the District/Town/Municipal/City Council, which must approve or reject with reasons within **7 days**; once approved, it is forwarded to the two responsible Ministers within **30 days** ([Clyde & Co CSR 2023](https://www.clydeco.com/en/insights/2023/08/the-mining-corporate-social-responsibility-regulat)). Examples of accepted projects ([Tume ya Madini CSR portal](https://www.tumemadini.go.tz/pages/local-content-csr-monitoring/)): water-supply boreholes and reservoirs, school classrooms and dormitories, road grading, rural dispensaries and maternity wards, electrification, agricultural inputs.

**International alignment.** The plan template should reflect **ICMM Performance Expectation 9 (Social Performance)** and **PE 10 (Stakeholder Engagement)** ([ICMM Principle 9](https://www.icmm.com/en-gb/our-principles/mining-principles/principle-9), [ICMM Principle 10](https://www.icmm.com/en-gb/our-principles/mining-principles/principle-10)), and the **OECD Guidelines for MNEs on Responsible Business Conduct** ([OECD MNE](https://www.businessatoecd.org/hubfs/FIN-2024-11%20The%20OECD%20Guidelines%20for%20MNEs%20on%20RBC%20-%20What%20Business%20Needs%20To%20Know.pdf?hsLang=en)). Effective grievance mechanisms must be **legitimate, accessible, predictable, equitable, transparent, rights-compatible and engagement-based** ([ICMM grievance guidance](https://www.icmm.com/en-gb/guidance/social-performance/2019/grievance-mechanism)).

## 5. Road negotiations

When a PL/PML holder must clear or upgrade a road across smallholder fields:

- Engage the **Village Chairperson and VEO** first to confirm right-of-way classification (village road, district road, or TARURA road).
- Walk the alignment with affected farmers; tag every tree to be felled and crop area to be lost; obtain a Registered Valuer's per-tree and per-acre estimate against the 2001 Compensation Regulations schedule.
- **Per-acre / per-tree compensation** is set by the District Valuer using the standing schedule of crop rates (revised periodically by the Chief Government Valuer). Mango, jackfruit, coconut and mature cashew carry highest per-tree values; maize, cassava and beans are valued per acre on expected yield.
- The **village leadership** chairs a public reading of the compensation roll, witnessed signatures/thumb-prints are collected, and the **payment schedule** (often two tranches) is recorded in the village minutes.
- For roads that cross or connect to a **district/regional road**, formal coordination with **TARURA** (rural) or **TANROADS** (trunk and regional) is required — work permits, traffic management plans, drainage approvals.
- Multi-day excavator clearings: notify the Village Council a minimum of 7 days in advance, agree working hours, post warning signs, schedule the operator's competent person on site, and log daily progress in the village book.

Letter templates Boji AI should ship: (i) Notice of Intended Entry to Village Council; (ii) Compensation Schedule Acknowledgement; (iii) Tree-and-Crop Inventory with photos; (iv) TARURA/TANROADS Work Permit Application; (v) Daily Site Notice.

## 6. Determination / research methods — from artisanal to JORC-grade

**Artisanal Tanzanian methods.** Local prospecting vocabulary documented by IPIS and IIED fieldwork in Geita, Mara, Shinyanga and Tunduru ([IPIS](https://ipisresearch.be/wp-content/uploads/2019/01/1901-ASM-Tanzania_web%C2%AE.pdf), [IIED](https://www.iied.org/sites/default/files/pdfs/migrate/16641IIED.pdf)):
- **Kuona mishipa** — direct visual observation of vein outcrops, gossan, quartz blow-outs, ironstone caps;
- **Kuchimba mtaro / hand-trenching** — narrow trenches across suspected strike to expose vein continuity;
- **Kuchimba shimo / hand-shaft** — vertical pit to follow vein at depth, typically 3–30 m with bucket-and-rope hoisting;
- **Kuchenjua / panning** for gold tails, **kuchekecha / sluicing** for gemstone gravels;
- **Kupiga ramli** — informal oral intelligence from veteran miners about old workings.

Toolkit: pick, shovel, jembe, sledge, mallet, chisels, hand-pan, basin, simple sluice box, headlamp, rope. Cost: TZS 200–800k for a starter kit.

**Semi-professional methods.** Hand-augur and hand-bore (for alluvial sands and laterite), dolly mill (small jaw + ball mill for liberation), simple gravity tables (knelson-style centrifuge at low end), shaker tables, magnetic separators. Useful at the small-PML scale before committing drill spend.

**Professional methods** (when targeting bankable resource):
- **Trenching grid** — systematic trenches across mineralised strike, channel-sampled.
- **Channel sampling** — continuous chip samples across vein width at fixed intervals; the dominant industry technique for surface vein evaluation ([RockMass core logging guide](https://rockmass.net/files/core_logging_guide.pdf)).
- **RC (Reverse Circulation) drilling** — fast, dry chips, good for >80% of greenfield programmes; in Tanzania, day-rates run roughly USD 60–110 per metre depending on contractor and depth ([Sinodrills](https://www.sinodrills.com/mineral-exploration-drilling/)).
- **Diamond core (HQ/NQ)** — solid rock core, essential for structural logging, dip/dip-direction, geotech, and high-confidence resource categories; day-rates USD 130–220/m in TZ.
- **Geophysics** — IP (Induced Polarisation) for sulphides, ground magnetics for BIF and ultramafics, GPR for shallow gemstone gravels, EM for conductors.
- **Geochemistry** — soil sampling on a grid, stream sediment for catchment-scale, rock chip for target validation.
- **Logging & sectioning** — drill core is logged for lithology, alteration, structure, vein width and dip relative to core axis; the **true thickness** is derived from drill-hole inclination and structure dip via trigonometry ([RockMass guide](https://rockmass.net/files/core_logging_guide.pdf)).
- **Bankable resource estimate** — must be reported under one of the CRIRSCO-family codes: **JORC 2012** (Australia), **NI 43-101** (Canada), or **SAMREC** (South Africa) ([JORC 2012](https://www.jorc.org/docs/JORC_code_2012.pdf), [NI 43-101 wiki](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Instrument_43-101), [IMC](https://imctucson.com/mineral-resource-and-reserve-reporting-codes/), [Mining.com](https://www.mining.com/understanding-the-major-reporting-codes-for-mineral-resources/)). All three require a **Competent Person / Qualified Person** with ≥5 years' relevant experience in the deposit style ([AusIMM CP review](https://www.ausimm.com/globalassets/downloads/jorc-competent-person---a-baseline-review-in-a-global-context-june-2022-final.pdf)).

**Decision logic** (Boji AI wizard): outcrop visible & small budget → trench + channel sample → if grade ≥ cut-off, hand-shaft + sluice OR commission RC programme; if blind target → geophysics + geochem → RC → core infill; if target is bankable → JORC/43-101-grade core programme with CP oversight.

## 7. Bore-hole / pit observation logic — small-miner edition

Every shaft or pit should generate a **bore-hole log**: depth (m), layer description (topsoil → laterite → saprolite/weathered → fresh rock → vein zone), layer colour and grain size, vein width and dip, host rock, mineralisation indicators (visible gold, sulphide specks, garnet, quartz, mica, oxide staining), sample number, sample mass, photo reference, GPS coordinate of collar, azimuth and dip of the pit/shaft, water inflow notes ([Geology Hub lithologging](https://geologyhub.com/lithologging-borehole-cores-sampling-techniques/), [Marjoribanks core logging](http://rogermarjoribanks.info/diamond-drill-core-log-sheets/)).

**Multi-shaft triangulation.** With three or more vein intersections from separate shafts, the miner can estimate **strike, dip, plunge, length, width, depth and volume** by plotting collars and intersection points on a long-section, then fitting a planar vein. Simple volumetric formula: **V (m³) = L × W × T_true**, where T_true = T_apparent × sin(angle between hole and vein plane). Tonnes = V × specific gravity (e.g. 2.7 t/m³ for typical quartz-host gold reef); contained metal = tonnes × average grade. References for best practice in volumetric and resource estimation: **CSA Global, SRK Consulting, RPM Global**, plus core-logging software like **GeologiX** and **Acquire** for digital capture ([CIM exploration best-practice](https://mrmr.cim.org/media/1080/cim-mineral-exploration-best-practice-guidelines-november-23-2018.pdf), [Lyell Collection QA/QC review](https://www.lyellcollection.org/doi/full/10.1144/geochem2023-046)).

This logic — three shafts → triangulated vein plane → tonnes-and-grade box — is exactly the cognitive step that separates "hopeful artisanal" from "investable artisanal", and is the highest-leverage feature in Boji AI's bore-hole logger.

## 8. Lab / assay supply chain — Tanzania & East Africa

**Commercial labs operating in or serving Tanzania:**
- **SGS** — Mwanza laboratory now offering fire assay (15/30/50 g lead-collection) and multi-element analysis; Mobile Sample Preparation Units deployed to cut transport delays ([SGS Tanzania](https://www.sgs.com/en-tz/services/fire-assay-analysis), [SGS news 2025](https://www.sgs.com/en-tz/news/2025/08/advancing-tanzania-mining-sector-sgs-enhances-testing-capabilities)).
- **Bureau Veritas** — preparation hubs in Mwanza and Geita; final analysis often routed to Perth or Johannesburg.
- **ALS** — sample-prep in Mwanza; chemistry in Johannesburg or Perth (Au-AA, Au-GRA, ME-ICP41/61, ICP-MS multi-element).
- **Intertek** — gold assay (fire + AAS / gravimetric finish) and multi-element ICP via Geita/Mwanza prep, Perth analytical hub ([Intertek precious metals](https://www.intertek.com/minerals/precious-metals-analysis/)).

**Public-sector and regional:**
- **Geological Survey of Tanzania (GST)** — Dodoma, **SADCAS-accredited to ISO/IEC 17025:2017** for chemical analysis of minerals; runs sample prep, mineral processing, mineralogy/petrology, petrophysics ([GST Resources](https://www.gst.go.tz/resources/), [GST charges](https://www.gst.go.tz/charges-for-labolatory-services/)). Tanzania broke ground in 2025 on a TZS 14.3 billion mineral testing laboratory in Kizota, Dodoma — to be the largest in East and Central Africa ([TanzaniaInvest](https://www.tanzaniainvest.com/mining/construction-mineral-testing-laboratory-kizota-dodoma)).
- **AMGC (African Minerals and Geosciences Centre, formerly SEAMIC)** — Dar es Salaam, regional sister lab to GST for analytical work and capacity building ([SEAMIC/AMGC](https://www.seamic.org/index.php/research-projects/35-geosciences-updates/234-gst)).

**Techniques and when to use them:**
- **Fire assay (FA-AAS, FA-GRA)** — gold and PGE; gold standard for Au; 15–50 g charge; turnaround 5–14 days depending on lab and queue.
- **AAS (Atomic Absorption)** — single-element finish for Au, Cu, Pb, Zn.
- **ICP-OES / ICP-MS** — multi-element packages (typically 33–61 elements); ICP-MS detection limits go to ppb.
- **LA-ICP-MS** — in-situ trace analysis on polished sections for gemstone provenance and zoning.
- **Gravimetric finish** — for high-grade ore (>10 g/t Au) where AAS saturates.
- **Screen Fire Assay (SFA)** — for coarse-gold "nuggety" deposits where standard FA mis-represents grade.
- **LECO** — for total carbon/sulphur in coal and refractory ores.

**Costs** (indicative 2025 commercial rates): Au-FA-AAS USD 12–18/sample; multi-element ICP USD 20–35/sample; ICP-MS USD 35–60; screen-fire USD 70–120. GST published rates run roughly 30–50% below commercial benchmarks ([GST charges](https://www.gst.go.tz/charges-for-labolatory-services/)).

**Sample-bag and chain-of-custody standard.** Calico bag inside heavy poly bag; **triplicate waterproof tag** — one inside the bag, one on the bag, one in the field book; bag number = hole-ID + from-depth + to-depth; sealed with crimp ties; manifest signed at handover at the lab gate ([CIM QA/QC](https://mrmr.cim.org/media/1080/cim-mineral-exploration-best-practice-guidelines-november-23-2018.pdf), [Mining Doc QA/QC](https://www.miningdoc.tech/question/what-best-practices-should-geologists-follow-to-ensure-qa-qc-in-sampling/)).

**QA/QC inserts.** Industry best practice: **5–10% of samples must be QC** — typically 1 in 20 a Certified Reference Material (matrix-matched, low/medium/high grade), 1 in 20 a coarse blank (barren quartz or marble), 1 in 20 a field duplicate (split or twin-pit). Charts kept in real time: standards within ±2 SD of certified value, blanks below 5× detection limit, duplicate scatter on log-log plot with ±10% bounds ([Lyell QA/QC review](https://www.lyellcollection.org/doi/full/10.1144/geochem2023-046), [Mineral Mountain QA/QC](https://mineralmountainresources.com/corporate-qaqc/)). This is the QA/QC pack a JORC/43-101 Competent Person will demand before signing a Mineral Resource Estimate.

## 10. Mineral-specific determination notes

- **Gold.** Reef/quartz-vein style (e.g. Lake Victoria Goldfield, Geita, Bulyanhulu) — focus on vein outcrops, shears, BIF contacts; channel samples, RC then core. Alluvial (Lupa, Mara) — pan, sluice, hand-augur, simple gravity. Eluvial — soil grids and shallow pits where the reef has weathered in situ.
- **Tanzanite.** **Mererani only** — graphitic gneiss host in Block A/B/C/D ([USGS 2017–18](https://pubs.usgs.gov/myb/vol3/2017-18/myb3-2017-18-tanzania.pdf), [Tanzanite Experience](https://www.tanzaniteexperience.com/what-gemstones-are-found-in-tanzania/)). Vertical reefs, deep shafts, tightly regulated by the Mererani wall and centralised trading hub.
- **Ruby & sapphire.** Winza (ruby, amphibolite-hosted, 2008 discovery), Songea (ruby), Tunduru (sapphire, alluvial), Umba (sapphire, garnet), Mahenge (spinel, ruby) ([GIA Fall 2016](https://www.gia.edu/gems-gemology/fall-2016-gemnews-update-colored-gemstone-mining-tanzania), [Ruby-Sapphire.com](https://www.ruby-sapphire.com/index.php/component/content/article/10-articles/798-tanzania-ruby-sapphire-spinel)). Alluvial gravels — washing and screening dominate.
- **Garnet (tsavorite, rhodolite).** Lindi (Lutela), Umba, Komolo — alluvial and primary skarn/gneiss hosts ([Gemstone Detective Umba](https://www.gemstonedetective.com/post/unearthing-tanzania-s-rare-gemstones-a-mining-adventure-to-explore-umba-sapphires-and-garnets)).
- **Emerald & alexandrite.** Manyara/Lake Manyara emerald, chrysoberyl alexandrite in pegmatite/schist hosts ([Endorphin Expeditions](https://www.endorphinexpeditions.co.za/gemstones-of-tanzania/)).
- **Diamond.** **Mwadui kimberlite** (Williamson Mine, world's largest economic kimberlite at 146 ha) plus alluvial trains downstream ([Mindat Mwadui](https://www.mindat.org/loc-16730.html)).
- **Industrial minerals.** Gypsum (Kilwa, Itigi), limestone (Tanga, Mbeya, Wazo Hill), salt (Bagamoyo solar evaporation; Uvinza rock salt), pozzolana (volcanic Kilimanjaro/Mbeya), kaolin (Pugu Hills), **graphite (Lindi — Lindi Jumbo, Walkabout Resources)** ([Energy Capital Power](https://energycapitalpower.com/tanzanias-chamber-of-mines-to-highlight-graphite-rare-earth-prospects-at-cma-2024/)), **rare earths (Ngualla — Peak Resources NdPr deposit)** ([Mining Technology](https://www.mining-technology.com/features/could-rare-earths-peak-in-tanzania/), [US trade.gov](https://www.trade.gov/market-intelligence/tanzania-rare-earth-and-critical-minerals)).
- **Coal.** **Songwe-Kiwira** (Mbeya, high-volatile bituminous, Ivogo 35.5 Mt, Kabulo 45.5 Mt, Ilima 20.5 Mt) and **Mchuchuma** (Ruvuma, 428 Mt) ([ScienceDirect Songwe-Kiwira](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0166516203001083)).
- **Heavy minerals.** Coastal placers in Pwani Region (ilmenite, rutile, zircon, monazite).

Each mineral type implies a different determination wizard in Boji AI: tanzanite is steep-reef-and-shaft logic; alluvial gems are pit-and-screen logic; gold is reef-vs-alluvial-vs-eluvial branching; graphite/REE/coal demand drill-and-assay programmes.

## 11. Safety & explosives — lawful compliance tracking only

Tanzania's **Explosives Act (Cap.45)** and the **Mining (Safe Working and Occupational Health) Regulations** establish the framework ([Tanzania Laws Explosives Act](https://tanzanialaws.com/sub-e/490-explosives-act), [Tanzlii Safe Working](https://media.tanzlii.org/media/legislation/317472/source_file/764d8b6dd9705b92/1999-219.pdf)).

Required records Boji AI should track at compliance level only:
- **Blasting Certificate** — 5-year validity, renewable, issued only after personal examination by an Inspector confirms competence; categorised by surface/underground and fuse type ([Tanzania Laws](https://www.tanzanialaws.com/e/113-explosives-act)).
- **Magazine Licence** — application requires a detailed 1:1,000 scale map of a 1-mile radius; storage segregated by class of explosive; daily issue and return reconciled.
- **Competent Person** — named for each shift; controls issue, transport, charging, firing.
- **Blast Design Documentation** — pattern, hole depth, charge weight, stemming, delay sequence (filed; *not* generated by Boji AI).
- **Exclusion Zone** — radius based on blast design and ground conditions; pre-blast siren, post-blast all-clear, misfire procedure.
- **OSHA-equivalent records** (Tanzania OSHA): risk assessment register, near-miss log, PPE issue log, medical surveillance, incident reports.

Boji AI's role is strictly to host certificates, expiry alerts, magazine inventory logs, exclusion-zone polygons and incident records — **never** to generate operational blast instructions.

| Lab / assay (§8) | **Lab Order Workflow**: bag-and-tag generator, manifest, courier dispatch, lab-cost compare (SGS/BV/ALS/Intertek/GST/AMGC), real-time QA/QC charts (standards, blanks, duplicates), turnaround SLA tracker. |

| Mineral-specific (§10) | Per-mineral wizards (Tanzanite-Mererani, Gold-reef-vs-alluvial, Tunduru-sapphire, Mwadui-diamond, Ngualla-REE, Lindi-graphite, Songwe-coal). |
