**Principal ore minerals.** Native gold (Au), electrum ((Au,Ag) — typically 20–80% Ag), calaverite (AuTe₂), sylvanite ((Au,Ag)Te₂), krennerite ((Au,Ag)Te₂), petzite (Ag₃AuTe₂), nagyagite (Pb₅Au(Te,Sb)₄S₅–₈), aurostibite (AuSb₂), maldonite (Au₂Bi). ### B. Geology of formation & deposit types

The six most productive families are: **orogenic** (mesothermal greenstone-belt quartz-carbonate veins), **Carlin-type** (sediment-hosted, disseminated, sub-µm gold in arsenian pyrite), **low-sulfidation epithermal** (volcanic-arc, bonanza vein), **high-sulfidation epithermal** (acid-leached caps), **porphyry Au–Cu** (large tonnage, low grade), **Au-rich VMS**, **intrusion-related (RIR)**, and **paleoplacer (Witwatersrand-type)**. ([USGS / 911 Metallurgist deposit models](https://911metallurgist.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/10/Models-and-Exploration-Methods-for-Major-Gold-Deposit-Types.pdf))

| Type | Typical grade | Typical tonnage | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Orogenic | 3–15 g/t Au | 1–50 Mt (per lode) | Bulyanhulu (TZ), Kalgoorlie (AU) |
| Carlin-type | 0.7–10 g/t Au | 30–500 Mt | Goldstrike (NV) — produces despite grades as low as 0.7 g/t [SEG / GSW](https://pubs.geoscienceworld.org/segweb/books/book/1998/chapter/16274135/Critical-Elements-in-Carlin-Epithermal-and) |
| LS epithermal | 5–30 g/t Au (bonanza shoots can be >100 g/t) | 1–30 Mt | Hishikari (JP) |
| Porphyry Au–Cu | 0.2–1.5 g/t Au + 0.3–1.0% Cu | 200 Mt – >2 Bt | Grasberg (ID) |
| Witwatersrand paleoplacer | 4–10 g/t Au | >10 Bt basin-wide | Witwatersrand has produced ~40% of all gold ever mined [OzGeology](https://ozgeology.com/en-us/blogs/news/exploring-the-major-gold-deposit-types) |
| Alluvial placer | 0.1–5 g/m³ | Highly variable | Lupa goldfield (TZ) |

### C. Tanzania / East Africa relevance

Tanzania is the **fourth-largest gold producer in Africa**. Production is overwhelmingly hosted in two metallogenic provinces: the **Lake Victoria Goldfield (LVGF)** in the north (Archaean Sukumaland and Nzega greenstone belts) and the **Lupa Goldfield** in the southwest (Mbeya / Chunya).

**Major industrial mines** (Lake Victoria Goldfield):
- **Geita Gold Mine** (AngloGold Ashanti) — Tanzania's largest, open-pit + underground, BIF-hosted; produced 486,000 oz in 2021 ([AngloGold Ashanti — Geita SAMREC 2022](https://reports.anglogoldashanti.com/22/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/AGA-GeitaSAMREC22.pdf))
- **Bulyanhulu** (Barrick Gold), Shinyanga — narrow-vein, underground; ~180,000 oz in 2023 ([Mining Technology](https://www.mining-technology.com/projects/bulyanhulu-gold-mine-shinyanga/))
- **North Mara** (Barrick), Tarime — open-pit + underground
- **Buzwagi** (Barrick) — closed 2021, transition to skills hub
- **Nyamongo / Nyanzaga** (OreCorp / Perseus), Mara
- **Tulawaka** (closed) — historic narrow-vein

**ASGM context.** Tanzania first **legalised ASGM in 1995** and the sector is the country's largest single source of gold by headcount: **approximately 1.5 million people directly involved** in ASGM, with ~7.2 million Tanzanians (≈13% of national population) indirectly dependent on it ([Mwamba Mining](https://mwambamining.com/artisanal-and-small-scale-gold-mining-asgm-in-tanzania/); [TZ NAP Minamata 2020–2025](https://minamataconvention.org/sites/default/files/documents/national_action_plan/TANZANIA-NAP-EN-2020.pdf)). National mercury consumption by ASGM is **13.2–24.4 tonnes/year** ([TZ Minamata NAP](https://minamataconvention.org/sites/default/files/documents/national_action_plan/TANZANIA-NAP-EN-2020.pdf)).

**Districts Boji must recognise:** Geita, Kahama, Shinyanga, Tarime, Mara, Singida (Sekenke), Chunya (Mbeya), Songwe, Tabora, Kigoma. Tanga area has minor occurrences.

### D. Exploration & determination

- **Geophysics.** IP/resistivity for disseminated sulphides; ground magnetics for BIF-hosted Au (Geita-type) and for mapping mafic dyke conduits; gravity for kimberlite + heavy-mineral-rich placers; VLF-EM for shallow shear zones.
- **Geochemistry.** Soil grids (typically 100 m × 25 m on first pass, infill 50 × 25 m). **Pathfinder elements for Au: As, Sb, Hg, Bi, Te, W, Ag** — order matters; As anomalies extend the largest haloes in orogenic systems ([USGS gold models](https://www.usgs.gov/centers/gggsc/science/new-mineral-deposit-models-gold-phosphate-rare-earth-elements-and-placer-rare)). Stream-sediment BLEG (Bulk Leach Extractable Gold) and panning concentrate for follow-up.
- **Drilling.** RC (reverse circulation) at 1 m sampling for early-stage and resource-grade work; **diamond core (HQ / NQ)** for structural / mineralogical confirmation, narrow-vein and underground definition. Depth: 80–300 m typical for RC; >500 m for orogenic depth tests. Spacing: 100 × 100 m for inferred → 25 × 25 m for measured.
- **Assay protocol.** Standard 30 g/50 g **lead-collection fire assay** with AAS or gravimetric finish — the global gold industry's reference method. Sample is fluxed (borax / soda ash / silica / litharge), fired at 850 → 950 → 1060 °C, slag poured, lead button cupelled at 950 °C to yield a doré bead, then digested in HNO₃ + HCl ([Actlabs](https://actlabs.com/geochemistry-analysis/precious-metal-analysis/); [ALS Global](https://www.alsglobal.com/en/geochemistry/precious-metals-analysis/platinum-group-elements)).
- **Coarse-gold ores** (nugget effect) require **screen-fire assay** (SFA): screen at ~106 µm, assay the +106 µm fraction in full and the −106 µm fraction by sub-sample. Failure to do this is the #1 cause of grade reconciliation disputes in ASM. ([SGS](https://www.sgs.com/en-us/services/fire-assay-analysis))

### E. Mining methods

| Setting | Method | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Large oxide cap | Open-pit + heap leach (or CIL) | Strip ratio 1:1 to 6:1 |
| Large sulphide / refractory | Open-pit or underground + CIL ± POX / BIOX | Higher capex; needs pre-treatment |
| Narrow-vein, deep, high-grade | Underground long-hole stoping, shrinkage, cut-and-fill | Bulyanhulu archetype |
| Alluvial / eluvial | Sluice, jig, dredge | Lupa goldfield archetype |
| ASGM hard-rock | Hand-shaft + bucket hoist, manual hammer + chisel, then *kuchenjua* / *kuchekecha* (panning + sluicing) | Depth typically <60 m; ventilation, dewatering and silicosis are headline risks |

### F. Comminution & beneficiation

- **Grind targets.** For free-milling Au: gravity circuit feed at **P80 75–150 µm**; for cyanidation feed P80 **75–106 µm**. Refractory ores require **ultra-fine grind to P80 10–20 µm** before POX/BIOX.
- **Gravity concentration.** Knelson / Falcon centrifugal concentrators operate at feed densities up to 75% solids and recover free-milling micron-sized gold ([911 Metallurgist — Knelson](https://www.911metallurgist.com/blog/knelson-concentrator/)). Industry-standard GRG test (LaPlante 2000) runs the sample at P80 of **850 µm, 300 µm, 75 µm** in three passes ([ALS — Gravity Gold](https://www.alsglobal.com/en/metallurgy-and-mineral-processing/gold-extraction/gravity-gold-recovery)). Jigs and shaking tables are mainstays for coarse alluvial.
- **Flotation.** For sulphide-hosted Au and PGMs, the workhorse collectors are **xanthates — SIPX (sodium isopropyl xanthate), PAX (potassium amyl xanthate)** — and dithiophosphates / dithiocarbamates as secondaries; frother is **MIBC** (sometimes blended with polyglycol frothers like Dowfroth 200/400). Typical PAX dose 30–150 g/t, MIBC 20–80 g/t at conditioning pH 9–11 ([Orica Flotation Guidebook](https://www.ausimm.com/globalassets/insights-and-resources/minerals-processing-toolbox/omc_flotation_guidebook.pdf)).
- **Cyanidation (CIL / CIP / heap leach).** Oxide ores leach in **4–24 h** with **0.05% NaCN** at P80 ~210 µm (65 mesh); sulphide ores need **10–72 h** with **0.1% NaCN** at P80 ~45 µm (325 mesh) ([EPA Technical Report](https://archive.epa.gov/epawaste/nonhaz/industrial/special/web/pdf/cyanide.pdf)). Cyanide consumption is typically **0.2–0.5 kg/t** in CIP and **10–30% lower** in CIL ([SBM China](https://www.sbmchina.com/media/articals/what-are-the-differences-between-cip-and-cil-processes.html)). Tank-leach recoveries: **85–98%** ([EPA](https://archive.epa.gov/epawaste/nonhaz/industrial/special/web/pdf/cyanide.pdf)). Dissolved oxygen must be maintained >6 mg/L by air sparging or oxygen injection.
- **Mercury amalgamation (ASGM only).** Hg–Au amalgam forms at roughly 50:50 mass ratio; with a properly run **retort**, ~95% of mercury can be condensed and recycled ([Zero Mercury](https://www.zeromercury.org/about-mercury/mercury-in-processes/artisanal-and-small-scale-gold-mining-asgm/)). Whole-ore amalgamation (mercury added to the ball mill) is the worst-practice variant the Minamata Convention seeks to eliminate.

### G. Hydrometallurgy / pyrometallurgy / refining

- **Loaded carbon → elution.** Acid wash (HCl) → Zadra or AARL elution → carbon regeneration kiln (~700 °C).
- **Electrowinning** of the eluate produces a sludge → smelted with borax/silica/soda flux into a **doré bar** (typically 70–90% Au + 5–25% Ag + Cu/Pb/Fe).
- **Merrill–Crowe** (zinc-dust precipitation) is the alternative to carbon when ore is high-Ag (Ag:Au > 5:1).
- **External refining.** Doré is sent to a Good-Delivery refinery to reach **LBMA 995.0 fineness** (minimum) — typically Miller chlorination (995.0–996.0) followed by Wohlwill electrolysis (999.9). Reference Good-Delivery refiners: **Rand Refinery, Metalor, Argor-Heraeus, Valcambi, PAMP, MKS, Heraeus** ([LBMA — Good Delivery](https://www.lbma.org.uk/good-delivery/about-good-delivery)). As of December 2024, the LBMA list has **65 gold refiners and 81 silver refiners** ([Wikipedia — Good Delivery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Good_Delivery)).
- **Good-Delivery bar spec (gold):** minimum fineness **995.0**, weight 350–430 fine troy oz (~10.9–13.4 kg), bar typically ~400 fine troy oz / 12.5 kg ([LBMA Technical Specs](https://www.lbma.org.uk/publications/good-delivery-rules/technical-specifications)).
- **Tanzania refining capacity:** **Mwanza Precious Metals Refinery (MPMR)** — 480 kg/day Au at 999.9 + 60 kg/day Ag ([MPMR brochure](https://mpmrcl.com/images/MPMR_Brochure.pdf)); **Geita Gold Refinery** — 450 kg/day ([TanzaniaInvest](https://www.tanzaniainvest.com/mining/mwanza-gold-refinery-inauguration)); **Eyes of Africa Ltd** (third BoT-designated refiner).

### H. Movement, packaging, security

- **From mine site to refinery.** TZ law requires routing through a **Mineral Trading Centre (MTC)** or buying station. There are **28 operational trading hubs** as of recent count, with major hubs in Geita (5 buying centres at Katoro, Nyarugusu, Nyakwage, Mbogwe, Bukombe), Mwanza, Chunya, Arusha, Kahama, Shinyanga and others ([Mining Technology — TZ trading hubs](https://www.mining-technology.com/features/inside-tanzanias-mineral-trading-hubs/); [TUME YA MADINI](https://www.tumemadini.go.tz/)).
- **Assay & valuation.** Government Mineral Officer (GMO) at the MTC weighs, assays, sets royalty + inspection fee, and issues clearance.
- **Dangerous-goods classification.** Doré is **not** a DG class itself, but mercury contamination and high-purity-acid retorts on-site trigger UN 2809 (Mercury) and Class 8 corrosives.
- **High-value secure logistics.** International chain-of-custody is dominated by **Brink's, Loomis International, Malca-Amit, Ferrari Group** — all LBMA-recognised secure-transport providers ([Brink's Precious Metals](https://us.brinks.com/precious-metals); [Malca-Amit](https://www.malca-amit.com/)).
- **Incoterms** for refined / doré export: CIP and DAP are typical for armoured-air movement to refinery; FOB for ocean (rare for high-value precious). Insurance is loco-to-loco at full replacement value.

### I. Pricing & markets

- **Benchmark.** LBMA Gold Price (twice daily, 10:30 and 15:00 London time) — auction with up to 16 participants ([LBMA](https://www.lbma.org.uk/publications/the-otc-guide/london-good-delivery-gold-and-silver)). All gold contracts globally reference this price ± purity / lot / location premium.
- **Demand mix (2024 full year, 4,974 t total — record):** Jewellery 1,877 t (−11%); investment 1,180 t (+25%, four-year high); central banks >1,000 t (third consecutive year); technology / electronics ~300 t. Annual market value reached US$382 billion ([World Gold Council — Gold Demand Trends 2024](https://www.gold.org/goldhub/research/gold-demand-trends/gold-demand-trends-full-year-2024); [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/02/05/worlds-demand-for-gold-hit-another-record-high-in-2024.html)).
- **Premium / discount drivers.** Origin (LBMA refiner > non-LBMA), purity (999.9 > 995.0), lot size (Good-Delivery bar > kilobar > granules), AML provenance (clear chain-of-custody > unknown).
- **Tanzania pricing route.** Two paths:
  1. **Conventional export (broker / private buyer):** Royalty **6%** + inspection fee **1%** + clearance, paid in foreign currency by the buyer. Total state-take ~7%. ([Mining Commission — Royalty & Inspection rates](https://www.tumemadini.go.tz/mineral-trade/mineral-royalties-and-inspection-fees-rates/); [VELMA Law](https://velmalaw.co.tz/news/bot-domestic-gold-purchase-program/))
  2. **BoT Domestic Gold Purchase Programme (DGPP):** Royalty **4%**, inspection fee **0%**, VAT zero-rated (input claimable), **100% payment within 24 h in TZS** at the BoT-published daily TZS/g price referenced to LBMA. Refining costs covered by BoT. Designated refineries: Geita Gold Refinery, Mwanza Precious Metals Refinery, Eyes of Africa ([BoT Public Notice 1 Oct 2024](https://www.bot.go.tz/Adverts/PressRelease/en/2024100206312713.pdf); [BoT Public Notice 8 Oct 2024](https://www.bot.go.tz/Adverts/PressRelease/en/2024100812030421.pdf)). As of 13 June 2025, BoT had purchased **5,022.85 kg refined gold (~US$554 million)**, exceeding its US$350 m FY2024/25 reserve-build target ([The Chanzo](https://thechanzo.com/2025/06/17/bank-of-tanzania-boosts-gold-reserves-with-new-agreements-to-strengthen-gold-purchase-programme/)).

### J. Environmental, health & safety

- **Mercury.** Tanzania is a Party to the Minamata Convention; ASGM operators in priority districts must comply with the National Action Plan ([TZ NAP 2020–2025](https://minamataconvention.org/sites/default/files/documents/national_action_plan/TANZANIA-NAP-EN-2020.pdf)).
- **Cyanide.** International Cyanide Management Code (ICMC) is voluntary but de-facto mandatory for any operation seeking project finance from IFC / OECD-aligned lenders. As of Jan 2024, **281 of 358 enrolled operations were certified**, including 100 gold mines, 40 cyanide producers, 141 transporters ([Cyanide Code](https://cyanidecode.org/)). Nine principles cover production, transport, handling, operations, decommissioning, worker safety, emergency response, training, communications.
- **Tailings.** Apply ICMM / GISTM (Global Industry Standard on Tailings Management) — non-negotiable for any TSF >Category 2.
- **Silicosis.** Quartz-vein dust at >0.1 mg/m³ respirable silica triggers progressive massive fibrosis after 5–20 years; mandatory wet-drilling, ventilation and PPE.
- **Acid mine drainage** where ore is sulphide-rich (pyrite > 3% in waste); requires PAG/NAG geochemical characterisation pre-mining.

### A. Identity

| Attribute | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Symbol | Ag (*argentum*) | [USGS MCS 2025 — Silver](https://pubs.usgs.gov/periodicals/mcs2025/mcs2025-silver.pdf) |
| Atomic number | 47 | — |
| Atomic mass | 107.8682 u | — |
| Density | 10.49 g/cm³ | — |
| Melting point | 961.78 °C | — |
| Stable isotopes | ¹⁰⁷Ag (51.84%), ¹⁰⁹Ag (48.16%) | — |
| Crustal abundance | ~0.075 ppm | [Periodictable.com](https://periodictable.com/Properties/A/CrustAbundance.an.html) |

**Principal ore minerals.** Native silver (Ag), argentite / acanthite (Ag₂S), proustite (Ag₃AsS₃), pyrargyrite (Ag₃SbS₃), stephanite (Ag₅SbS₄), polybasite ((Ag,Cu)₁₆Sb₂S₁₁), cerargyrite / chlorargyrite (AgCl), bromargyrite (AgBr). Silver also reports to galena (PbS), sphalerite (ZnS), chalcopyrite (CuFeS₂) and tetrahedrite ((Cu,Fe,Ag)₁₂Sb₄S₁₃) as a substitution / by-product.

### B. Geology of formation & deposit types

The bulk of mined silver is **by-product** from base-metal mines, not primary silver mines. Key types:
- **Low-sulfidation epithermal Ag–Au veins** (e.g., Fresnillo, Pachuca — Mexico). Bonanza grades 500–2,000 g/t Ag.
- **Polymetallic carbonate-replacement deposits (CRD) / skarns** (e.g., Cerro de Pasco — Peru): 100–400 g/t Ag with Pb–Zn.
- **VMS** (Kuroko-type): 30–200 g/t Ag with Cu–Zn–Pb.
- **Sediment-hosted Cu** (e.g., Kupferschiefer): 30–80 g/t Ag.
- **Porphyry Cu–Mo by-product:** 1–10 g/t Ag at multi-billion-tonne scale.

The world's richest silver districts sit along the **Pacific Ring of Fire** (Mexico, Peru, Bolivia, Chile), where magmatic-hydrothermal systems precipitate Ag from circulating fluids ([Bullion Trading LLC](https://bulliontradingllc.com/blog/where-does-silver-come-from/)).

### C. Tanzania / East Africa relevance

**Tanzania is not a primary silver producer.** Silver appears as a **doré-bar by-product** at every TZ gold mine — typical Ag:Au mass ratio in TZ doré is **0.2–1.5** (lower than typical epithermal doré, which can hit 5–20). Bulyanhulu's polymetallic ore body has the most appreciable Ag content. There is no dedicated silver mine in Tanzania and no ASM silver-only economy.

For Boji: **treat silver as a doré-bar credit, not a primary commodity, when modelling a TZ owner's revenue.**

### D. Exploration & determination

Same field methods as gold; silver is treated as a co-pathfinder for Au in epithermal systems (Ag/Au > 10 indicates epithermal level, Ag/Au < 1 indicates orogenic). Assay: **lead-collection fire assay with gravimetric or AAS finish for Ag**, or **aqua-regia digest + ICP-OES/MS** for low-grade base-metal ores. Detection limit by fire-assay-gravimetric: ~5 g/t; by aqua-regia ICP-MS: ~0.05 g/t.

### E. Mining methods

Identical to base-metal sulphide methods (open-pit + underground sublevel stoping). Silver-only operations are rare; almost all silver is recovered from polymetallic flowsheets.

### F. Comminution & beneficiation

- **Flotation** is the workhorse: SIPX / PAX collectors, MIBC frother, pH 9–11 with lime. Differential flotation (Pb → Cu → Zn) sequentially recovers Ag-bearing concentrates. Silver typically reports preferentially to the **Pb concentrate** (galena is the most efficient host).
- **Cyanidation** for oxide / native Ag ores (chlorargyrite caps) — NaCN dose **higher than for Au (1–4 kg/t)** because Ag–CN complex stoichiometry is 1:2 vs Au's 1:2 but kinetics slower; residence time 24–48 h.
- **Merrill–Crowe (zinc dust precipitation)** is the preferred recovery from clarified pregnant solution when Ag:Au > 5:1, because activated carbon has poor selectivity for silver-cyanide complex.

### G. Hydrometallurgy / refining

- **Parting (acid digestion).** Doré is treated with hot **65% nitric acid** — silver dissolves as AgNO₃, gold remains as insoluble sponge. NaCl precipitates AgCl, which is reduced back to Ag metal (zinc / iron cementation) ([Metalorix — silver refining](https://metalorix.com/en/learn/production-industry/silver-refining-methods-cupellation-electrolysis-and-more)).
- **Moebius electrolytic cell.** Anode: doré silver (≤20 ‰ base metals). Electrolyte: 1% HNO₃ + AgNO₃. Cathode: pure Ag. Output: **999.0–999.85 fineness Ag**, often reduced to 998.0 by adding Cu (no premium paid in English market above 998.0) ([Noble Gold — Moebius](https://noblegoldinvestments.com/learn/blog/moebius-process-silver-refining/); [911 Metallurgist](https://www.911metallurgist.com/blog/purification-gold-electrolysis/)).
- **Modern variants** (e.g., emew cell) achieve **>99.99% Ag** in single pass.

### H. Movement, packaging, security

- **LBMA Good-Delivery silver bar** — ~1,000 troy oz (~32 kg), ±10% tolerance, **minimum fineness 999.0** ([LBMA Technical Specs](https://www.lbma.org.uk/publications/good-delivery-rules/technical-specifications)).

### I. Pricing & markets

- **TZ specifics.** Royalty on silver export is **6%** (same as gold) under the 2017 amended Mining Act; no BoT DGPP equivalent exists for silver ([Mining Commission](https://www.tumemadini.go.tz/mineral-trade/mineral-royalties-and-inspection-fees-rates/); [Lexology — TZ mining amendments](https://www.lexology.com/library/detail.aspx?g=f26f7200-ee53-4271-9560-2f9b64afb8d0)).

Same cyanide and tailings considerations as gold. Nitric-acid parting in refineries generates NOx; abatement (scrubbers, condenser cascades) is mandatory. AgNO₃ solutions are corrosive (Class 8) and oxidising (Class 5).

### A. Identity

| Attribute | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Symbol | Pt | [RSC — Platinum](https://periodic-table.rsc.org/element/78/platinum) |
| Atomic number | 78 | — |
| Atomic mass | 195.084 u | [Wikipedia — Platinum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Platinum) |
| Density | 21.45 g/cm³ | — |
| Melting point | 1,768.3 °C | — |
| Stable isotopes | ¹⁹⁰Pt, ¹⁹²Pt, ¹⁹⁴Pt, ¹⁹⁵Pt, ¹⁹⁶Pt, ¹⁹⁸Pt | — |
| Crustal abundance | ~0.005 ppm | [Wikipedia — Platinum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Platinum) |

**Principal ore minerals.** Native platinum (Pt–Fe alloy "isoferroplatinum"), **sperrylite (PtAs₂)** — the single most abundant Pt mineral globally, **cooperite (PtS)**, **braggite ((Pt,Pd,Ni)S)**, moncheite (PtTe₂), Pt-bearing pentlandite ((Fe,Ni,Pt)₉S₈) and chalcopyrite ([Two Rivers Mine mineralogy](https://www.academia.edu/79889716/A_mineralogical_perspective_on_the_recovery_of_platinum_group_elements_from_Merensky_Reef_and_UG2_at_the_Two_Rivers_mine_on_the_Eastern_limb_of_the_Bushveld_Complex_in_South_Africa)).

### B. Geology of formation & deposit types

PGE deposits are dominantly **magmatic**, forming from sulphide-saturated mafic / ultramafic melts. Five families:

1. **Reef-type stratiform deposits** in layered mafic intrusions:
   - **Merensky Reef** (Bushveld) — pyroxenite-pegmatoid; Pt:Pd ≈ 2:1; total PGE+Au **3–8 g/t** over ~1 m mining cut ([Bushveld mineralogy](https://www.academia.edu/79889716/A_mineralogical_perspective_on_the_recovery_of_platinum_group_elements_from_Merensky_Reef_and_UG2_at_the_Two_Rivers_mine_on_the_Eastern_limb_of_the_Bushveld_Complex_in_South_Africa)).
   - **UG2** (Bushveld) — chromitite, 20–400 m below Merensky; **4–7 g/t PGE**; lower Pt, higher Rh and Ru.
   - **Platreef** (Bushveld northern limb) — disseminated, open-pittable, **2–5 g/t PGE+Au**; the world's largest PGE open pit.
   - **J-M Reef** (Stillwater, Montana) — narrow troctolite/pegmatoid reef; **Measured & Indicated 57.2 Mt @ 17.0 g/t Pt+Pd (31.3 Moz)** plus Inferred 92.5 Mt @ 16.6 g/t (49.4 Moz) ([Stillwater Critical Minerals](https://criticalminerals.com/projects/stillwater-west-pge-ni-cu/)).
2. **Magmatic Ni–Cu–PGE sulphide deposits** in conduits / sills: **Norilsk-Talnakh** (Russia) — world's largest Pd resource, by-product Pt.
3. **Alluvial / placer Pt** (Urals, Colombia, Choco): historically the source of Pt before Bushveld.
4. **Hydrothermal PGE** (rare, e.g., New Rambler Wyoming) — economically marginal.
5. **Black-shale-hosted PGE** (Yukon, Poland Kupferschiefer by-product).

**Bushveld Complex** hosts **>70% of world Pt reserves** and resources estimated at **>100 million kg PGE** ([USGS PGM MCS 2024](https://pubs.usgs.gov/periodicals/mcs2024/mcs2024-platinum-group.pdf)).

### C. Tanzania / East Africa relevance

**Tanzania has no commercial Pt production.** Minor PGE occurrences are noted in Proterozoic mafic-ultramafic intrusions of the Ubendian Belt (SW Tanzania) and the Kapalagulu Layered Complex (Kigoma region), but no resource has been declared at feasibility-study level. Boji should treat any TZ Pt prospect as **early-stage exploration**, not a producing asset.

In East Africa the relevant analogue is the **Ntaka Hill Ni-Cu-PGE prospect** (south Tanzania, Mtwara region) — owned previously by IMX Resources / Continental Nickel — modest Ni-Cu sulphides with PGE credits, not in production.

### D. Exploration & determination

- **Geophysics.** Ground / airborne EM and gravity for sulphide bodies in mafic hosts; magnetics to map intrusion architecture; downhole EM to vector to off-hole massive sulphide.
- **Geochemistry.** Soil grids analysed for Pt, Pd, Cu, Ni, Cr, S; pathfinder ratios **Pt/Pd, Pd/Ir, Cu/Pd** to discriminate magmatic chemistry. Ir / Os / Ru anomalies often indicate primitive chromitite-hosted PGE.
- **Drilling.** Diamond core almost exclusively (sulphides require structural / textural logging); typical 60–120 m × 60–120 m grid in feasibility.
- **Assay.** **Nickel-sulphide fire assay collection** is the industry standard for the full PGE suite (Ru, Rh, Pd, Os, Ir, Pt + Au) with Te coprecipitation and ICP-MS finish. Internal standards: Cd for the light triplet (Ru, Rh, Pd), Tl for the heavies (Os, Ir, Pt, Au) ([ScienceDirect — NiS FA + Te + ICP-MS](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/000925419090144V); [ALS — PGE assay](https://www.alsglobal.com/en/geochemistry/precious-metals-analysis/precious-metals-analysis/platinum-group-elements)). Pb-collection FA can be used for Pt + Pd + Au + Rh but **misses Ir, Os, Ru**.

### E. Mining methods

- **Conventional Bushveld reef** mining: trackless / hybrid mechanised stoping in narrow (~1 m) reefs at depths 500–2,000 m; very labour-intensive.
- **Platreef** and **J-M Reef** are mined by mechanised open-pit and large-stope underground respectively.
- **Norilsk** uses bulk underground methods (sublevel caving, cut-and-fill) given the massive sulphide bodies.

### F. Comminution & beneficiation

Pt is recovered via **flotation** of the base-metal sulphides that host it. Typical Bushveld UG2 flowsheet:

1. **Primary crush + SAG/ball mill** to P80 **75 µm**.
2. **Rougher flotation** at pH 9 with PAX/SIBX (50–150 g/t) + dithiophosphate + MIBC (30 g/t).
3. **Regrind** to P80 ~25–35 µm.
4. **Cleaner flotation** — 2–3 cleaning stages.
5. Final concentrate: ~150–300 g/t PGE+Au, sent to smelter.

Recoveries: Merensky 80–88% PGE; UG2 75–85% (chromite-rich, harder to float clean); Platreef ~75–85% ([Bushveld mineralogy review](https://encyclopedia.pub/7603)).

### G. Pyrometallurgy & refining

PGM refining is the most chemically intricate metallurgy in the precious-metals world. Major route:

1. **Smelter (electric furnace)** at ~1,400 °C produces a **PGM-bearing sulphide matte** (Cu-Ni-Fe).
2. **Converter** removes iron as FeO slag.
3. **Slow-cooled matte** is crushed, magnetic-separated (alloy fraction) and leached (sulphide fraction).
4. **Base-metal refinery (BMR)** — pressure leach in autoclaves with H₂SO₄/O₂ to dissolve Ni / Cu / Co, leaving a PGM-rich residue.
5. **Precious-metal refinery (PMR)** — sequential precipitation / solvent extraction:
   - Dissolve in aqua regia.
   - Selectively precipitate Au with FeSO₄; Pd with dimethylglyoxime; Pt as (NH₄)₂PtCl₆; Rh, Ir, Ru via complex SX and ion-exchange.
6. Final metals reduced under H₂ to **sponge or powder at 99.95%+ purity**.

Major PGM refineries: **Anglo American Platinum (Rustenburg / PMR Johannesburg), Impala Refining Services, Sibanye-Stillwater (Columbus MT + Springs SA), Norilsk Nickel (Krasnoyarsk), Johnson Matthey (UK), Heraeus (DE), Tanaka (JP)** ([Johnson Matthey](https://matthey.com/products-and-markets/pgms-and-circularity/pgm-markets/ruthenium); [USGS MCS 2024 PGM](https://pubs.usgs.gov/periodicals/mcs2024/mcs2024-platinum-group.pdf)).

### H. Movement, packaging, security

- PGM concentrate is shipped in sealed drums (1–2 t each) under chain-of-custody from mine → smelter; cross-border movements require export permit + customs assay.
- Refined metal moves in **sealed shotted sponge, ingots or powder** in tamper-evident containers via Brink's / Malca-Amit.
- Insurance valued per metal; rhodium-laden lots are highest-risk-per-kg in the entire bullion world.

### I. Pricing & markets

- **Benchmark.** LBMA Platinum Price (twice daily — 09:45 and 14:00 London).
- **2024 supply.** Global Pt mine production ~180 t; South Africa contributed **~70%**, Russia ~10%, Zimbabwe ~8%, North America ~5% ([USGS MCS 2025 PGM](https://pubs.usgs.gov/periodicals/mcs2025/mcs2025-platinum-group.pdf)).
- **Demand split.** Autocatalyst ~40%, jewellery ~25%, industrial (glass, chemical, electrical) ~25%, investment ~10%. Hydrogen / PEM fuel cells are the watch-this-space growth segment.
- **2024 price action.** Platinum prices changed only slightly vs 2023; substitution back from Pd → Pt in gasoline TWCs is supporting demand ([Auronum](https://auronum.co.uk/substitution-wars-how-platinum-is-winning-back-the-autocatalyst-market/); [USGS MCS 2025 PGM](https://pubs.usgs.gov/periodicals/mcs2025/mcs2025-platinum-group.pdf)).

### J. Environment, health & safety

- Chromite-bearing UG2 ore generates **Cr(VI) risk** in dust and leach solutions.
- Smelter SO₂ emissions are major; abatement (scrubbers, acid plants) is mandatory.
- Pt-salt sensitisation (occupational asthma) in refinery workers is a recognised hazard.

### K. Decision triggers Boji should encode

- `IF (owner = TZ ASM) AND (prospect = Pt-bearing) → DO NOT advise sole development; recommend "JV with major (e.g., Anglo Platinum, Sibanye, Impala, Implats) or sell prospect outright." Pt metallurgy is too capital-intensive for ASM.`
- `IF (PGE+Au assay) → require Ni-sulphide FA, NOT lead-collection FA, to avoid under-reporting Ir/Os/Ru.`
- `IF (UG2-style chromitite host) → flag chromite contamination of PGM concentrate as a smelter-penalty risk; require Cr₂O₃ < 3% in concentrate.`

Same as Pt for reef-type; for Norilsk-Talnakh, large-tonnage underground methods (mass-mining, sublevel-caving) on disseminated and massive sulphide ores.

### F. Comminution & beneficiation

### G. Pyrometallurgy & refining

### H. Movement, packaging, security

### I. Pricing & markets

- **2024 price action.** Pd average annual price **−27%** vs 2023 as substitution to Pt and EV penetration eroded gasoline-TWC demand ([USGS MCS 2025 PGM](https://pubs.usgs.gov/periodicals/mcs2025/mcs2025-platinum-group.pdf)).
- **US import sources, palladium (2020–23):** Russia 32%, South Africa 32%, Belgium 8%, Italy 8%, other 20% ([USGS MCS 2025 PGM](https://pubs.usgs.gov/periodicals/mcs2025/mcs2025-platinum-group.pdf)).

### J. Environment, health & safety

Same as Pt. Pd-DMG processing handles toxic organic reagents; ventilation and PPE mandatory.

## 5. Rhodium (Rh)

### A. Identity

| Attribute | Value |
|---|---|
| Symbol | Rh |
| Atomic number | 45 |
| Atomic mass | 102.9055 u |
| Density | 12.41 g/cm³ |
| Melting point | 1,964 °C |
| Stable isotopes | ¹⁰³Rh (only) |
| Crustal abundance | ~0.0002 ppm (one of the rarest elements in the crust) |

**Principal ore minerals.** No major rhodium-only mineral exists. Rh is hosted as **substitution in laurite (RuS₂) and related Os–Ir–Ru sulphides** (in chromitite layers, especially **UG2** of Bushveld), and as unnamed (Pt,Rh,Cu)S phases ([Bushveld mineralogy](https://encyclopedia.pub/7603)).

### B. Geology of formation & deposit types

Almost exclusively recovered from **chromitite-hosted PGE reefs**, especially **UG2 (Bushveld)** and the **LG/MG chromitite seams** of the lower Bushveld. UG2 is the world's primary Rh source.

### C. Tanzania / East Africa relevance

**Effectively zero TZ ASM presence.** No commercial Rh production in Tanzania or East Africa. Any prospect with Rh credits must be considered a sub-component of a multi-PGE play; never primary.

### D. Exploration & determination

Ni-sulphide FA + ICP-MS only. Pb-collection FA recovers Rh at ~80% efficiency (sub-optimal). Detection limits: <1 ppb by NiS-FA-ICP-MS.

### E. Mining methods

Same as UG2 mining at Bushveld — narrow-reef mechanised stoping.

### F. Comminution & beneficiation

Same UG2 flotation flowsheet as Pt/Pd. Rh recovery to concentrate ~70–80% — slightly lower than Pt/Pd due to its preferential partitioning into refractory chromite-associated phases.

### G. Refining

Within the PGM refinery, Rh is one of the **last metals separated**, after Au-Pd-Pt removal. Anion exchange and selective precipitation as **(NH₄)₃RhCl₆** then reduction under H₂. Refined to 99.95%+ sponge / powder.

### H. Movement, packaging, security

By value-per-kg, refined Rh is the most valuable PGM in transport. Small sealed lots (often <1 kg) move via Brink's / Malca-Amit under specialist insurance.

### I. Pricing & markets

- **No daily LBMA auction**; reference price is the **Johnson Matthey base price** (twice daily) and the LPPM (London Platinum & Palladium Market) reference.
- **2024 price action.** Rh average annual price **−31%** vs 2023, recovering from a >US$15,000/oz peak in 2021 but still elevated by absolute standards ([USGS MCS 2025 PGM](https://pubs.usgs.gov/periodicals/mcs2025/mcs2025-platinum-group.pdf); [Amanda van Dyke — Rhodium criticality](https://amandavandyke.substack.com/p/rhodium-the-second-most-critical)).
- **Supply.** South Africa supplies **>80%** of mined Rh ([Phoenix Refining — iridium supply analog](https://www.phoenixrefining.com/blog/global-iridium-supply-geology-concentration-and-strategic-risk)); rest from Russia + Zimbabwe + recycling.
- **Demand.** **~80–85% autocatalyst** — Rh is the unique NOx-reduction catalyst in three-way converters, **no viable substitute** ([BR Metals 2024 forecast](https://www.brmetalsltd.com/2024-price-forecast-for-platinum-palladium-rhodium-and-navigating-the-future-of-catalytic-converter-recycling/)). Euro 7 (2025) tightens NOx limits, increasing Rh loading per catalyst ([BR Metals](https://www.brmetalsltd.com/2024-price-forecast-for-platinum-palladium-rhodium-and-navigating-the-future-of-catalytic-converter-recycling/)).
- **Persistent structural deficit** — mining output flat since 2020, 5–10% annual supply gap ([BR Metals](https://www.brmetalsltd.com/2024-price-forecast-for-platinum-palladium-rhodium-and-navigating-the-future-of-catalytic-converter-recycling/)).

### J. Environment, health & safety

Cr(VI) risk from UG2 chromitite processing; otherwise same hazard profile as Pt/Pd refining.

### K. Decision triggers Boji should encode

- `IF (any TZ prospect claims Rh primary economics) → flag as commercially non-viable for ASM; advise multi-PGE JV with major or commodity rights sale.`
- `IF (catalyst recycling opportunity in TZ supply chain) → high Rh-per-tonne value justifies separate recycling stream from spent autocatalysts; refer owner to ICMI-aligned recyclers.`

**Principal ore minerals.** Mostly as **substitution in laurite (RuS₂)**, irarsite (IrAsS), **osmiridium (Os,Ir alloy)**, **iridosmine ((Ir,Os) alloy)**, and Ir-bearing Pt-Fe alloys (isoferroplatinum) ([Wikipedia — Iridium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iridium)).

### B. Geology of formation & deposit types

Recovered as by-product from PGE reefs (mainly **UG2 Bushveld**, **Norilsk**, **Stillwater**). Slightly enriched in chromitite-hosted ores vs sulphide-hosted reefs. No primary iridium mine exists.

### C. Tanzania / East Africa relevance

**Effectively zero TZ presence.** No ASM economy for Ir.

### D. Exploration & determination

Ni-sulphide FA + ICP-MS only. Pb-collection FA does not recover Ir reliably ([ALS PGE](https://www.alsglobal.com/en/geochemistry/precious-metals-analysis/precious-metals-analysis/platinum-group-elements)).

### E–F. Mining and beneficiation

Recovered piggybacked on PGE flotation flowsheets.

### G. Refining

Within PGM refinery, Ir is separated by **anion exchange** and selective hydrolysis after Os removal (Os is volatilised as OsO₄ first). Final reduction under H₂ to Ir powder at 99.95%+.

### H. Movement, packaging, security

Same as Rh — small high-value sealed lots.

### I. Pricing & markets

- **Demand.** **Electronics ~55%** (sapphire / single-crystal growth crucibles), spark plugs (long-life iridium-tip), chemical catalysts (acetic acid via Cativa process), **PEM electrolyser electrodes (green hydrogen)**, IMO ballast-water treatment electrodes (effective 2024) ([Sa Materials](https://www.samaterials.com/5-common-uses-of-iridium.html); [Phoenix Refining](https://www.phoenixrefining.com/blog/global-iridium-supply-geology-concentration-and-strategic-risk); [Brian D. Colwell](https://briandcolwell.com/interesting-facts-about-iridium-a-critical-raw-material/)).
- **2024 price action.** Ir average annual price **+3%** — the only PGM to rise in 2024 ([USGS MCS 2025 PGM](https://pubs.usgs.gov/periodicals/mcs2025/mcs2025-platinum-group.pdf)). Driven by green-hydrogen capex (BASF 54-MW PEM electrolyser commissioned 2025) ([Brian D. Colwell](https://briandcolwell.com/interesting-facts-about-iridium-a-critical-raw-material/)).
- **Supply.** South Africa **>80%**; Russia **11–12%** (~200–400 kg/yr); rest Zimbabwe + recycling ([Phoenix Refining](https://www.phoenixrefining.com/blog/global-iridium-supply-geology-concentration-and-strategic-risk)).

### J. Environment, health & safety

Refining risk: **osmium tetroxide (OsO₄)** co-produced during Ir/Os separation is highly toxic; closed-circuit absorption mandatory.

### K. Decision triggers Boji should encode

- `IF (any TZ owner claims Ir-primary opportunity) → reject as primary; only viable as multi-PGE by-product credit.`
- `IF (green-hydrogen-electrolyser supply chain query) → flag Ir as a long-run scarcity risk; substitution research (Ru-Ir, Ni-mesh) is active but immature.`

## 7. Osmium (Os)

### A. Identity

| Attribute | Value |
|---|---|
| Symbol | Os |
| Atomic number | 76 |
| Atomic mass | 190.23 u |
| Density | 22.59 g/cm³ (often cited as the densest stable element) |
| Melting point | 3,033 °C |
| Stable isotopes | ¹⁸⁴Os, ¹⁸⁶Os, ¹⁸⁷Os, ¹⁸⁸Os, ¹⁸⁹Os, ¹⁹⁰Os, ¹⁹²Os |
| Crustal abundance | ~0.0015 ppb (rivals Ir for rarity) |

**Principal ore minerals.** **Osmiridium ((Os,Ir))**, **iridosmine**, **laurite (RuS₂) with substitutional Os**, native osmium alloys with Pt and Ir. ([MetaMetals](https://metametals.com/osmium-info))

### B. Geology of formation & deposit types

By-product of PGE mining (especially Bushveld UG2 and Norilsk), with historic alluvial sources in the Urals.

### C. Tanzania / East Africa relevance

**Zero commercial relevance.**

### D. Exploration & determination

Ni-sulphide FA + ICP-MS or specialist Carius-tube digestion + Os isotope dilution MS for Re-Os geochronology.

### G. Refining

**Os is volatilised as OsO₄ at the start of PGM refining** (boiling point of OsO₄ is ~130 °C — the lowest-boiling oxide of any metal). Absorbed in KOH → reduced → Os powder. The OsO₄ vapour is **acutely toxic** (irritant, corneal damage) — closed-circuit handling and SCBA mandatory.

### I. Pricing & markets

- Tiny global market — only **a few hundred kilograms produced per year** ([MetaMetals](https://metametals.com/osmium-info)).
- **Primary uses.** OsO₄ for biological electron-microscopy fixative; Os–Ir tips for fountain pens, compass bearings, instrument pivots; minor jewellery / collectible markets.
- No daily benchmark; quoted by Heraeus / Johnson Matthey on enquiry. Investment osmium ("crystalline osmium") is a niche collectible market with its own certification chain.

### J. Environment, health & safety

OsO₄ is the headline hazard — TLV-TWA 0.0002 mg/m³ (one of the lowest workplace exposure limits in chemistry). Refineries handle Os under sealed scrubbers.

### K. Decision triggers Boji should encode

- `IF (owner inquires re Os exposure or supply) → flag as non-investable at ASM scale; advise sourcing through specialist Os recyclers, not mining.`

## 8. Ruthenium (Ru)

### A. Identity

| Attribute | Value |
|---|---|
| Symbol | Ru |
| Atomic number | 44 |
| Atomic mass | 101.07 u |
| Density | 12.45 g/cm³ |
| Melting point | 2,334 °C |
| Stable isotopes | ⁹⁶Ru, ⁹⁸Ru, ⁹⁹Ru, ¹⁰⁰Ru, ¹⁰¹Ru, ¹⁰²Ru, ¹⁰⁴Ru |
| Crustal abundance | ~0.001 ppb |

**Principal ore minerals.** **Laurite (RuS₂)** — the most abundant Ru mineral, common in chromitites; ruarsite (RuAsS), Ru-bearing alloys with Os and Ir. ([Bushveld mineralogy](https://encyclopedia.pub/7603))

### B. Geology of formation & deposit types

By-product of PGE reef and Cu-Ni-PGE sulphide mining. Concentrated in chromitite layers (UG2, LG6), so UG2 is the major commercial Ru source.

### C. Tanzania / East Africa relevance

**Zero commercial relevance.**

### D. Exploration & determination

Ni-sulphide FA + ICP-MS.

### F. Beneficiation

Recovered with the rest of the PGE in flotation concentrate.

### G. Refining

In the PGM refinery, after Os and Ir are removed, Ru is precipitated as **(NH₄)₃RuCl₆** or distilled as **RuO₄** (analogous to OsO₄), reduced to powder. Final purity 99.9–99.95%.

### H. Movement & security

Same as Rh / Ir — small sealed lots, specialist couriers.

### I. Pricing & markets

- **No formal daily fix**; Johnson Matthey / Heraeus reference prices.
- **2024 price action.** Ru average annual price **−6%** vs 2023 ([USGS MCS 2025 PGM](https://pubs.usgs.gov/periodicals/mcs2025/mcs2025-platinum-group.pdf)).
- **Demand mix.**
  - **Electronics — hard-disk-drive (HDD) perpendicular-magnetic-recording (PMR) under-layer**: Ru is the seed-layer that made >10 TB drives possible ([Tanaka Precious Metals](https://tanaka-preciousmetals.com/en/library/element/column04/); [US Patent US20130235490A1](https://patents.google.com/patent/US20130235490)). Cloud-storage CAPEX is the main demand driver.
  - **Chlor-alkali industry** — Ru-coated DSA® (dimensionally stable anodes) for chlorine production ([Johnson Matthey](https://matthey.com/products-and-markets/pgms-and-circularity/pgm-markets/ruthenium)).
  - **IMO ballast-water treatment electrodes** (Ru-Ir mixed oxides) — effective September 2024 ([Brian D. Colwell](https://briandcolwell.com/interesting-facts-about-iridium-a-critical-raw-material/)).
  - **Chemical catalysts** — ammonia synthesis variants, hydrogenations, fine-chemicals.
  - **Emerging** — Ru-based PEM-electrolyser anode research (substitute for Ir).
- **Supply.** South Africa >80%, Russia ~10%, rest Zimbabwe + recycling.

### J. Environment, health & safety

RuO₄ analogous to OsO₄ — volatile, toxic, oxidising. Closed-circuit only.

### K. Decision triggers Boji should encode

- `IF (TZ owner has Ru-bearing concentrate) → only relevant inside a multi-PGE concentrate; refining must be at certified PGM refinery (Anglo Plat / Impala / JM / Heraeus / Tanaka).`
- `IF (cloud-storage CAPEX cycle improving) → expect Ru demand tailwind from PMR-HDD substrates.`

### M1. Junior-Agent routing matrix

| Owner question | Primary Junior Agent | Secondary |
|---|---|---|
| "What's my best beneficiation route for sulphide-hosted Au at 4 g/t with 60% sulphide-S?" | Metallurgy | Geology |

| "How much cyanide do I need for 1 t of oxide ore?" | Metallurgy | EHS |
| "Is my mercury practice Minamata-compliant?" | Compliance | EHS |

### M2. Worked example — "Sulphide-hosted Au at 4 g/t, 60% S²⁻"

If a Boji owner reports **Au = 4 g/t, ore_type = sulphide, S²⁻ = 60% sulphide sulphur (i.e., highly pyritic), As ~0.3%, Sb ~0.1%**:

1. **Refractoriness test:** bottle-roll CN leach. If Au recovery < 80% → REFRACTORY pathway. With 0.3% As this is likely.
2. **Recommended flowsheet:** Crush to P80 ~2 mm → SAG/ball-mill grind to **P80 75 µm** → gravity (Knelson) for any free Au (typically captures 10–25% of contained Au upstream) → bulk sulphide flotation (PAX 80 g/t, MIBC 30 g/t, pH 9, CaO conditioning) → concentrate grade ~30–50 g/t Au at 80–88% Au recovery.
3. **Concentrate treatment:** because S²⁻ is very high, sell concentrate to a smelter (e.g., Boliden, Glencore) under custom-smelter terms, OR run **pressure oxidation (POX) on-site** if scale > 200 kt/yr; ultra-fine grind → POX at 220 °C / 32 bar / 60–120 min → cyanide leach of oxidised residue → recovery typically 90–95% of contained Au.
4. **Capex check:** POX plant is US$150–500 m capex — only justified above ~200 koz/yr production. **For an ASM-scale owner, Boji must default to "sell concentrate, do not build POX."**

### M3. Tanzania regulation cheat-sheet (gold + silver doré)

- **Royalty:** 6% (conventional export) OR 4% (BoT DGPP route only). ([Mining Commission](https://www.tumemadini.go.tz/mineral-trade/mineral-royalties-and-inspection-fees-rates/))
- **Inspection fee:** 1% conventional, 0% DGPP.
- **VAT:** Standard 18%, zero-rated for DGPP refined gold to BoT.
- **Export permit:** Required for every shipment, issued by Mining Commission at MTC after GMO valuation.
- **Settlement currency:** TZS for DGPP (24-h payment); USD for conventional export (typically T+3).
- **Refining-in-country requirement:** Since 2019, doré >250 g must be refined in TZ before export.

### M4. PGM-for-ASM "stop sign"

If a Tanzanian owner approaches Boji with a PGM-bearing prospect, the **default recommendation is one of three**:

1. **Joint-venture with a major** (Anglo American Platinum, Impala Platinum, Sibanye-Stillwater, Norilsk Nickel) — they have the refining infrastructure.
2. **Sell the asset / option** to a junior PGE explorer (Group Ten, Bravada Gold, similar).
3. **Convert to a Cu-Ni primary play** if base-metal grades are economic standalone, with PGE as by-product credit.

Boji should **never** recommend that a TZ ASM owner build a stand-alone PGM mine or refinery. The chemistry, capex and downstream commercial relationships are out of reach for any non-major operator in 2026.
