## 1. Uranium (U)

Uranium is the single most politically charged commodity Boji will ever advise on. The decision tree is not "how do I sell this?" — it is "should this owner even be in possession of this material, and which government agency must know first?" Every Boji response for a uranium prospect must trigger the regulatory-pathway flag before any commercial discussion.

### 1.A Identity

Uranium occurs in nature almost exclusively as a 99.27% ²³⁸U / 0.72% ²³⁵U / trace ²³⁴U isotope mix. ²³⁵U is the fissile isotope; everything in the front-end fuel cycle exists to concentrate it from 0.72% to 3-5% (LWR fuel) or 19.75% (HALEU for advanced reactors and SMRs).

Principal ore minerals (ordered by commercial importance):

| Mineral | Formula | Typical grade range | Where it dominates |
|---|---|---|---|
| Uraninite | UO₂ (variably oxidised toward U₃O₈) | 0.05% – 20%+ U₃O₈ | Unconformity, vein deposits |
| Pitchblende | Massive cryptocrystalline uraninite | Up to 20%+ U₃O₈ at Cigar Lake | Athabasca Basin, Shinkolobwe |
| Coffinite | USiO₄·nH₂O | 0.05% – 0.3% U₃O₈ | Sandstone-hosted (Wyoming, Kazakhstan) |
| Brannerite | (U,Ca,Ce)(Ti,Fe)₂O₆ | Refractory, 0.1% – 0.5% | Olympic Dam, Crocker Well |
| Carnotite | K₂(UO₂)₂(VO₄)₂·3H₂O | Surface oxidised | Colorado Plateau, Niger calcretes |
| Autunite | Ca(UO₂)₂(PO₄)₂·10H₂O | Secondary oxidised | Phosphate by-product, supergene |

The grade benchmark for global market thinking: Athabasca unconformity ores at McArthur River average **6.55% U₃O₈** in proven and probable reserves, two orders of magnitude richer than the global average grade of roughly 0.1% [Cameco 2023 Cigar Lake Technical Report](https://www.cameco.com/sites/default/files/documents/cameco-2023-cigar-lake-technical-report.pdf). Cigar Lake processes ores rated >15% U₃O₈ at the McClean Lake mill — the highest commercial grade on earth [Cameco — Cigar Lake](https://www.cameco.com/businesses/uranium-operations/canada/cigar-lake).

### 1.B Geology of formation & deposit types

Uranium is concentrated by six dominant deposit-forming processes; Boji must be able to recognise the *deposit type* from the owner's description because the deposit type dictates everything downstream — mining method, leach chemistry, environmental risk, and licensing pathway.

1. **Unconformity-type** (highest grade on earth). Hydrothermal fluids at a basement / overlying sandstone interface; reductant-rich graphitic pelites trap U⁶⁺ → U⁴⁺. Athabasca Basin (Canada): McArthur River, Cigar Lake, Key Lake. Grades 1% – 20%+ U₃O₈. McArthur River 2024 output: **20.3 Mlb U₃O₈ (+43% YoY)** [INN — Uranium Mining in Canada 2025](https://investingnews.com/daily/resource-investing/energy-investing/uranium-investing/uranium-mining-in-canada/).
2. **Sandstone-hosted (roll-front)** — the global supply backbone. Oxidised groundwater carries soluble U⁶⁺ as uranyl carbonate; precipitates at a redox front against organic-rich or pyritic sands. Found in Wyoming, Texas, and crucially the Chu-Sarysu and Syr-Darya basins of Kazakhstan. Grades 0.02% – 0.3% U₃O₈ but extracted by in-situ recovery at very low cost.
3. **IOCG (iron oxide-copper-gold)** — Olympic Dam, South Australia. Uranium is a by-product of a giant Cu-Au-Ag system; brannerite is refractory.
4. **Vein deposits** — historic Shinkolobwe (DRC), Jáchymov (Czechia), Limousin (France). Mostly exhausted.
5. **Calcrete / surficial** — Langer Heinrich (Namibia), Yeelirrie (Australia). U adsorbed on caliche; alkaline-leachable.
6. **Quartz-pebble conglomerate (palaeoplacer)** — Witwatersrand (South Africa). U as by-product of historic gold mining, sub-economic at modern uranium prices.
7. **Phosphate by-product** — uranium recovered from wet-process phosphoric acid (Florida, Morocco). Marginal economics, occasionally revived when U > $80/lb.

**Tanzania-relevant geology:** the Mkuju River deposit (Selous-Selous-Selous corridor, Namtumbo District) is hosted in Karoo Supergroup sandstones — a **sandstone-hosted, mostly oxidised, very-low-grade** orebody. Average grade is only **0.037% U₃O₈** [Mining.com — Russian state corp suspends Mkuju](https://www.mining.com/russian-state-corporation-suspends-1-2-billion-uranium-project-tanzania/) — five times lower than even the global sandstone-hosted average. Only ISR economics can make this work.

The Tanzanian government has initiated a Strategic Environmental Assessment from November 2024 onward [Pan African Visions — Tanzania's Uranium Dawn](https://panafricanvisions.com/2026/05/tanzanias-uranium-dawn-deputy-minister-pushes-mantra-to-accelerate-mkuju-river-project/).

2. Any uranium showing reported by a Boji user almost certainly falls under TAEC jurisdiction (see 1.J).

### 1.D Exploration & determination

Uranium is unique among Boji's commodity universe in that it gives off its own diagnostic signal: gamma radiation from ²¹⁴Bi and ²¹⁴Pb (daughters of ²²²Rn from the ²³⁸U series). The workflow:

1. **Airborne radiometric survey** with a 256-channel gamma-spectrometer. Three windows are measured: K (¹⁴⁰⁰-¹⁵⁷⁰ keV), U-equivalent via ²¹⁴Bi (¹⁶⁶⁰-¹⁸⁶⁰ keV), and Th-equivalent via ²⁰⁸Tl (²⁴¹⁰-²⁸²⁰ keV). The "U" channel always reads *equivalent U₃O₈ assuming secular equilibrium* — disequilibrium (radon loss, leaching) is the single largest source of error.
2. **Ground gamma spectrometry** with RS-230 / RS-125 instruments for anomaly follow-up.
3. **Track-etch radon detectors** for buried orebodies.
4. **Drilling with downhole gamma logging** (cps × calibration factor → eU₃O₈ grade per 0.1 m interval). For ISR projects, gamma logs are the *primary* grade record; chemical assays only spot-check.
5. **Confirmation chemical assay** by delayed-neutron activation analysis (DNAA) or ICP-MS (after acid digestion).
6. **Pathfinder elements** for sandstone-hosted: Mo, V, Se, P, Cu — all enriched at the same redox front.

Lab houses for uranium-specific work: SGS Lakefield (Ontario), Saskatchewan Research Council (SRC), ALS Mineral Division (Vancouver/Brisbane). African field samples typically routed through SGS Johannesburg.

### 1.E Mining methods

| Method | Where dominant | Footprint | Capex | Boji guidance |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **In-situ recovery (ISR)** | Kazakhstan, USA, now planned at Mkuju River | Wellfield only; no waste rock, no tailings | Low | Default for sandstone-hosted |
| **Open-pit** | Husab (Namibia), Rössing (Namibia), historically Ranger (Australia) | Large; tailings dam | Very high | Only viable >0.1% U₃O₈ |
| **Underground** | Cigar Lake (freeze-mining), McArthur River (raise-bore) | Small surface, complex underground | Highest | Only for unconformity ultra-high-grade |
| **By-product** | Olympic Dam, phosphate operations | Shared with host metal | Sunk | Boji never advises on this — host metal economics dominate |

**ISR is now over 50% of global uranium output, ~32,000 tU in 2024** [Kazatomprom 2024 Annual Report](https://www.kazatomprom.kz/storage/59/kap_iar_2024_eng_0205__clean.pdf). Of that, **Kazatomprom alone produced 23,300 tU (100% basis) in 2024 — 73% of all global ISR production, 21% of global mined uranium on attributable basis** [Kazatomprom 2024 IAR](https://www.kazatomprom.kz/storage/59/kap_iar_2024_eng_0205__clean.pdf).

How ISR works:
1. Drill a pattern of injection wells (typically 5-spot or 7-spot, 30-50 m spacing) into a confined, permeable, mineralised aquifer.
2. Inject a lixiviant — either **sulphuric acid + oxidant** (Kazakh/US standard) or **sodium carbonate-bicarbonate + oxygen** (alkaline, used at Beverley/Honeymoon Australia and proposed for Mkuju to protect carbonate gangue).
3. Pregnant solution flows toward production wells, surfaces via pumps.
4. Liquor → ion-exchange (IX) column with strong-base resin → elute with NaCl or NH₄NO₃ → precipitate yellowcake.

ISR delivers cash costs in the **$15-25/lb U₃O₈** range vs. **$30-50/lb** for conventional underground. This is why Mkuju's 0.037% grade is even contemplated.

### 1.F Beneficiation & processing

For conventional ore (Athabasca, Namibia):

1. Crush and grind to 80% passing 75 µm.
2. **Acid leach**: sulphuric acid (50-100 kg/t ore) + oxidant (MnO₂, NaClO₃, Fe³⁺, or H₂O₂); 4-24 hr; 50-60°C. U⁴⁺ → UO₂²⁺.
3. **Alkaline leach** for high-carbonate ores: Na₂CO₃ + NaHCO₃ + air/O₂; longer, hotter, higher reagent cost, but avoids dissolving carbonate gangue.
4. **Solid-liquid separation**: CCD thickeners or belt filter.
5. **Ion exchange (IX)**: strong-base anionic resin (Dowex, Purolite); load up to 60 g U/L resin.
6. **Solvent extraction (SX)**: tertiary amines (Alamine 336) in kerosene; strip with NaCl or (NH₄)₂SO₄.
7. **Precipitation**: ammonium di-uranate (ADU, yellow), magnesium di-uranate (MDU), or sodium di-uranate; or H₂O₂ for uranium peroxide (UO₄·2H₂O).
8. **Calcination** at 700-800°C → U₃O₈ powder ("yellowcake"), 75-85% U₃O₈ assay basis.

**Conversion → enrichment → fabrication** (off-mine, off-Boji-scope but understand):
- U₃O₈ → UF₆ at Cameco Port Hope (Canada), Orano Pierrelatte (France), CNNC Lanzhou (China)
- UF₆ → 3.5-5% ²³⁵U via gas centrifuge (Urenco, Orano, Tenex, CNNC)
- Enriched UF₆ → UO₂ pellets → fuel rods at fabricators (Westinghouse, Framatome, GNF, KEPCO NF)

### 1.H Movement, packaging, hazard

Uranium yellowcake is a **Class 7 (radioactive)** dangerous good but moves under the **Low Specific Activity (LSA-I)** sub-category — it is far less hazardous than enriched material because the alpha emission is contained by the drum wall.

- **UN 2912** — Radioactive material, LSA-I — natural uranium U₃O₈ in steel drums (208 L), 4-5 drums per ISO container, max ~12 t U₃O₈/container.
- **UN 3324 / 3325** — fissile LSA (rare for natural U).
- **UN 2977 (UF₆ fissile) / UN 2978 (UF₆ non-fissile)** — Type B(U) cylinders (48Y for natural UF₆, 30B for enriched).
- IAEA Safety Series SSR-6 governs transport.
- **Tanzania route approval**: TAEC must authorise routing under the Packaging and Transport of Radioactive Material Regulations 2011 [TAEC IRRS report](https://www.iaea.org/sites/default/files/2025-03/irrs_tanzania_final_report.pdf).

## 2. Thorium (Th)

Thorium is uranium's poorer cousin: 3-4x more abundant in the crust (~10 ppm vs U ~3 ppm), but with no commercial fuel-cycle pull because no country has commercialised a Th-fuelled power reactor at scale. Boji's posture: **handle Th as a regulatory by-product of REE/Ti/Zr mineral-sands operations, not as a sellable commodity.**

### 2.A Identity

| Mineral | Formula | Th content |
|---|---|---|
| Monazite | (Ce,La,Nd,Th)PO₄ | 4-12% ThO₂ |
| Thorite | ThSiO₄ | Up to 80% ThO₂ (rare) |
| Thorianite | ThO₂ | Pure (very rare) |

The world's commercially significant thorium is **monazite found in heavy-mineral beach sand placers**, almost always co-mined with ilmenite, rutile, zircon, and minor REEs.

### 2.B Geology of formation & deposit types

Monazite is a heavy (4.6-5.4 SG), chemically robust accessory mineral. It survives weathering, gets transported to rivers, ends up on beaches where wave action sorts it into placer concentrations alongside other heavy minerals. Globally significant resources:

- **Kerala coast, India** — the world's classic monazite beach (and historic source of India's strategic Th stockpile).
- **Brazil — Bahia, Espírito Santo** coast.
- **Madagascar** — Toliara, Ranobe.
- **Tanzania Pwani coast** — Tajiri, Fungoni (see 2.C).

There is also a smaller carbonatite-hosted Th class (Steenkampskraal, South Africa).

### 2.C Tanzania / East Africa relevance

Two Boji-relevant projects:

1. **Tajiri Heavy Mineral Sands Project (Nyati Mineral Sands Ltd)** — 30 km of coastline in Pangani District, Tanga Region. **~268 Mt of heavy mineral sands; 74 Mt proven reserves**, will produce **zircon, titanium minerals, monazite (REE-bearing), and garnet** [Medium — Tanzania's heavy mineral sands](https://medium.com/@kheri.mahimbali/tanzanias-promising-heavy-mineral-sands-projects-fungoni-and-tajiri-72c44f905fdc).
2. **Fungoni HMS Project (Strandline Resources / Jacana Resources)** — open-pit, ~25 km south of Dar es Salaam port; produces ilmenite, rutile, and a combined zircon-monazite product [Mining Technology — Fungoni Heavy Mineral Sands Project](https://www.mining-technology.com/projects/fungoni-heavy-mineral-sands-project/).

For both, **the monazite stream contains 6-9% ThO₂ and 0.2-0.3% U₃O₈** — both NORM (Naturally Occurring Radioactive Material). Under IAEA Safety Standards GSR Part 3 and Tanzania's Mining (Radioactive Minerals) Regulations 2018, monazite handling, storage, and transport requires TAEC licensing whenever activity concentration exceeds 1 Bq/g (a threshold that monazite always crosses).

### 2.D Exploration & determination

- Same airborne radiometrics as uranium (Th channel, ²⁰⁸Tl peak).
- Beach traverses with hand-held gamma-spec.
- Bulk sampling → heavy-liquid separation (sodium polytungstate or LST) → mineralogical analysis by QEMSCAN or TIMA.
- Chemical assay by XRF (for ThO₂) or ICP-MS.

### 2.E Mining methods

- **Dredge mining** (wet mining) is standard for active beach/lagoon sands.
- **Dry mining** with dozers/loaders for elevated palaeo-beaches.

### 2.F Beneficiation & processing

1. Wet concentrator plant (WCP): spiral concentrators concentrate heavy minerals to 95%+ heavy mineral concentrate (HMC).
2. Mineral separation plant (MSP): electrostatic + magnetic separators yield discrete streams of ilmenite, rutile, zircon, monazite/xenotime.
3. **Monazite cracking**: NaOH digestion at 140-150°C (Bhabha process) → soluble Na₃PO₄ + insoluble (RE, Th)(OH)₃ cake → selective acid dissolution → solvent extraction separates Th from REEs.
4. ThO₂ rarely commercialised; usually stockpiled or returned to tailings under regulatory permit.

### 2.G Refining / final product

There is essentially no merchant market for ThO₂. Indian DAE, Chinese CNNC, and a handful of research institutes are the only buyers. ThO₂ contracts when they exist are bilateral, opaque, and quoted in tens of USD per kg.

### 2.H Movement, packaging, hazard

Monazite mineral concentrate moves as **UN 2912 (LSA-I)** in big bags or ISO containers; specific activity 100-200 Bq/g; route approval required. ThO₂ purified product moves as **UN 2910 / 2912** depending on form.

### 2.I Pricing & markets

No transparent price index. Th is effectively a **regulatory liability cost line** for HMS producers, not a revenue line. Boji should encode this: *"For a beach-sand operation, model Th as cost (storage, monitoring, NORM disposal) not revenue, unless the buyer is named (DAE India, CNNC Beijing)."*

### 2.J EHS

NORM regime: alpha-emitting Th-232 series; ²²⁰Rn (thoron) in handling areas; long-lived ²²⁸Ra and ²²⁸Th in tailings. Mitigation via dust suppression, ventilation, engineered tailings under IAEA SSG-26.

### 2.K Decision triggers

- **HMS operator must declare monazite stream to TAEC even if not selling it.**
- **Never advise selling monazite into REE channels without separating Th** — the buyer will reject on radiometric scan at port.

## 3. Coal

Coal is Boji's most numerically-spread commodity: a smallholder in Mbeya might bag and truck 5 t/day of cooking-grade coal to a Dar nyumbani brick kiln; a STAMICO-tied operation at Kiwira targets 200 MW thermal power exports plus regional industrial customers. The single most important Boji rule for coal: **differentiate thermal vs coking BEFORE you price**. The spread is routinely 3-5x.

### 3.A Identity — rank and classification

Coal is classified by **rank**, which correlates with carbon content, calorific value, volatile matter, and moisture. The standardised rank ladder (ASTM D388):

| Rank | Fixed Carbon (DAF) | VM (DAF) | GCV (NAR, kcal/kg) | Typical use |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Lignite (brown) | <60% | >32% | 2,500-4,200 | Mine-mouth power |
| Sub-bituminous | 60-69% | 25-35% | 4,200-5,500 | Power (Indonesian RB1/RB2 export) |
| Bituminous high-vol | 69-78% | 22-31% | 5,500-7,200 | Power (Newcastle 6000), some coking |

### 3.B Geology of formation & deposit types

Coal is the diagenetic and metamorphic product of accumulated plant material from peat swamps. Three globally relevant epochs:

1. **Carboniferous (~360-300 Ma)** — Northern hemisphere classics: Appalachians, UK, Donbass, Ruhr. Mostly bituminous to anthracite.
2. **Permian (~300-250 Ma)** — Southern hemisphere Gondwana: Karoo Supergroup across Southern Africa, Bowen Basin Australia, Damodar Valley India. **This is the geological setting for Tanzanian coal** (Songwe-Kiwira, Mchuchuma, Ngaka basins).
3. **Tertiary (~65-2 Ma)** — Indonesian (Kalimantan), Australian Surat Basin, lignites.

Tanzanian coal basins all sit in **Karoo Supergroup rift sediments** — the Permian Ecca Group equivalent — across the Songwe-Kiwira, Ketewaka-Mchuchuma, Ngaka, and Galula sub-basins of the Tanzania-Malawi-Zambia trijunction.

### 3.F Beneficiation & processing

Standard prep plant flowsheet:
1. **Run-of-mine (ROM)** crushing to <50 mm.
2. **Heavy-media (dense-media) cyclone wash** at 1.50-1.70 g/cc cut-point — separates clean coal float from rock/middlings sink.
3. **Spiral/jig** for intermediate size fractions.
4. **Froth flotation** for fines (<0.5 mm).
5. **Dewatering** (centrifuge for coarse, filter press for fines).
6. **Briquetting** for low-rank coal (lignite, sub-bit), often with molasses or starch binder.

For **coking coal** (does not apply to Tanzania currently): carbonisation in slot-type coke ovens at 1100°C for 16-20 hr → metallurgical coke + coke oven gas + tar + ammonia + light oils.

## 4. Helium (He)

Helium is Tanzania's most globally strategic and least understood mineral story. The Rukwa discovery is unlike anything else in the East African mining narrative: a non-hydrocarbon noble gas, sourced from radiogenic alpha decay in Precambrian basement, trapped under Karoo-age sediments, and accessible to a tiny AIM-listed explorer (Helium One Global) that could realistically supply 5-10% of world demand if commercialised. Boji's job: **route helium prospects to the right gas industry counterparts (Air Liquide / Linde / Air Products), priced in USD per litre of liquid helium, never to a minerals dealer.**

### 4.A Identity

Helium = ⁴He, the alpha-decay daughter of ²³⁸U, ²³⁵U, ²³²Th in the deep crust over geological time. The ⁴He atom is the most diffusive, lightest noble gas; once liberated from its parent crystal, it migrates rapidly through the crust and is *only retained* in trap structures with **exceptional cap-rock integrity** (anhydrite, halite, thick shale). The other isotope, ³He, is primordial mantle-derived, found in trace amounts and worth ~$10,000/L for fusion research and neutron detection — but ³He is not what the Rukwa story is about.

### 4.B Geology of formation & deposit types

Commercial helium is a **non-hydrocarbon natural-gas reservoir** where helium concentration is high enough (typically >0.3% mol He) to extract economically. Three global classes:

1. **Hydrocarbon by-product (LNG / natural gas)** — the dominant supply route. He concentrations 0.04% – 0.5%. Producers piggyback He extraction onto cryogenic LNG plants. Examples: Hugoton (USA), North Field (Qatar — world's #2 He source), In Salah (Algeria), Amur (Russia, ramping fast).
2. **Helium-rich nitrogen gas (no methane)** — the Rukwa story. Reservoir gas is dominantly N₂ with high He (3-10% mol), no commercial methane. Other examples: Doe Canyon (Colorado), Galkynysh adjacent fields (Turkmenistan, contested).
3. **Helium from mantle plume systems** — rift basins like East African Rift; deep basement degassing of accumulated radiogenic He.

The **Rukwa Rift** is Tanzania's contribution to the global He map. Continental rifting since the Neoproterozoic has thinned the crust, creating both heat flux and trap geometries; the Karoo-age sediments overlying Precambrian basement provide the trap; the basement itself is the source rock (high U + Th content of Tanzanian craton granites generates ⁴He over Ga timescales).

### 4.D Exploration & determination

- **Soil gas sampling** for He anomaly (handheld mass-spec sniffers)
- **Seismic reflection** for trap structure (2D regional + targeted 3D)
- **Geochemical sampling** of springs and seeps; isotope ratios (³He/⁴He, indicates mantle vs crustal source)
- **Drilling**: standard rotary, mud logging with **helium-calibrated mud-gas detectors** (a non-trivial calibration — gas chromatographs at most rigs are tuned for hydrocarbons)
- **DST/EWT (extended well test)**: vertical or horizontal completion, surface gas separation, He concentration measured continuously
- **Resource categorisation under PRMS** (Petroleum Resources Management System): Contingent Resources (1C/2C/3C) and eventually Reserves (1P/2P/3P) when development plan firm and FID near

### 4.E Mining methods

Helium is produced exactly like natural gas:
1. Drill production well (vertical or deviated)
2. Surface separator removes water, condensate (none at Rukwa), CO₂
3. Pipeline to processing facility (for Rukwa, would be a purpose-built modular plant near site)

### 4.F Beneficiation & processing

Three competing technology paths from raw gas (~5% He, ~85% N₂, balance) to pipeline-grade helium:

1. **Cryogenic distillation** — chill to -195°C, condense N₂, He stays gaseous; the LNG-plant standard. Highest purity, highest capex.
2. **Pressure Swing Adsorption (PSA)** — selective adsorption on activated carbon or zeolite at pressure swings. Lower capex, suitable for smaller plants. Likely path for Rukwa.
3. **Membrane separation** — polymer membranes differentially permeable to He vs N₂. Best as a pre-concentration step.

Final polishing to commercial purity grades (5N = 99.999%; 6N = 99.9999%; 7N = 99.99999%) typically uses cryogenic final stage + getter bed.

**Packaging**: gaseous helium in high-pressure tube trailers (~200 bar); or liquefied at -269°C (4.2 K) and shipped in ISO-tank dewars (vacuum-jacketed, ~40,000 L liquid).

## 5. Natural Graphite Battery-Anode Link

Graphite is treated in depth in **file 03 — Battery and EV Minerals**. This section flags the energy-mineral cross-reference only.

**Linkage:**
- Tanzania hosts world-class flake graphite projects: Nachu (Magnis Energy Technologies), Lindi Jumbo (Walkabout Resources), Epanko (EcoGraf), Mahenge (Black Rock Mining). Combined contained graphite resource on the order of hundreds of Mt.
- Battery-grade demand: ~95% of a lithium-ion anode is graphite. EV pull is the structural demand growth.
- **Spherical purified graphite (SPG)** is the anode-grade form, requiring micronisation, spheroidisation, and HF or alkali purification to 99.95%+ C.
- China currently dominates downstream processing (>95% of SPG capacity). Tanzanian flake exporters need to either ship concentrate to China or stand up domestic spheroidisation — the latter is the higher-value path Boji should encourage.

**Boji decision trigger for graphite-mention**: cross-reference to file 03 §3 for full treatment; do not double-handle here.

## 6. Geothermal-Related Rare Elements (Li, Sr, B from Geothermal Brines)

A small but strategically interesting cross-over between energy infrastructure and critical minerals. The Boji guidance is brief because the resource has not been characterised in Tanzania, but the principle is worth encoding.

### 6.A The concept

High-enthalpy geothermal brines from rift settings can contain economic concentrations of **lithium, strontium, boron, rubidium, caesium, and bromine** — recoverable as a co-product of power generation. The flagship is the **Salton Sea Geothermal Field (California)**: brine at 250-380°C, **lithium 202 ppm, rubidium 110 ppm, caesium 19.8 ppm, bromine 91 ppm** [Economic Geology — Salton Sea Li characterization](https://pubs.geoscienceworld.org/segweb/economicgeology/article/120/5/1071/660729/Characterization-of-Li-in-the-Salton-Sea); estimated **127,000 t LCE per year** recoverable; total in-place ~3,400 kilotons Li [LBL — Salton Sea Lithium](https://emp.lbl.gov/publications/characterizing-geothermal-lithium).

### 6.B East African Rift relevance

The East African Rift, including Tanzania's **Songwe geothermal prospect** in the south-west and several northern Rift Valley prospects (Lake Natron, Ngozi), shares many tectonic features with the Salton Sea: thinned crust, deep magmatic heat, sedimentary brine traps. **Geothermal reserves in East Africa's Rift Valley are estimated at ~15,000 MW potential** [ResearchGate — Tanzania geothermal map](https://www.researchgate.net/figure/Geological-map-of-Tanzania-showing-the-locations-of-the-geothermal-prospects-and-the_fig1_287210038). However, **Tanzanian geothermal brines have not been chemically characterised for Li, Sr, B, Rb, Cs** at any scale.

**Boji posture:** when any developer proposes a geothermal power plant in Tanzania (Songwe, Lake Natron, Ngozi), recommend **brine geochemistry sampling for Li, Sr, B, Rb, Cs** as part of the resource characterisation. The marginal cost is trivial; the optionality is substantial; if Tanzania holds a Salton-Sea-grade brine, it is a critical-mineral asset Tanzania does not yet know it has.

### 6.C DLE — Direct Lithium Extraction

If a Tanzanian brine is characterised at >100 ppm Li, the recovery technology is **Direct Lithium Extraction (DLE)** using sorbents (alumina, MnO₂, TiO₂), ion exchange, or solvent extraction. The technology is still pre-commercial outside of Salton Sea pilot work but maturing fast (EnergySource Minerals, Standard Lithium, Lake Resources).
