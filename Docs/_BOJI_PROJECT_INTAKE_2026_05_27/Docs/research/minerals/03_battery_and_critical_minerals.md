## 1 · Lithium (Li)

### A · Identity
- Atomic number 3, mass 6.94, density 0.534 g/cm³ (lightest metal), melting point 180.5 °C.
- Crustal abundance ~ 20 ppm; concentration in seawater 0.17 mg/L.
- Principal ore minerals:
  - **Spodumene LiAlSi₂O₆** — 8.0% Li₂O theoretical (max), monoclinic; the dominant hard-rock source.
  - **Petalite LiAlSi₄O₁₀** — 4.9% Li₂O; ceramic-glass grade direct.
  - **Lepidolite K(Li,Al)₃(Al,Si,Rb)₄O₁₀(F,OH)₂** — 3-4% Li₂O; high-F penalty; usually rejected.
  - **Eucryptite LiAlSiO₄**, **Amblygonite LiAl(PO₄)(F,OH)** — minor.
  - **Brines** (continental, oilfield, geothermal) — Li⁺ in solution at 100-2,000 ppm with Mg²⁺, Ca²⁺, K⁺, Na⁺, B, SO₄²⁻.
  - **Sedimentary** — hectorite-rich clays (Thacker Pass, NV), jadarite Na(Li,Mg)Si(BO₃)(OH) (Serbia, single-occurrence mineral).

### B · Geology & deposit types
- **LCT pegmatites** (Lithium-Caesium-Tantalum) — most prolific hard-rock source: Greenbushes (WA, world's largest), Pilgangoora, Wodgina, Mt Marion, Mt Cattlin, Manono (DRC), Bald Hill, Kathleen Valley, Sigma Mine (Brazil), Mibra (Brazil).
- **Continental brines** — closed-basin salars: Salar de Atacama (Chile, SQM + Albemarle), Hombre Muerto (Argentina, Livent), Olaroz (Allkem), Cauchari, Salar de Uyuni (Bolivia, world's largest reserve but high Mg:Li).
- **Geothermal brines** — Salton Sea (CA), Vulcan / Cornish Lithium (UK/Germany Rhine graben).
- **Sedimentary clays** — hectorite (smectite Mg-Li clay): Thacker Pass, Rhyolite Ridge.
- **Oilfield brines** — Smackover formation (Arkansas) and similar — DLE (Direct Lithium Extraction) candidate.

### C · Tanzania / East Africa relevance
- Tanzania has **minor** Li potential: some LCT pegmatites in the south-east (Mtwara, Lindi) but no producing mine.
- **DRC's Manono** (Roche Dure, AVZ Minerals) is the regional flagship — adjacent to Tanzania-DRC border trade route via Lake Tanganyika.
- **Zimbabwe's Bikita / Arcadia / Sandawana** are the closest African producers.
- Boji should track regional Li projects as TZ owners may eventually serve as logistics or services partners.

### D · Exploration & determination
- **Hard-rock pegmatites**:
  - Visual outcrop (spodumene exhibits diagnostic cleavage and "shiny mica-like" appearance; petalite is more glassy).
  - Hand-held **LIBS (laser-induced breakdown spectroscopy)** for field Li detection.
  - **pXRF** for K/Rb/Cs (pegmatite indicator), Ta, Sn pathfinders.
  - Channel sampling of pegmatite dykes; bulk samples for DMS (heavy-media) test work.
  - Diamond core drilling.
- **Brines**:
  - **Salar drilling** for porosity and salar-specific yield (Sy).
  - **Pump tests** to estimate flow rates.
  - Geophysical mapping (gravity, AMT) to define brine reservoir boundaries.
- **Lab analysis**:
  - Li by ICP-MS or AAS (sample digested with mixed acids; spodumene needs LiBO₂ fusion for total).
  - Mg:Li ratio is the single most important brine quality metric (< 6 is "easy", > 30 is "hard"; Atacama is 6, Uyuni is 30).
  - B, SO₄²⁻, K⁺, Ca²⁺ also impact processing.

### E · Mining methods
- **Hard-rock pegmatite**: open-pit quarry, drill-and-blast; some narrow-vein underground (Bald Hill, Mibra).
- **Brines**: pumped to surface from wellfield → solar evaporation ponds; 12-24 months pond residence → concentrated Li-rich brine.
- **DLE (Direct Lithium Extraction)** — emerging: adsorption (e.g., Energy Source Minerals), IX, membrane (e.g., Lilac Solutions), solvent extraction (e.g., Eramet) — bypass evaporation ponds; weeks not months; lower water footprint.
- **Sedimentary clay**: open-pit + acid leach.

### F · Beneficiation & processing

**Spodumene hard-rock route:**
1. Crush + grind to p80 ~ 150 µm.
2. **DMS (Heavy-Media Separation)** at SG ~ 2.7-2.8 (FeSi medium): coarse spodumene (SG 3.1-3.2) sinks → pre-concentrate.
3. **Mica de-sliming** and reverse mica flotation with cationic amine collectors at pH 3 → mica rejected.
4. **Spodumene flotation** with **fatty-acid collector (oleic acid)** + Na-oleate at pH 9-10 with NaOH/Na₂CO₃; hydroxamates as alternative.
5. **Magnetic separation** removes Fe-bearing minerals.
6. Final product: **SC6 (Spodumene Concentrate, 6.0% Li₂O)** at ~ 80-85% concentrate-recovery; SC6.5+ for premium.

**Spodumene → battery-grade Li₂CO₃ / LiOH conversion:**
1. **Decrepitation** (α → β phase change) by calcining at 1050 °C for 1 hour — β-spodumene is acid-leachable.
2. **Sulphate roast** with H₂SO₄ at 250 °C — Li₂SO₄ formed.
3. **Water leach** → Li₂SO₄ solution.
4. **Purification**:
   - Lime soda for Ca/Mg removal,
   - IX or SX for B, K, Na, F polishing,
5. **Precipitation**:
   - **Li₂CO₃** with Na₂CO₃ at 90 °C → battery-grade Li₂CO₃ (99.5% min, < 50 ppm Na, Ca, Mg, K).
   - OR **LiOH·H₂O** via Li₂SO₄ + Ca(OH)₂ → LiOH solution → evaporative crystallisation → LiOH·H₂O (56.5% LiOH).

**Brine route:**
1. Pump well brine to surface.
2. Solar evaporation through 4-8 ponds (each removing NaCl, KCl, Na₂SO₄ at different solubility points).
3. Boron removal by SX or IX.
4. Mg removal by lime + soda (precipitates Mg(OH)₂, CaCO₃).
5. Final Li₂CO₃ precipitation OR LiCl → causticisation to LiOH.

**Clay (Thacker Pass type)**:
- Sulphuric-acid leach of hectorite clay → solution → Na/K/Mg removal → Li₂CO₃.
- Capex-heavy due to acid consumption.

### G · Final battery-grade product specifications
- **Li₂CO₃ technical grade**: 99.0% min, ≤ 200 ppm Na.
- **Li₂CO₃ battery grade**: 99.5% min, ≤ 50 ppm Na, ≤ 30 ppm Mg, ≤ 30 ppm Ca, ≤ 80 ppm K.
- **LiOH·H₂O battery grade**: 56.5% LiOH equivalent, ≤ 50 ppm Na, ≤ 10 ppm Fe.
- **SC6 spodumene concentrate**: 6.0% Li₂O, ≤ 0.6% Fe₂O₃, particle size +0.2 −1.0 mm.
- LiOH is preferred for high-nickel NCM811 / NCA cathodes (lower decomposition temperature, better handling).

### H · Movement, packaging, hazard
- **SC6**: bulk vessel; UN 3077 if classified as marine pollutant.
- **Li₂CO₃**: super-sack or drum; not DG; not particularly hazardous.
- **LiOH·H₂O**: **UN 2680, Class 8 Packing Group II** (alkaline corrosive); drum + secondary containment; absorbs CO₂ and moisture so airtight packaging essential.
- **LiCl, LiBr**: hazardous + hygroscopic.

### I · Pricing & markets
- **Fastmarkets**, **S&P Global Platts**, **Argus**, **Asian Metal**, **Benchmark Mineral Intelligence** — daily assessments:
  - Li₂CO₃ battery-grade CIF China (USD/kg)
  - LiOH·H₂O battery-grade CIF China
  - SC6 spodumene concentrate CIF China
- Massive 2022-2023 spike (USD 80+/kg LCE) then 2024-2025 correction (USD 9-15/kg LCE); 2026 stabilisation.
- **LCE (Lithium Carbonate Equivalent)**: industry-wide unit; Li metal × 5.323, LiOH × 0.880, Li₂CO₃ × 1.000, SC6 × 0.1564 (assuming 6% Li₂O).
- Long-term off-takes dominate: Tesla, GM, Ford, CATL, LG Energy Solution, Samsung SDI lock multi-year volumes.

### J · EHS
- **Water consumption** controversy at Atacama / Salar — community-conflict risk.
- **Lithium dust** mild irritation; not particularly toxic at exposure levels.
- **LiOH burns** — alkaline (corrosive); first-aid water flushing.
- **HF** is used in some hydromet alternatives — controlled.
- **Tailings**: acid sulphate / fluoride / boron residue; design for long-term stability.

### K · Decision triggers for Boji
- **Li hard-rock prospect in TZ pegmatite belt**: require DMS recovery test on bulk sample before any flotation flowsheet design; advise channel sampling + diamond core.
- **SC6 vs converted Li₂CO₃ / LiOH economics**: TZ has no Li-conversion plant; advise SC6 export to Chinese converters (Ganfeng, Tianqi, Yahua) unless capex case made for in-country plant.
- **Brine project**: extremely rare in TZ; if encountered, model 12-24 month pond timeline + Mg:Li ratio.
- **Long-term off-take contract** preferred over spot for cash-flow stability.
- **DLE technology** is risky — pilot scale; do not over-promise to investors.

## 2 · Cobalt (Co)

### A · Identity
- Atomic number 27, mass 58.93, density 8.90 g/cm³, melting point 1495 °C.
- Crustal abundance ~ 25 ppm; concentration in seawater 0.2 ng/L.
- Principal ore minerals:
  - **Cobaltite CoAsS** — primary; 35.5% Co; arsenide form.
  - **Heterogenite CoO·OH** — supergene, weathered Cu-Co (DRC dominant).
  - **Erythrite Co₃(AsO₄)₂·8H₂O** — pink "cobalt bloom".
  - **Linnaeite Co₃S₄**, **carrollite Cu(Co,Ni)₂S₄** — Cu-Co connections.
  - **Asbolite** — Mn-Co laterite.
  - Most Co (~ 65% of world supply) is **by-product of Cu** (DRC Copperbelt) and **by-product of Ni** (Indonesia HPAL, Sudbury, Norilsk).

### B · Geology
- **Central African Copperbelt (DRC + Zambia)** — supergene Cu-Co; ~ 70% of world Co supply.
- **Indonesian / Philippine / New Caledonian laterite** — HPAL by-product.
- **Sudbury / Voisey's Bay / Norilsk sulphide** — minor primary.
- **Cobalt-rich crusts** — Pacific Ocean seamounts; "deep-sea mining" controversy.

### C · Tanzania / East Africa relevance
- **Kabanga Ni-Cu-Co** (NW TZ) — significant Co credit alongside Ni.
- **Ntaka Hill** (Lindi/Mtwara) — Ni-Co-Cu.
- **Kapalagulu** layered intrusion.
- DRC border proximity makes TZ a potential **transit corridor** for DRC Co — politically sensitive, OECD-due-diligence relevant.

### D · Exploration
- Geophysics: EM for sulphide; gravity for massive sulphide.
- Pathfinders: As, S, Cu, Ni co-occurrence.
- Lab: ICP-MS for trace Co; **electrochemical methods** for sulphide speciation.

### E · Mining
- DRC: **artisanal "creuseurs"** hand-dig pits for Cu-Co; major human-rights flag.
- Industrial: large open-pit / underground (Tenke Fungurume, Mutanda, KCC, Kamoa-Kakula).
- Indonesian laterite: strip mining for HPAL feed.

### F · Beneficiation & processing
- **In Cu flowsheet**: Cu cathode → tailings carry Co → SX cascade with **Cyanex 272** to split Co/Ni (Co more extractable at lower pH); → CoSO₄ crystallisation.
- **Heterogenite (oxide Co)**: H₂SO₄ + Na₂S₂O₅ (reducing) leach → Co into solution → SX → cathode Co metal (electrowin) OR CoSO₄·7H₂O / Co₃O₄.
- **From Ni-laterite HPAL**: MHP (Mixed Hydroxide Precipitate) → re-leach → SX → battery-grade CoSO₄.

### G · Refining → battery-grade
- **CoSO₄·7H₂O battery-grade**: ≥ 20.5% Co, ≤ 50 ppm impurity total (Ni, Mn, Fe, Cu, Zn, Pb).
- **Co₃O₄** (cobalt tetraoxide): for LCO cathode (laptop battery legacy).
- **Co metal** (LME-deliverable cathode or briquette, ≥ 99.8% Co): aerospace super-alloy, hard-alloy.
- DRC artisanal Co → traders in Lubumbashi → Chinese refiners (Huayou Cobalt, GEM, Hanrui Cobalt) dominate.

### H · Movement, hazard
- **Cobalt hydroxide** (intermediate): bulk super-sack, ≤ 50% Co content typical, 60-65% payable.
- **CoSO₄·7H₂O**: super-sack; **UN 3077 PG III** (marine pollutant); inhalation/skin sensitisation hazard.
- **Co metal**: not DG (in bulk form); dust inhalation hazardous.
- DRC trader chains require **OECD Due Diligence Guidance + RMI (Responsible Minerals Initiative) + RMAP audit** for downstream sale to EV makers.

### I · Pricing
- **LME Cobalt metal** cash; ranges USD 25-60/kg historically.
- **CoSO₄·7H₂O CIF China** (Fastmarkets, Asian Metal).
- **Cobalt hydroxide CIF China** at % payable Co content (60-65% typical payable).
- Cobalt is volatile because:
  - 70% of supply concentrated in DRC,
  - 80% of refining concentrated in China,
  - Demand swings with EV chemistry shifts (LFP gaining vs NMC).

### J · EHS
- **DRC artisanal child labour** — Amnesty International, Pact, RMI, EVA, OECD scrutiny; Ford / Tesla / Apple supply-chain audit pressure.
- **Heterogenite dust** — inhalation lung hazard.
- **Arsenic-co-occurrence** (cobaltite is arsenosulphide) — worker exposure.
- **Co metal IARC Group 2B carcinogen** (possibly).

### K · Decision triggers for Boji
- **DRC-context trader chain**: require OECD due diligence + RMAP audit + chain-of-custody documentation before any handling recommendation.
- **TZ Kabanga / Ntaka Hill**: model Co as ~ 1/3 of project NPV (because Co payable can dominate Ni payable on by-product basis).
- **TZ-DRC transit**: Boji should flag the regulatory and reputational risk of routing DRC Co through Tanzanian ports — buyers will demand traceability.
- **LFP vs NMC cathode demand**: Boji should track the chemistry mix in the destination market; LFP-rising means Co demand soft.

## 3 · Graphite (C) — Tanzania's flagship critical mineral

### A · Identity
- Pure carbon C; hexagonal crystal; soft (Mohs 1-2); high thermal/electrical conductivity along basal plane; high thermal stability.
- Three forms by morphology:
  - **Flake graphite** — natural crystalline platelets, in mica-schist host; most economically valuable.
  - **Vein graphite** (Sri Lanka type) — hydrothermal; highest purity but tiny world supply.
  - **Amorphous graphite** — micro-crystalline; lower-value bulk applications (refractory, foundry).
- **Synthetic graphite** — from petroleum coke at 3000 °C; competes with natural for battery anode but more energy-intensive (4× CO₂ footprint of natural).

### B · Geology
- Metamorphic carbon-bearing sediments — pelitic schist + carbonate metamorphosed to upper-amphibolite or granulite facies.
- Major basins: **Tanzania Mozambique Belt** (Lindi/Mahenge), Madagascar, Mozambique, Brazil (Salobo), China (Heilongjiang, Shandong).
- Vein graphite: Sri Lanka (Bogala mine, near-exclusive).

### C · Tanzania — the world's emerging flake-graphite hub
**This is the most important Tanzanian critical-minerals story.** TZ hosts arguably the largest concentration of high-quality flake-graphite projects globally, primarily in the Lindi region:

- **Lindi Jumbo** (Walkabout Resources, ASX:WKT) — 41.6 Mt @ 9.8% TGC, first production 2024; flake size with 39% in jumbo +80 mesh fraction.
- **Mahenge Graphite** (Black Rock Mining, ASX:BKT) — 212 Mt @ 7.8% TGC (one of the world's largest); Module 1 + 4 staged build-out.
- **Bunyu / Nachu** (Volt Resources, ASX:VRC; also Magnis Energy / Imerys legacy) — 461 Mt @ 4.9% TGC; 100% spheronisation capacity proposal.
- **Epanko** (Tirupati Graphite / AMG) — Mahenge area; near-production.
- **Chilalo** (Evolution Energy Minerals) — Lindi region.

**Flake size distribution** is the value driver:
| Mesh | Microns | Premium |
|---|---|---|
| +50 mesh (jumbo) | >300 µm | Highest — expandable graphite, gaskets, premium battery |
| +80 mesh (large) | 180-300 µm | Premium |
| +100 mesh | 150-180 µm | Standard battery anode |
| +200 mesh (medium) | 75-150 µm | Refractory, foundry, lubricant |
| -200 mesh (fine) | <75 µm | Industrial, cheapest |

### D · Exploration & determination
- **Geophysics**: SP (self-potential, graphite is electrically conductive); EM (graphite is highly conductive); gravity.
- **Lab**:
  - **TGC (Total Graphitic Carbon)** by LECO combustion — gold-standard grade.
  - **Carbon by acidulation** — leach with HCl/HF to remove carbonate + silicate, then weigh residue.
  - **Particle-size distribution** by wet-screening at 50/80/100/150/200 mesh.
  - **Crystallinity** by XRD (interlayer d-spacing 3.354 Å for perfect; broader peaks = poorer).
  - **Resistivity** test.

### E · Mining
- Open-pit; soft schist host allows simple excavator + truck.
- Low strip ratios (0.5-2) for tropical-weathered ore.
- Low energy intensity vs hard-rock.

### F · Beneficiation
- **Critical flowsheet principle: preserve flake size at all costs.**
- **Staged crushing/grinding** with **rod mill** (gentler than ball mill) to liberate flakes without breaking them; multiple cleaner stages with re-grind only on coarse fractions; classification by hydrocyclone.
- **Flotation reagents**:
  - Collector: **diesel / kerosene / fuel oil** (graphite is naturally hydrophobic — collector is just oil for emulsification).
  - Frother: **MIBC, polyglycol**.
  - Depressant for mica/silicate: **dextrin, CMC, sodium silicate**.
- Rougher → cleaner × 4-6 stages → final concentrate **94-96% TGC**.
- **Sizing**: dry screening into mesh fractions; coarse fractions priced premium.
- **Acid leach upgrade**: HCl + HF for further upgrade to 99.5%+ TGC (intermediate-purity for some markets).
- **Thermal purification**: 2,800-3,000 °C for 99.95%+ TGC (battery-anode "uncoated SPG" feed).
- **Spheronisation**: ball mill spherical-fines crusher → "potato-shaped" particles with smooth surface; yield only 30-50% (material loss is huge).
- **Coating**: CVD pitch coating to make finished battery-anode "coated SPG" (Spherical Purified Graphite).

### G · Final-product specifications
| Grade | Purity | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Refractory / foundry | 88-95% TGC | crucibles, foundry coatings |
| Expandable graphite | 94-97% TGC, +80 mesh | flame retardant, gaskets |
| Battery feed (uncoated) | 99.95% TGC, spheronised | LIB anode feed |
| Battery anode (coated SPG) | 99.95%+ TGC, coated, spheronised | finished LIB anode |

### H · Movement / hazard
- Concentrate: super-sack, FCL; not DG; dust nuisance.
- Battery-anode finished: argon-flushed for high-grade coated SPG.

### I · Pricing
- **Fastmarkets** flake graphite basis (-100 mesh, -200 mesh, +80 mesh) — daily indices.
- Battery-anode finished SPG: USD 4,000-7,000/t.
- Flake graphite 94-97% TGC: USD 600-1,500/t depending on flake size.
- Synthetic anode: USD 4,500-8,000/t (more expensive than natural).
- China dominates ~ 65% world flake supply; **2023 export-control regulations** affected non-Chinese OEMs — major risk and opportunity for TZ projects.

### J · EHS
- Dust (silica from gangue + carbon dust); **dust-explosion risk** in fine-grinding/spheronisation.
- HF use in acid-purification — fluoride waste handling.
- Spheronisation generates significant ultrafine waste — landfill or fines-recovery market needed.

### K · Decision triggers for Boji — Tanzania flake-graphite
- **Flake-size first**: bulk-sample wet-screen test BEFORE any flowsheet; the +80/+50 jumbo fraction is the value driver.
- **Off-take requirement**: TZ flake-graphite owners should secure off-take with European, Japanese, or Korean OEMs (Northvolt, LGES, Samsung SDI, CATL European fab) before final investment decision — China export-control creates a structural pricing opportunity for non-Chinese supply.
- **Spheronisation in-country?**: capex case for an in-country spheronisation/coating plant in Lindi or Mtwara is being explored; currently most flake exports to China for spheronisation; **Tanzanian critical-minerals policy may incentivise local value addition (Mining Act 2024 / GN 260/2025)**.
- **Thorium-bearing host**: some Lindi graphite host carries minor Th — NORM disposal regulatory.
- **Synthetic-graphite displacement** risk: track CATL / BYD chemistry adoption; LFP cells use less anode than NMC.

## 4 · Battery-grade Nickel sulphate (NiSO₄·6H₂O)

> See file `02_base_metals.md` § 4 for the full Ni metallurgy context. This section focuses on battery-grade specifications and TZ relevance.

### Specifications
- **NiSO₄·6H₂O battery grade**: ≥ 22.3% Ni, ≤ 50 ppm Co, ≤ 20 ppm Cu, ≤ 20 ppm Fe, ≤ 10 ppm Mn, ≤ 100 ppm Na+K.
- Crystalline emerald-green; soluble in water; commonly shipped as 30% solution or solid super-sack.

### Production routes
- **From Class I nickel metal**: dissolve in H₂SO₄ → solution → crystallise. Premium, low-impurity.
- **From sulphide MHP/MSP**: SX (Cyanex 272 for Co/Ni split) → NiSO₄ crystallisation.
- **From laterite HPAL MHP**: same; Indonesian Class II to Class I via refining (e.g., PT Vale, IXM, Nornickel).

### TZ relevance
- **Kabanga Nickel hydromet refining route** is proposed to produce battery-grade NiSO₄ **in-country** (Lifezone Metals novel approach), bypassing matte smelter → avoiding Indonesian / Chinese refining intermediation; if successful, a structurally new Class I source for European/American battery supply chains.

### Pricing & decision triggers
- Premium over LME Ni metal × Ni content × premium factor (1.10-1.30).
- Boji decision trigger: **TZ owner with Ni sulphide concentrate**: model both Class II (Indonesian / Chinese smelting) and Class I (Kabanga-style hydromet, premium-priced) routes; the latter may be a 2-3× revenue uplift if executed.

## 5 · Battery-grade Manganese sulphate (MnSO₄·H₂O)

> See file `02_base_metals.md` § 8 for full Mn context.

### Specifications
- **MnSO₄·H₂O battery grade**: ≥ 32.5% Mn, ≤ 5 ppm Fe, ≤ 5 ppm Cu, ≤ 1 ppm Pb, ≤ 1 ppm Cd, ≤ 1 ppm As.
- High-purity manganese metal (HPMnSO₄, "battery-grade") is dramatically purer than the ferro-Mn route used for stainless steel.

### Production
- From Mn ore: acid leach → multi-stage purification (Fe removal, ion-exchange for Co/Ni, sulphide-precipitation for Cu/Pb/Cd) → crystallise.
- China currently dominates production but TZ Manganese deposits remain under-explored — possible future opportunity.

### Decision triggers
- Mn-rich NMC cathodes (NMC811, NMC9.5.5, NMC532) drive demand; LFP cells use less Mn.
- Boji should track NMC vs LFP market share for demand forecasting.

## 6 · Vanadium (V) — steel additive + VRFB redox-flow batteries

### A · Identity
- Atomic number 23, mass 50.94, density 6.0 g/cm³, melting point 1910 °C.
- Crustal abundance ~ 120 ppm.
- Principal ores:
  - **Magnetite-vanadium-titanium (Ti-V magnetite)** — Bushveld titaniferous magnetite seam, **Liganga TZ** ~ 0.8% V₂O₅ in the magnetite.
  - **Carnotite K₂(UO₂)₂(VO₄)₂·3H₂O** — sandstone-hosted U-V (Colorado Plateau).
  - **Patronite VS₄** — Peruvian Mina Ragra.
  - **Vanadinite Pb₅(VO₄)₃Cl** — lead-vanadate.
  - **Shale-hosted** — Bakken-style V-rich petroleum shales; co-extraction with oil shale.

### B · Tanzania relevance
- **Liganga Fe-V-Ti project** (Mchuchuma-Liganga complex, Mbeya region) — 126 Mt @ 51% Fe + 0.8% V₂O₅ + 13% TiO₂; major project tied with Mchuchuma coal for integrated steelmaking.

### C-F · Mining & processing
- Magnetite-Ti-V ore: open-pit + magnetic separation + smelting via direct-reduction or BF route → pig iron + V slag (~ 15% V₂O₅).
- **Salt roast** of vanadium slag with Na₂CO₃/NaCl at 800 °C → water-leach Na-vanadate → acid-precipitate V₂O₅ (98%+).
- **V₂O₅ flake** is the trade unit.
- **FeV80** (ferrovanadium 80%) for steel additive.
- **VRFB electrolyte**: 1.6 M VO²⁺/VO₂⁺ in 4 M H₂SO₄; redox potentials -0.26 V (V²⁺/V³⁺) and +1.00 V (VO²⁺/VO₂⁺).

### G · Final products & pricing
- **V₂O₅ flake**: USD 6-12/lb.
- **FeV80**: USD 25-50/kg V.
- **VRFB electrolyte**: USD 50-150/kg V.
- Fastmarkets, Argus indices.

### J · EHS
- **V₂O₅ inhalation toxic** (lung scarring, IARC Group 2B); UN 2862 PG III.
- Fugitive dust controls mandatory.

### K · Decision triggers for Boji
- **Liganga-class TZ owner**: model V₂O₅ credit separately from Fe; track steel vs VRFB demand.
- VRFB market is small but growing (long-duration battery storage); a "differentiated" route to market for V producers.

## 7 · Rare-earth elements (REE)

### A · Identity — the 17 elements
- **Light REE (LREE)**: La, Ce, Pr, Nd, Pm (radioactive, not commercial), Sm.
- **Heavy REE (HREE)** + Y, Sc: Eu, Gd, Tb, Dy, Ho, Er, Tm, Yb, Lu, Y, (Sc).
- Key economic drivers:
  - **Nd, Pr** — NdFeB permanent magnets (EV motors, wind turbines, hard drives, robotics).
  - **Dy, Tb** — temperature-stable NdFeB magnets (high-temperature operation).
  - **Eu, Y, Tb** — phosphors (legacy).
  - **La, Ce** — catalysts, autocat.
  - **Sm** — SmCo magnets.
  - **Ho, Er, Tm, Yb, Lu** — niche; small markets.

### B · Principal ore minerals
- **Bastnäsite (Ce,La,Nd,Y)CO₃F** — LREE-rich; Mountain Pass, Bayan Obo.
- **Monazite (Ce,La,Nd,Th)PO₄** — LREE + Th; mineral sands, beach placers.
- **Xenotime YPO₄** — HREE-rich.
- **Loparite** — Russia.
- **Eudialyte** — agpaitic syenite.
- **Ionic-clay deposits** (ion-adsorbed REE in weathered granite) — south China, Myanmar; HREE-rich; ammonium-sulphate in-situ leach.

### C · Geology — deposit types
- **Carbonatite-hosted** (Mountain Pass CA, Bayan Obo Mongolia, Mt Weld Australia, Songwe Hill Malawi, **Ngualla Tanzania**, Lofdal Namibia): LREE-dominant; carbonatite + alkali igneous intrusions.
- **Pegmatite / alkali granite**: HREE; Strange Lake, Norra Kärr.
- **Ionic-adsorption clay** (SE Asia, Myanmar): HREE-rich, environmentally controversial.
- **Mineral sand / beach placer** (Pwani TZ coast, Florida, Malaysia, India): monazite-bearing sand; Th-NORM co-product.

### D · Tanzania — Ngualla and beyond
- **Ngualla** (Peak Resources, ASX:PEK) — Mbeya region; one of the largest undeveloped REE deposits globally; ~ 214 Mt @ 2.15% REO including 0.4% NdPr-oxide. Project includes Teesside (UK) downstream refining proposal — bypassing Chinese refining intermediation, an analogue to Lynas Mt Weld → Malaysia.
- **Wigu Hill** (Montero Mining, prior) — carbonatite LREE.
- **Songwe Hill** (Mkango Resources, Malawi) — regional analogue.
- **Pwani-coast beach sand** — minor REE in monazite fraction (file 08).

### E · Exploration & determination
- **Spectral remote sensing** — carbonatite Cr/Mn ratios; rare earth pathfinders.
- **Gamma-spectrometry** (Th, U channels) — monazite tracer.
- **Geochemistry**: Nb, Ta, P, F, Sr, Ba pathfinders.
- **Drilling**: HQ/NQ core.
- **Lab analysis**: full lanthanide-suite ICP-MS (15-element REE+Y+Sc); **alkali-fusion + acid digest** for total REE; **ash leach** for ionic-clay deposits.

### F · Beneficiation
- **Bastnäsite/monazite flotation**: hydroxamate (e.g., octyl hydroxamic acid) or fatty-acid collector at pH 9; depress silicate gangue with sodium silicate / lignosulphonate.
- **Concentrate to 30-60% REO** typically.

### G · Cracking → leach → solvent extraction (the famous part)
- **Bastnäsite cracking**: roast at 500-700 °C → CO₂ off-gas → leach in HCl → REE chloride solution.
- **Monazite cracking**: hot conc. H₂SO₄ at 200-230 °C OR NaOH at 145-220 °C → digestion → REE/Th separation; Th hydroxide precipitates separately (NORM waste).
- **Solvent extraction cascade**:
  - Reagent: **DEHPA (di-2-ethylhexyl phosphoric acid)**, **PC88A**, **Cyanex 572**, **Versatic 10**, **Aliquat 336**.
  - Adjacent REE have separation factors of just 1.5-2.0 (the "rare-earth separation factor" problem) → 12-18 stages needed.
  - Cascade: Ce/La split → Nd/Pr split → SEG (Sm-Eu-Gd) → Dy/Tb split → HREE polishing.
- **Precipitation**: REE oxide as REE₂O₃ at 95%+; for battery / magnet grade > 99.5%; for laser/phosphor > 99.99% / 99.999%.
- **Reduction to metal**: calciothermic (Ca metal + REE oxide), molten-salt electrolysis, or carbothermic.
- **NdFeB alloy**: NdPr metal + Fe + B → strip-cast → milled to powder → pressed in magnetic field → sintered.

### H · Movement / hazard
- REE oxides / chlorides: super-sack; not DG; some are mildly toxic.
- **Monazite NORM** — Th-bearing; IAEA / NEMC disposal regulatory pathway mandatory.
- **NdFeB magnets**: not DG but strong field; transport with shielding.

### I · Pricing
- **Argus**, **Asian Metal**, **Fastmarkets**:
  - Nd₂O₃ FOB China — USD 50-100/kg (high volatility).
  - Pr₂O₃ — similar.
  - Dy₂O₃ — USD 250-500/kg.
  - Tb₂O₃ — USD 1,000-2,500/kg.
  - La₂O₃, CeO₂ — over-supplied, ~ USD 2-5/kg (cheap by-product).
- **China export controls 2023-2024** on Nd/Pr/Dy/Tb permanent-magnet-related materials created supply-security risk and pricing premium for non-Chinese supply.

### J · EHS
- **Ionic-clay leaching environmental disaster** — China clean-up costs USD 5-10bn; do NOT recommend without major capex.
- **Thorium NORM** disposal regulated; specialised storage required.
- **Acid** waste from cracking — H₂SO₄ / HCl.
- **Solvent inventory** — flammable, exposure-controlled.

### K · Decision triggers for Boji — Ngualla & beyond
- **TZ owner with REE prospect**: do NOT advise development without **long-term off-take agreement** — China supply-glut + REE price volatility risk.
- **HREE-rich (Dy, Tb) deposits**: prioritise; LREE-only (La, Ce) deposits face over-supply.
- **Carbonatite vs ionic clay**: carbonatite has higher capex but lower long-tail environmental cost; ionic clay is faster cash but reputation risk.
- **Refining route choice**:
  - Sell concentrate to Lynas / Solvay / China — straightforward but margin-thin.
  - In-country refining (Peak Resources Ngualla → Teesside model) — high capex but commands premium for Western supply-chain.
- **NORM compliance from Day 1**.

## 8 · Tungsten (W) — critical-minerals adjacency

### A · Identity
- Atomic number 74, mass 183.84, density 19.3 g/cm³, melting point **3422 °C** (highest of all metals).
- **Wolframite (Fe,Mn)WO₄** — primary ore; magnetic; tabular crystals.
- **Scheelite CaWO₄** — fluoresces blue-white under UV; gravity-amenable.

### B · Geology
- Sn-W granite belts: China (Jiangxi, 80% world supply), Vietnam (Núi Pháo), Portugal, Spain, Bolivia, Korea.
- **3T (Tin-Tungsten-Tantalum)** ASM context in DRC/Rwanda — ITSCI traceability.

### C · TZ relevance
- Minor wolframite in NW pegmatite belts; not commercially significant.

### D-F · Beneficiation
- **Gravity** (shaking tables, spirals, Wilfley) — scheelite SG 5.9-6.1 and wolframite SG 7.1-7.5 are gravity-perfect.
- **Magnetic separation** to remove wolframite from scheelite (wolframite is paramagnetic).
- **UV-fluorescence sorting** for scheelite.
- **Flotation** with hydroxamate or fatty acid as final cleaner.

### G · Refining → APT → metal / carbide
- **Soda-ash leach** of concentrate → sodium tungstate Na₂WO₄ → ion-exchange or solvent extraction.
- **Ammonium paratungstate (APT)** (NH₄)₁₀[H₂W₁₂O₄₂]·4H₂O — the trade unit; crystallised from purified solution.
- APT → **tungsten trioxide WO₃** (calcine) → **W metal powder** (H₂ reduction at 700-900 °C).
- W powder + carbon → **tungsten carbide WC** (carburise at 1500 °C); + cobalt binder → cemented carbide for cutting tools.
- **Ferro-tungsten (FeW80)** for steel additive.

### H-I · Movement / pricing
- APT super-sack; not DG.
- Pricing: **Fastmarkets APT** 88.5% WO₃ Europe, Asian Metal APT FOB China; ranges USD 200-400/MTU WO₃ (per metric-tonne-unit; 1 MTU = 10 kg of WO₃).
- Ferro-tungsten EU: USD 30-50/kg W content.

### J-K · EHS / decision triggers
- ITSCI tagging for DRC/Rwanda 3T context.
- Heavy gravity-amenable mineral — favour gravity over flotation.
- Cemented-carbide tool demand drives downstream pull.
- China dominance creates supply-chain-security upside for non-Chinese producers.
