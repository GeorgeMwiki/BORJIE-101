## 0. Common house style (read first)

- **Hardness** = Mohs. **RI** = refractive index. **SG** = specific gravity. **DR** = double refraction (birefringence).
- **Chromophore** = trace element that imparts colour: Cr³⁺ (red/green), V³⁺ (green/violet), Fe (yellow/blue/green), Ti⁴⁺+Fe²⁺ pair (blue, intervalence charge transfer), Mn²⁺/Mn³⁺ (pink/red), Cu²⁺ (paraíba electric blue), C-defects / N-defects (diamond yellow/pink), radiation centres (smoky/blue).
- **Origin determination** = LA-ICP-MS trace-element fingerprint + FTIR + UV-Vis-NIR + microscopy. Top labs: GIA, AGL, SSEF, Gübelin, Lotus, GRS.
- **Per-carat price scales non-linearly with size**: a "doubling" in carat weight for top quality can mean 3–5× per-carat — this is the **size premium**. Boji must price gems by **(species, colour grade, clarity grade, weight bucket, treatment status, origin)** — not by weight alone.
- **Treatment disclosure** is mandatory under CIBJO and the LMHC (Laboratory Manual Harmonisation Committee, [LMHC info sheets](https://www.lmhc-gemmology.org/)). Failure = fraud; Boji's Treatment Agent must encode every step in the chain of custody.
- **Tanzanian royalty (gemstones) = 6%** on gross value + **1% inspection fee** at the Mining Commission ([TUMEMADINI royalties](https://www.tumemadini.go.tz/pages/mineral-royalties-and-inspection-fees-rates/), [Lexology overview](https://www.lexology.com/library/detail.aspx?g=398c68a3-74b3-441f-8888-169a3ffee73a)).
- **Tanzania mineral & gem auctions** are now electronic via the **Tanzania Mercantile Exchange (TMX)** and the Mining Commission — 3rd electronic gemstone auction was held in Arusha 27 Nov 2025, USD 88,772 transacted ([BizLens 2025-11-27](https://thebizlens.co.tz/2025/11/27/gemstone-buyers-embrace-tanzanias-new-online-auction-system/), [Daily News auction plan](https://dailynews.co.tz/tanzania-unveils-plans-to-launch-more-gem-and-mineral-auctions/)). Boji's Sales Agent must support TMX as the default sales path for >USD 5k parcels.

## 1. Diamond (C — Carbon)

### A. Identity

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Formula | C (pure carbon) |
| Crystal system | Cubic (Fd3̄m) |
| Hardness | 10 (defines the top of Mohs) |
| RI | 2.417 (single refraction — isotropic) |
| SG | 3.52 |
| Dispersion | 0.044 (the "fire" — highest of common gems) |
| Lustre | Adamantine |
| Chromophores | N (yellow Cape), B (blue Type IIb), plastic deformation (pink/red), irradiation defects (green/blue) |
| Type classification | Type Ia (N aggregates, ~98% gem), Ib (isolated N — fancy yellow), IIa (N-poor, exceptional whites, "Golconda"), IIb (B-bearing — Hope blue) |

### B. Geology of formation & deposit types

Diamond crystallises in the **sub-cratonic lithospheric mantle** at 140–200 km depth, 900–1300 °C, then erupts to surface via **kimberlite** (typical) or **lamproite** (rare — Argyle in Australia) magma. Diamond carbon isotopes (δ¹³C) cluster around −5‰ for **peridotitic** parageneses (pyrope, chrome diopside, olivine inclusions) and span −5 to −25‰ for **eclogitic** parageneses (omphacite, almandine-pyrope inclusions). Eclogitic diamonds dominate at Mwadui ([Mindat Williamson mineralogy](https://www.mindat.org/loc-16730.html)).

Deposit types:
- **Primary kimberlite pipe** (Mwadui, Cullinan, Orapa, Jwaneng, Diavik): carrot-shaped diatreme + root zone, often diamondiferous in upper crater facies only.
- **Lamproite pipe** (Argyle WA): pink-rich, geologically distinct.
- **Alluvial trains** (Shinyanga, Vaal River, Marange, Lunda Norte): drainage-concentrated; lower grade, higher quality (weak crystals destroyed in transport).
- **Marine placer** (Namaqualand, Namibia coast): beach + offshore.

### C. Tanzania / East Africa — Mwadui (Williamson)

- **Location:** Shinyanga Region, 23 km NE of Shinyanga town. Pipe discovered March 1940 by Canadian geologist **Dr John Williamson** ([Wikipedia Williamson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Williamson_diamond_mine), [Mining Technology profile](https://www.mining-technology.com/projects/williamson-mine-shinyanga-province/)).
- **Geometry:** **146 ha surface area — the largest economic kimberlite pipe in the world by area** (Camafuca in Angola is larger but lower-grade water-saturated). Olivine-rich, weathered top facies (yellow ground → blue ground) processed by DMS.
- **Ownership history & 2025 transition:** Petra Diamonds held 75% / Government of Tanzania 25% from 2009 to early 2025. In May 2025 Petra transferred its entire interest to **Pink Diamonds Limited** (Tanzanian) — Tanzania now holds an effective **37%** through the deal ([African Mining Market](https://africanminingmarket.com/tanzania-now-owns-37-precent-of-williamson-diamonds-ltd/12143/), [Mining Weekly 2025-01-22](https://www.miningweekly.com/article/petras-entire-interest-in-williamson-headed-for-tanzanias-pink-diamonds-2025-01-22)). Production data for FY2025/26 is therefore not yet published.
- **Historic production:** 19+ million carats since 1940; produced the **54.5 ct fancy pink Williamson diamond** gifted to Queen Elizabeth II in 1947 — set into a brooch (1953) and one of the most photographed coloured diamonds in the world.
- **Shinyanga alluvial trains:** small artisanal recoveries persist downstream of Mwadui; STAMICO has periodically licensed concentrators.

### D. Exploration & determination

- **Indicator-mineral chemistry (KIMs):** pyrope garnet (G9/G10 sub-calcic chrome-rich = diamond-bearing field), chrome diopside, picroilmenite (Mg-rich = preserved diamond), chromite, forsteritic olivine. Sampled from streams, soils, glacial till, ant hills (yes — termite-mound geochem in Botswana is a textbook technique).
- **Geophysics:** airborne magnetics (kimberlite magnetic high or low vs host), gravity (kimberlite typically low density vs Archean granite/gneiss), AEM for water-saturated pipes.
- **Bulk sampling:** mini-bulk (10–100 t) → large-diameter drilling (LDD) → trial mining (10,000+ t) to establish grade (cpht — carats per hundred tonnes) and stone-size distribution.
- **Sampling protocol — gem coloured stones:** parcel of rough → wash + sort + grade → split into size lots (<100 ct, 100–500 ct, 500 ct+) → per-lot valuation; **per-carat value is strongly non-linear with size**, so the lot construction itself is value-creating.

### E. Mining methods

- **Open-pit kimberlite (Mwadui):** truck-and-shovel, conventional bench mining; the upper 200 m worked since 1940.
- **Underground kimberlite (Cullinan, Finsch):** sub-level cave or block cave when pit depth uneconomic.
- **Alluvial:** dredge + trommel + DMS for river gravels; pump-and-suction for offshore.

### F. Recovery & sorting

- **Dense-media separation (DMS):** ferrosilicon slurry at SG 2.7–3.2 — diamond (3.52) sinks, gangue floats.
- **X-ray sorting:** diamond fluoresces under X-ray, separated from heavy minerals by X-ray luminescence (Tomra, Bourevestnik, BSI, Steinert). Now the dominant secondary recovery step; grease tables (diamond hydrophobicity) phased out except in legacy plants.
- **Final pass:** hand-sort + magnetic + UV.

### G. Cutting, treatment & grading

- **Cutting centres:** Surat (India — 90% of polished volume, mid/lower tier), Antwerp (high-end + sights), Ramat Gan (Israel — large + fancy), New York (top end), Mumbai. Hong Kong dominates trading.
- **Treatments:** HPHT colour enhancement (Type IIa brown → colourless), laser drilling (inclusion removal), irradiation + annealing (fancy blues/greens/yellows), fracture filling (legally disclosable; voids GIA reports if found).
- **Lab-grown diamond (LGD):** CVD and HPHT-grown stones are physically diamond. Detection via spectroscopy (FTIR, photoluminescence at LN₂ temperature — N3, NV, SiV centres). Required equipment: GIA iD100, De Beers SynthDetect, Yehuda Sherlock.
- **Grading — natural diamond:** GIA 4Cs (Carat, Cut, Colour D–Z + Fancy, Clarity FL–I3). Reports: GIA Diamond Grading Report, AGS (now part of GIA) light performance.
- **Pricing reference:** **Rapaport price list** ([rapaport.com](https://www.rapaport.com/)) — weekly polished prices in USD per carat by colour/clarity/weight grid; rough is benchmarked to ~60–75% of polished after yield loss.

### H. Movement, packaging, security — Kimberley Process

- **Kimberley Process Certification Scheme (KPCS):** launched 2003, mandatory for **all rough diamond cross-border movement**. 60 participants / 86 countries. **India chairs 2026** ([kimberleyprocess.com](https://www.kimberleyprocess.com/), [Canada NRCan import/export](https://natural-resources.canada.ca/minerals-mining/services-mining-industry/importing-exporting-rough-diamonds-kimberley-process), [US CBP](https://www.cbp.gov/trade/programs-administration/kimberley-diamonds-process-certification)). 2026 Intersessional scheduled 11–14 May at JIO Mumbai.
- **Required:** tamper-resistant sealed parcel + KP Certificate from exporting authority. Tanzania's KP focal point is the Ministry of Minerals / Mining Commission.
- **Logistics:** Brink's, Malca-Amit, Loomis, Ferrari Group for insured air freight DAR/JRO → Antwerp / Mumbai / Tel Aviv / New York.

## 2. Tanzanite — (Ca,Sr)Al₂(SiO₄)(OH), V-bearing zoisite, Mererani ONLY

### A. Identity

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Formula | Ca₂Al₃(SiO₄)(Si₂O₇)O(OH) — vanadium-bearing variety of zoisite |
| Crystal system | Orthorhombic (Pnma) |
| Hardness | 6.5 (CRITICAL: soft for jewellery — set protected; avoid ultrasonic) |
| RI | 1.691–1.700 |
| SG | 3.35 |
| Dispersion | 0.030 |
| Birefringence | 0.009 (low) |
| Chromophore | V³⁺ (with minor Cr³⁺) substituting for Al³⁺; oxidation state shift on heat is the colour-change mechanism |
| Pleochroism | **Strong trichroism** unheated: violet / blue / yellow-brown along three optic axes; heated → strong dichroism violet / blue |
| Variety names | Tanzanite (blue-violet, trade), "fancy tanzanite" (yellow, green, pink — rare), green zoisite, anyolite (with ruby + green zoisite, ornamental from Longido) |

### B. Geology — the most geographically restricted gem on Earth

Tanzanite occurs in **ONE deposit in the world**: a 4 km long × 2 km wide × ~3 km vertical envelope at **Mererani / Merelani**, ~70 km SE of Arusha at the foot of Kilimanjaro. Hosted in **graphitic gneiss / boudinaged tremolite-actinolite-zoisite-bearing horizon** within the Neoproterozoic **Mozambique Belt** at greenschist-to-lower-amphibolite facies. Structurally trapped in **boudin necks and shear-bound pods**; mineralisation is a localised vanadium-rich metamorphic-hydrothermal product of the East African Orogeny (~600 Ma) ([Wikipedia — Tanzanite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tanzanite), [Mindat — Tanzanite locality](https://www.mindat.org/loc-2920.html)).

This is **the most geologically restricted commercial gem deposit in the world** — every gem-quality tanzanite on Earth comes from a few square kilometres in northern Tanzania. The supply is therefore inherently finite (estimates: 20–30 more years at current production rates).

### C. Tanzania — Mererani Blocks A/B/C/D, Wall, STAMICO

In 1990 the government split the deposit into four blocks ([Raregemstone Co. — Mererani mining](https://www.theraregemstonecompany.com/gemology-articles/tanzanite-mining-small-scale-vs-large-scale), [TanzaniaInvest — TanzaniteOne Mine](https://www.tanzaniainvest.com/tanzanite)):

- **Block A** — **Kilimanjaro Mines Ltd** (formerly Bridgepoint / Afgem-linked). Mechanised underground operation.
- **Block B** — reserved for **artisanal & small-scale (ASM) miners**, ~hundreds of Primary Mining Licences under AREMA (Arusha Regional Miners' Association).
- **Block C** — **TanzaniteOne Mining Ltd (TTM)** — flagship industrial-scale shaft mine; was Richland Resources, now 50% Tanzanian State (STAMICO) under the 2010 local-content law; controlling private operator is Sky Associates Group.
- **Block D** — **ASM**, ~700 mines under PMLs.

**The Mererani Wall** — completed September 2018 by President Magufuli's administration — is a **24 km (15-mile) perimeter wall** with CCTV, a single export gate, and ID-scanned worker entry/exit. **Production tripled** from 147.7 kg (2018) to **781.2 kg (2019)** as smuggling collapsed and royalties flowed to TRA ([Wikipedia — Tanzanite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tanzanite)). The Wall is **the single most successful gem-formalisation infrastructure in African history** and is now studied as a model for ruby (Songea) and gold (Geita).

**Vertical extent:** Block C shafts are now > 800 m deep; ASM blocks (B/D) routinely descend 500–700 m via hand-winched shafts under highly hazardous conditions — collapse and CO₂ asphyxiation kill miners every year.

### D. Exploration & determination

- **Empirical** — ASM follows the boudin neck along strike ("mishipa" = veins in Swahili); industrial uses **3D structural modelling + LIDAR + downhole geophysics**. The mineralised horizon dips ~45° SE; the strike trends NE.
- **Geochemistry:** V content in soil/rock geochem distinguishes potential tanzanite horizons; nearby tremolite + graphite gneiss is the lithological vector.
- **Drill testing:** core drilling rarely intersects gem pods (size is 5–50 cm) — bulk drift mining is needed to confirm reserve.

### E. Mining methods

- **Industrial (Block A, C):** vertical shaft + horizontal drives following the mineralised horizon; mechanised drill-and-blast small-diameter rounds, careful blasting to avoid gem fracture; trolley winches for ore transport.
- **ASM (B, D):** hand-dug narrow shafts (1 m × 1 m), bucket-and-winch ore haulage, hand-cobbing at surface. Extremely hazardous — multiple fatality events per year — and the Wall has improved but not eliminated risk.

### F. Recovery & sorting

- Hand-cobbing at the mine face — gem-quality pods are visually identified and removed by hand.
- Surface washing + UV-flash sort + hand-picking.
- Pre-form grading: rough sorted into **brown (heatable to blue)** vs **already-blue natural** vs **green/yellow fancy** vs **bicolour**. Block C runs a sorting house with calibrated daylight booths.

### G. Cutting, treatment & grading

- **Heat treatment is industry standard for tanzanite** and disclosed automatically (>95% of all tanzanite sold has been heated). Process:
  - Temperature: **450–600 °C** (some references say 400 °C suffices; literature converges around 500 °C / 30–60 min in air or oxidising atmosphere; controlled cooldown ([Earth's Treasury heat treatment](https://www.earthstreasury.com/2024/01/25/heat-treatment-of-tanzanite-zoisite/), [Raregemstone Co. heating facts](https://www.theraregemstonecompany.com/gemology-articles/tanzanite-heating), [MDPI 2020 — Cause of colour modification in tanzanite after heat treatment](https://www.mdpi.com/1420-3049/25/16/3743), [PMC version](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7465713/)).
  - Mechanism: V³⁺ → V³⁺ but with oxidation of associated traces — the absorption band at ~450 nm (the yellow component) is suppressed, eliminating the brown/yellow pleochroic direction; pleochroism collapses from trichroic to dichroic violet-blue.
  - **Permanent and stable.** ~3× value uplift for the blue colour vs equivalent unheated brown rough.
- **Cutting centres:** Jaipur (mid-tier), Bangkok (high-volume), Idar-Oberstein (specialty + large), Arusha cutting houses (TanzaniteOne, TanzaniteAfrica) for domestic value addition.
- **Grading — Tanzanite Foundation scale (TF):**
  - Colour: **vB (violetish-blue)** to **bV (blueish-violet)**; saturation **1–6** (6 = vivid).
  - Clarity: **IF, VVS, VS, SI, I** (eye-clean threshold ~VS).
  - "Investment grade" = **AAA** (vivid blue or violet, eye-clean, >3 ct, well-cut).
- Reports: Tanzanite Foundation, GIA (origin = Tanzania automatic by species), AGL.

### H. Movement, packaging, security

- **Export route (formalised):** miner → Mererani Trading Centre (MTC, at the Wall gate) → Mining Commission valuation by **Government Mineral Officer (GMO)** → royalty 6% + 1% inspection paid → export permit + **Certificate of Origin (Tanzania — only origin in the world)** → courier (Brink's, Malca-Amit) to JRO / DAR → JFK / Antwerp / Bangkok / Jaipur.
- **TMX auction channel:** since 2023 the **Tanzania Mercantile Exchange** runs electronic gem auctions; rough tanzanite is increasingly cleared here ([BizLens — TMX online gem auction 2025](https://thebizlens.co.tz/2025/11/27/gemstone-buyers-embrace-tanzanias-new-online-auction-system/)).
- **Sky Associates / TanzaniteOne** runs its own scheduled rough auctions; Bonas Group acts as auctioneer for some Geneva tenders.

## 3. Ruby — Al₂O₃ (corundum), Cr-coloured

### A. Identity

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Formula | Al₂O₃ (corundum), red variety; pink corundum >0.1% Cr by convention is "ruby" in trade |
| Crystal system | Trigonal (R3̄c) |
| Hardness | 9 (second only to diamond) |
| RI | 1.762–1.770 |
| SG | 4.00 |
| Dispersion | 0.018 |
| Birefringence | 0.008–0.009 (negative uniaxial) |
| Chromophore | Cr³⁺ in Al-site; broad band absorption at 410 nm (violet) and 550 nm (yellow-green) → transmits red + emits red fluorescence (the "glow" of Burmese ruby) |
| Pleochroism | Dichroic — purplish-red ∥ optic axis, orange-red ⊥ |
| Fluorescence | Strong red (Cr) under UV — diagnostic; Fe quenches fluorescence (Thai/Songea less red, more brownish) |

### B. Geology — deposit types

- **Marble-hosted (Mogok Burma, Mong Hsu Burma, Luc Yen Vietnam, Jegdalek Afghanistan):** classic "pigeon-blood" — low iron, strong fluorescence; ruby formed in metamorphosed limestone where evaporite-derived Cr meets Al-rich pelite.
- **Amphibolite/gneiss-hosted (Winza Tanzania, Mozambique Montepuez):** higher iron → more saturated but less fluorescent; large crystals common.
- **Basalt-hosted (Thailand, Cambodia Pailin, Australia):** high-iron, brownish-red; gem corundum is xenocryst transported by alkali basalt.
- **Skarn / metasomatic (Songea, Longido):** Cr-rich metasediment + mafic intrusion.

### C. Tanzania — Songea, Winza, Mahenge, Longido

- **Songea (Ruvuma Region)** — discovered 1991 by a rice farmer; main villages **Masuguro, Ngembambili**, ~60 km W of Songea town ([Gem Adventurer — Songea](https://www.gemadventurer.com/gemstones/songea-ruby/), [Mindat — Songea locality](https://www.mindat.org/loc-152378.html)). High Mg, high Fe → deeply saturated red and orange-red rubies; also pink, purple, blue, green, yellow sapphires from the same alluvial gravels. Weak red fluorescence due to Fe content.
- **Winza (Mpwapwa District, Dodoma Region)** — discovered late 2007. Hosted in amphibolite at the contact with ultramafics. Trace-element fingerprint: high Fe, low V, low Ga, low Cr (relative to Burmese), zero or near-zero Ti. Distinctive **hollow-tube inclusions, mineral inclusions of green amphibole, kyanite, rutile silk** ([SSEF — Winza new mine](https://www.ssef.ch/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/2008_Krzemnicki_New_Tanzania_mine_uncovers_source_of_exceptional_Rubies.pdf), [GRS — Winza rubies identified](https://www.gemresearch.ch/assets/publication_articles_documents/journal-07-1.pdf), [GIA Winter 2008 G&G — Winza rubies](https://www.gia.edu/doc/Winter-2008-Gems-Gemology-Rubies-Sapphire-Winza-Tanzania.pdf), [SSEF — Winza hollow tube](https://www.ssef.ch/winza-ruby-with-hollow-tube-as-a-helix/)). Some Winza rubies show **pinkish-purple to red colour change**.
- **Mahenge (Morogoro Region)** — known for spinel (see §8) but minor ruby occurrences in the same Mozambique Belt amphibolite.
- **Longido (Arusha Region)** — ornamental "anyolite" (ruby + green zoisite + black hornblende) — slabbed for sculpture, not gem-cut ruby.

### D. Exploration & determination

- **Geochem vectors:** Cr (LiBO₂ fusion + ICP), V, Ga, Fe, Ti — combined plotted on origin-discrimination diagrams (Pardieu/Sutherland diagrams).
- **Trace-element LA-ICP-MS fingerprinting** is the standard origin tool — Mozambique Montepuez vs Tanzanian Winza vs Burmese Mogok form separable clusters.
- **ASM exploration:** trench across amphibolite contact, hand-screen alluvial gravel from drainage downstream of host outcrop.

### E. Mining methods

- **Songea:** alluvial — pit-and-screen, washing plant, hand-pick. Some hard-rock at source areas.
- **Winza:** hard-rock — shafts and trenches into the amphibolite host; bulk material to hand-sort.
- **Mahenge area:** open-cut + tunnel into marble/pegmatite contact for spinel-ruby co-extraction.

### F. Recovery & sorting

- Hand-picking after wash-screen — UV flashlight for Cr fluorescence confirms ruby vs garnet/spinel.
- **Pre-form / cobbing** at site removes opaque waste before parcel goes to grading.
- Conveyor-belt sort lines at industrial operations.

### G. Cutting, treatment & grading

- **Heat treatment** is industry standard (~95% of all ruby) — temperature 1,400–1,800 °C in reducing or oxidising atmosphere, dissolves silk, improves clarity, may shift colour. Stable + disclosed as "H".
- **Flux-assisted heat** ("heat with borax/flux residue") — heals fractures with re-crystallised corundum + flux glass; disclosed as "H(Fl)" or by specific lab nomenclature.
- **Beryllium diffusion** — Be infusion at 1,800 °C alters colour-zoning, controversial; disclosed (LMHC).
- **Lead-glass filling** — low-end ruby drowned in PbO glass to mask fractures; legally must be disclosed; aggressively controversial (sold as "ruby" but durability is glass-like, not corundum-like).
- **Grading bodies:** GIA, AGL, SSEF (Geneva), Gübelin (Lucerne), **Lotus Gemology** (Bangkok — Richard Hughes; specialty in ruby/sapphire/spinel origin), GRS. Top labs issue **origin opinions** and **treatment disclosure** as separate findings.
- **Origin premium:** for similar quality, **Burmese (Mogok) ruby trades 3–5× Mozambique ruby**; Mozambique > Madagascar > Tanzanian Winza > Songea > Thai. **Winza top stones can approach Burmese pricing** when "Burma-like" inclusions and low Fe/high Cr coincide.

### H. Movement, packaging, security

- **Songea route:** Songea → Arusha (TAMIDA gem dealers' network) → Bangkok cutting → Geneva auction.
- **Winza route:** Dodoma → Arusha → Bangkok / Idar-Oberstein → grading at SSEF or Lotus → top-end retail.
- **No KP for ruby** — only Certificate of Origin from Tanzania + commercial invoice.

## 4. Sapphire — Al₂O₃ (corundum), Fe+Ti coloured

### A. Identity

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Formula | Al₂O₃ corundum (all non-red corundum = sapphire) |
| Crystal system | Trigonal |
| Hardness | 9 |
| RI | 1.762–1.770 |
| SG | 4.00 |
| Chromophore | Blue: Fe²⁺/Ti⁴⁺ intervalence charge transfer (IVCT); pink: Cr (low); yellow: Fe³⁺ + colour centres; padparadscha: combined Cr+Fe (pink-orange "lotus" colour); green: Fe; violet: V+Cr; colour-change: V dominant |
| Varieties | Blue, pink, yellow, green, padparadscha, colour-change, white (colourless), star (asterism by rutile silk), parti-coloured (Australian/Tanzanian Umba) |

### B. Geology

- **Basalt-hosted (Australia, Thailand, Madagascar Ilakaka, Tanzania Tunduru)** — xenocrystic corundum brought up by alkali basalt; high-iron, often green/blue/yellow-brown.
- **Metamorphic (Kashmir, Sri Lanka, Madagascar, Tanzania Umba)** — marble or gneiss host; low-iron, vivid colours, classic Kashmir cornflower velvet.
- **Pegmatite-affiliated** — some Sri Lankan and Madagascan deposits.

### C. Tanzania — Tunduru, Umba, Songea, Mahenge

- **Tunduru (Ruvuma Region)** — alluvial gravels, multi-colour sapphires + ruby + alexandrite + chrysoberyl + garnet + spinel. The world's most diverse alluvial gem deposit per source area. Hilly terrain, shallow valleys, vertical pits 1–10 m to wash gravel ([NaturalSapphireCo. — Africa](https://www.thenaturalsapphirecompany.com/education/sapphire-mining/sapphire-mines-africa/), [Brilliyond — sapphire mining TZ](https://www.brilliyond.com/blog/sapphire-mining-in-tanzania-an-overview)).
- **Umba River Valley (Tanga Region)** — fancy sapphires (orange, yellow, padparadscha, green, parti-colour) hosted in gneiss/amphibolite; producing for 50+ years ([Mindat — Umba sapphire](https://www.mindat.org/locentry-832941.html)).
- **Songea** — also produces fancy sapphires alongside ruby.
- **Mahenge** — minor blue sapphire.
- **Tunduru is also the source of Tanzania's gem-grade alexandrite + chrysoberyl + zircon** — see §6 + §11.

### D. Exploration & determination

- Stream-sediment heavy-mineral panning → red/blue concentrate → assay.
- LA-ICP-MS distinguishes Tanzania Umba (low Ga, moderate Fe) from Madagascar Ilakaka (low Fe, moderate Ga) from Kashmir (very low Fe, very low Ga, "old mines" trace pattern).

### E. Mining methods

- **Tunduru/Umba** — pit-and-screen alluvial: vertical hand-dug pits to bedrock, gravel hauled to wash plant, screen + sort.
- Mechanised operations: trommel + screen + jig.

### F. Recovery & sorting

- Wash → screen size fractions → hand-pick using UV (blue sapphire has weak chalky-blue UV; pink/padparadscha shows orange-red Cr fluorescence under LW-UV — diagnostic).

### G. Cutting, treatment & grading

- **Heat treatment standard** (~90% of blue sapphire). 1,600–1,800 °C in oxidising or reducing atmosphere — dissolves rutile silk, deepens blue, decolourises silk-blocked stones.
- **Beryllium diffusion** — for orange/padparadscha and yellow conversions. Highly controversial; must be disclosed.
- **Surface diffusion** — older method; thin colour layer, removed if re-polished.
- **Cutting centres:** Bangkok dominant; Sri Lanka (Beruwala, Ratnapura), Jaipur, Idar.
- **Origin reports:** SSEF, Gübelin, Lotus, GIA, GRS.

### H. Movement & I. Pricing

|---|---|---|---|---|
| Tunduru blue heated commercial | 100–400 | 400–1,500 | 800–3,000 | 1,500–6,000 |
| Tunduru blue fine heated | 500–1,500 | 2,000–6,000 | 5,000–15,000 | 10,000–30,000 |
| Umba padparadscha unheated | 1,500–4,000 | 5,000–15,000 | 15,000–40,000 | 30,000–100,000 |
| Kashmir cornflower unheated | 20,000–60,000 | 60,000–200,000 | 200,000–500,000 | 500,000–1.5M+ |

Record: 27.68 ct "Blue Belle of Asia" (Ceylon Kashmir-like) Christie's Geneva Nov 2014, USD 17.3M (~USD 625k/ct).

### J. EHS — same as ruby; child-labour and pit-collapse risk at Tunduru.

### K. Decision triggers (Boji)

- "Umba padparadscha → unheated + Cr/Fe LA-ICP-MS fingerprint mandatory; origin report from SSEF or Gübelin if >USD 10k/ct."
- "Tunduru blue with star asterism → grade as 'star sapphire' separately — different price segment."
- "Be-diffusion test (FTIR + LA-ICP-MS surface profile) on any orange-yellow sapphire before sale."

## 5. Emerald — Be₃Al₂Si₆O₁₈ (beryl), Cr/V coloured

### A. Identity

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Formula | Be₃Al₂Si₆O₁₈ — Cr (or V) substituting for Al |
| Crystal system | Hexagonal (P6/mcc) |
| Hardness | 7.5–8 (but **fracture-prone** — natural fissures = "jardin") |
| RI | 1.577–1.583 |
| SG | 2.72 |
| Birefringence | 0.005–0.009 (low) |
| Chromophore | Cr³⁺ (Colombia, Zambia, Tanzania) and V³⁺ (Brazil, some Tanzanian); Fe acts as "dampener" giving bluer hue |
| Pleochroism | Distinct — bluish-green / yellowish-green |
| Inclusions | Three-phase (Colombia: gas+liquid+halite), two-phase (most other origins), mica/biotite plates (schist-hosted) |

### B. Geology

- **Schist-hosted (Tanzania Manyara, Brazil Itabira, Zambia Kafubu, Russia Ural, Zimbabwe Sandawana)** — Be from pegmatite intrusion meets Cr/V from mafic/ultramafic schist; emerald in mica schist + pegmatite contact zone.
- **Black-shale / hydrothermal (Colombia Muzo, Chivor, Coscuez)** — sedimentary brine + organic-rich shale + tectonic veining; produces world's finest emeralds with three-phase inclusions and trapiche pattern.
- **Pegmatite (some Madagascar, Afghan Panjshir)** — single-source pegmatite emerald.

### C. Tanzania — Manyara, Sumbawanga

- **Lake Manyara emerald deposit (Arusha Region)** — discovered late 1960s, ~3 km west of Lake Manyara, south of Maji Moto hot springs. **Biotite-phlogopite schist + pegmatite contact**. Mined small-scale; same horizon also hosts **alexandrite** (see §6). P-T of growth: 370–470 °C and 3.0–7.0 kbar ([ScienceDirect — fluid inclusion Manyara + Sumbawanga emeralds](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0899536201800701), [Gemdat — Lake Manyara](https://www.gemdat.org/loc-189614.html), [Mindat — Magara mine](https://www.mindat.org/locentry-154992.html), [ResearchGate — Zircon inclusions in Manyara emeralds](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/362875754_Zircon_inclusions_in_emeralds_from_Lake_Manyara_Tanzania)).
- Co-occurring minerals: apatite, yellow chrysoberyl, pyrope-almandine garnet, spinel, olivine, clinopyroxene, chondrodite, corundum, phenakite, tourmaline, beryl.
- **Sumbawanga (Rukwa Region)** — smaller emerald + green beryl occurrence in similar schist setting.

### D. Exploration & determination

- **Lithology vector:** find biotite-phlogopite schist + pegmatite contact within Mozambique Belt or Tanzanian Shield boundary zones.
- **Geochem:** Be in rock, Cr+V in schist; pathfinders.
- **LA-ICP-MS fingerprint** for origin (Zambia Kafubu = high Fe; Colombia Muzo = low Fe high alkalis + 3-phase inclusions; Tanzania Manyara = moderate Fe, characteristic phlogopite inclusions).

### E. Mining methods

- **Trench + tunnel along schist horizon.** Minimal blasting — emerald is brittle, gem-loss risk extreme. Pneumatic hand tools, careful chiselling preferred.
- Hand-cobbing of pocket material.

### F. Recovery & sorting

- Hand-pick from washed crushed schist; UV-LW (Cr fluorescence is partially quenched by Fe — Colombian fluoresces; Zambian/Tanzanian less).
- Iodide heavy-liquid float-sink and dichromate gravity rarely needed.

### G. Cutting, treatment & grading

- **Fissure filling (oiling) is industry standard** — cedarwood oil (traditional), Opticon resin (modern), Joban filler, ExCel. Disclosed as "Minor / Moderate / Significant".
- **Cutting centres:** Jaipur (dominant for emerald globally), Bogotá (Colombian), Idar-Oberstein.
- **Step cut ("emerald cut")** invented for this stone — minimises stress on inclusions.
- **Reports:** GIA, AGL, SSEF, Gübelin, GRS. Origin (Colombia / Zambia / Brazil / Tanzania) and clarity-enhancement level are the two value drivers.

### H. Movement & I. Pricing

| Origin & quality | 1 ct | 3 ct | 5 ct |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tanzania Manyara commercial | 100–400 | 300–1,200 | 600–2,500 |
| Tanzania Manyara fine vivid green | 500–1,500 | 2,000–6,000 | 4,000–12,000 |
| Zambia Kafubu fine | 1,500–4,000 | 5,000–15,000 | 10,000–30,000 |
| Colombia Muzo top (minor oil) | 8,000–25,000 | 30,000–80,000 | 80,000–200,000+ |

Record: Rockefeller emerald (Colombian, 18.04 ct) Christie's NY Jun 2017 — USD 5.5M (~USD 305k/ct).

### J. EHS — Schist-tunnel collapse; silicosis from dry drilling — wet drilling mandatory.

### K. Decision triggers (Boji)

- "Manyara emerald → oil-only filling; report fill level on every invoice (LMHC)."
- "Emerald > 2 ct → grade at GIA Jaipur or SSEF Geneva; record Cr vs V dominance for origin defence."
- "Cut at Jaipur for export markets; do not local-cut high-value rough — yield loss in inexperienced cutting can be 60%+."

## 6. Alexandrite — BeAl₂O₄ (chrysoberyl), Cr-coloured colour-change

### A. Identity

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Formula | BeAl₂O₄ — chrysoberyl variety with Cr³⁺ substituting for Al³⁺ |
| Crystal system | Orthorhombic (Pbnm) |
| Hardness | 8.5 (very durable) |
| RI | 1.746–1.755 |
| SG | 3.73 |
| Birefringence | 0.009 |
| Chromophore | Cr³⁺ (also responsible for ruby red and emerald green; works because alexandrite transmits both red AND green bands, balance shifts with illuminant) |
| Variety | Alexandrite = strong colour-change (green daylight → red incandescent); colour-change chrysoberyl = weaker; cat's-eye chrysoberyl = chatoyant variety |

### B. Geology

Beryllium-rich silica-poor environment: pegmatite intruding Cr-rich ultramafic schist or marble. Same biotite-phlogopite host as Manyara emerald — they co-occur.

### C. Tanzania — Manyara & Tunduru

- **Manyara** — primary deposit, **phlogopite schist** host. Colour change usually mid-range (40–60%); Cr content 0.03% (vs Brazilian 0.30–0.73% — Brazilian shows stronger change but Tanzanian larger crystals possible) ([ResearchGate — Alexandrite Lake Manyara](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/270373838_Alexandrite_and_colour-change_chrysoberyl_from_the_Lake_Manyara_alexandrite-emerald_deposit_in_northern_Tanzania), [Boris Portal — Manyara alexandrite](https://boris-portal.unibe.ch/items/380918e1-cc80-43f8-9819-6b5e12ee28ff)).
- **Tunduru** — alluvial alexandrite + chrysoberyl + cat's-eye chrysoberyl. Inclusions: actinolite, mica, monazite, xenotime, rounded metamict zircon ([GIA Winter 2019 — Alexandrite origin determination](https://www.gia.edu/gems-gemology/winter-2019-alexandrite-geographic-origin-determination)).

### D–G. Exploration, mining, recovery, grading

- ASM placer recovery at Tunduru; pegmatite-tunnel at Manyara.
- **No heat treatment** in commercial use — colour change is structural.
- **Origin** determined by LA-ICP-MS — Ural (Russia) original locality is the gold standard; Brazilian, Tanzanian, Sri Lankan, Madagascan are separate clusters by Fe/Ga/Sn ratios.

## 7. Garnet group — tsavorite, rhodolite, mandarin (spessartine), hessonite, demantoid

The garnet group is **(Mg,Fe,Mn,Ca)₃(Al,Cr,Fe,V)₂(SiO₄)₃** — six end-members in two solid-solution series (pyralspite: pyrope-almandine-spessartine; ugrandite: uvarovite-grossular-andradite). Tanzania produces ALL major commercial garnets at world-class scale.

### A. Identity table

| Garnet | Formula | Hardness | RI | SG | Chromophore | Tanzanian source |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Tsavorite (grossular) | Ca₃Al₂(SiO₄)₃ | 7–7.5 | 1.734 | 3.6 | Cr³⁺, V³⁺ | **Lemshuko, Komolo, Lualenyi, Lelatema fault** |
| Rhodolite (pyrope-almandine) | (Mg,Fe)₃Al₂(SiO₄)₃ | 7–7.5 | 1.745–1.760 | 3.74–3.94 | Fe²⁺ + minor Cr | Umba, Tunduru |
| Mandarin/spessartine | Mn₃Al₂(SiO₄)₃ | 6.5–7.5 | 1.79–1.81 | 4.16 | Mn²⁺ | Loliondo, Kangala |
| Hessonite (grossular) | Ca₃Al₂(SiO₄)₃ | 6.5–7.5 | 1.74 | 3.6 | Fe³⁺/Mn²⁺ | Umba |
| Demantoid (andradite) | Ca₃Fe₂(SiO₄)₃ | 6.5–7 | 1.888 | 3.85 | Fe (yellow-green), Cr (vivid green) | Loliondo (Merelani Hills) |
| Mali garnet (grossular-andradite) | mixed | 7 | 1.79 | 3.7 | Fe | Mali (not Tanzania — comparator) |
| Colour-change garnet | pyrope-spessartine | 7 | varies | varies | V+Cr | Bekily Madagascar; small TZ |

Garnets are **singly refractive (isotropic)** — diagnostic vs corundum/tourmaline/quartz.

### B. Geology

- **Tsavorite:** Ca-rich (calc-silicate) metasediments + graphitic gneiss, V/Cr-bearing, in the Mozambique Belt — same belt as tanzanite. Lithology: graphitic gneiss + sulphide-bearing horizons.
- **Rhodolite/spessartine:** pegmatite + skarn + amphibolite.
- **Demantoid:** ultramafic-hosted serpentinite + skarn.

### C. Tanzania — tsavorite is the headline

- **Tsavorite discovery:** Campbell R. Bridges (Scottish-born Kenyan gemologist) found the first tsavorite in 1967 at **Komolo** in the **Lelatema Hills**, northeastern Tanzania. The Tanzanian-Kenyan border bisects the same fault system — Lemshuko (Tanzania) is 15 km from Komolo; Tsavo (Kenya, the trade-name source) is 100 km away ([SosnaGems tsavorite guide](https://sosnagems.com/blogs/about-gemstones/tsavorite-garnet-guide), [Tanzanite Experience — tsavorite](https://www.tanzaniteexperience.com/tsavorite/), [Gemstone Detective — tsavorite rarity](https://www.gemstonedetective.com/post/discovering-the-rarity-of-tsavorite-garnet), [iRocks — Lindi tsavorite specimen](https://www.irocks.com/minerals/specimen/5737), [AGTA Prism — Bridges Tsavorite](https://agta.org/from-prism-volume-1-2023-map-the-mine-green-gem-boom-bridges-tsavorite-in-kenya/)).
- **Modern Tanzanian production:** Komolo + Lemshuko + **Lualenyi** + Merelani Hills + Lutela in Lindi-Tanga zone. ASM dominant.
- **Mandarin spessartine** — Loliondo, Kangala (north of Lake Natron).
- **Rhodolite + hessonite + mixed garnet** — Umba River Valley.

### D. Exploration & determination

- **Lithology vector:** graphitic gneiss + Ca-Al calc-silicate boudins + sulphide rust = tsavorite hot zone.
- Geochem: Cr/V geochem; **graphite is the redox pathfinder** — Bridges' original insight.

### E. Mining methods

- Hand-dug shafts and adits along the mineralised horizon; ASM-dominant.
- Trommel + wash + hand-pick for alluvial garnet.

### F. Recovery & sorting

- Hand-pick after wash; vivid green tsavorite is unmistakable in daylight.
- No UV signature for garnet — relies on visual.

## 8. Spinel — MgAl₂O₄ — Mahenge red is world-class

### A. Identity

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Formula | MgAl₂O₄ — Cr/V/Co colours |
| Crystal system | Cubic (Fd3̄m) — singly refractive |
| Hardness | 8 |
| RI | 1.712–1.717 |
| SG | 3.58–3.61 |
| Chromophore | Cr³⁺ (red — Mahenge "neon" red), Co²⁺ (rare cobalt blue — Lukande/Sri Lanka), Fe (lavender/grey), V (purple) |
| Distinction from ruby | Singly refractive vs ruby's birefringence; no pleochroism; lower SG (3.6 vs 4.0); different absorption spectrum |

### B. Geology

Marble + skarn metamorphism; co-occurs with corundum and graphite. Mozambique Belt is the world's spinel belt — Burma Mogok, Tajikistan Pamir, Vietnam Luc Yen, Tanzania Mahenge are the four world-class deposits.

### C. Tanzania — Mahenge

- **Mahenge plateau, Morogoro Region** — discovered as a major source in **2007** when massive ~50 kg red spinel boulder was unearthed at Ipanko, producing >50,000 ct of cuttable rough including stones >100 ct ([Reuven Veksler — Mahenge spinel](https://reuvenveksler.com/education/mahenge-spinel-what-is-it/), [Farmonaut — Mahenge spinel 2026 guide](https://farmonaut.com/mining/mahenge-spinel-red-spinel-price-per-carat-2026-guide), [Tommaso Bazzano — Mahenge spinel](https://www.tommasobazzano.com/blog/why-mahenge-spinel-became-one-of-the-most-desired-coloured-stones), [Ruby-Sapphire — Mahenge gem hunt](https://www.ruby-sapphire.com/index.php/about/10-articles/798-tanzania-ruby-sapphire-spinel)).
- **Neon Mahenge red:** Cr-rich, no Fe-quench → fluorescent red glow under daylight (the "Jedi" tier — name borrowed from Burmese Mogok pinks).

### D. Exploration & determination

- Marble + spinel + corundum geochem; magnetic anomaly because spinel co-occurs with magnetite in some skarn zones.

### E. Mining methods — Tunnel + pocket mining in marble + alluvial recovery from streams.

### G. Cutting & grading

- **Treatments: NONE commercial** — natural colour, no heat. (Some claim heat is sometimes used; LMHC does not yet list spinel as heat-treated industry-standard.)
- Cutting: Bangkok + Idar.
- Reports: GIA, Lotus, SSEF, GRS.

## 9. Tourmaline group — chrome, indicolite, rubellite, watermelon, paraíba

### A. Identity

Tourmaline is a **borosilicate group** with end-members elbaite (Li), schorl (Fe), dravite (Mg), uvite (Ca-Mg), liddicoatite (Ca-Li). General formula: **XY₃Z₆(T₆O₁₈)(BO₃)₃V₃W** where X = Na/Ca/vacancy; Y = Li/Fe/Mn/Mg/Al; Z = Al/Cr; T = Si.

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Crystal system | Trigonal — strongly birefringent (0.014–0.040) |
| Hardness | 7–7.5 |
| RI | 1.620–1.655 |
| SG | 3.06 |
| Pleochroism | **Strong** — orient long axis ⊥ table to avoid dark zone |
| Trade varieties | Chrome (Cr³⁺ green), indicolite (Fe blue-green), rubellite (Mn red-pink), paraíba (Cu+Mn neon blue-green), watermelon (zoned pink/green), achroite (colourless), Brazilianite (yellow-green) |

### B. Geology

- **Granitic pegmatite** — the classic source (Brazil Minas Gerais, Madagascar, California, Mozambique).
- **Metasomatic schist** — chrome tourmaline.
- **Cu-bearing pegmatite + LCT (Li-Cs-Ta) suite** — paraíba.

### C. Tanzania — chrome tourmaline + paraíba-type

- **Chrome tourmaline** — Lake Manyara/Komolo area, vivid green (Cr³⁺ dominant); known since 1970s.
- **Paraíba-type Cu-Mn tourmaline** — small finds reported from **Komolo/Komatte** in Tanzania but the dominant African paraíba source is **Mozambique (Mavuco / Alto Ligonha pegmatite district**, discovered 2001, Cu-content confirmed 2003, widely traded 2005+) ([GIA Spring 2008 — Cu-bearing tourmaline Mozambique](https://www.gia.edu/gems-gemology/spring-2008-copper-bearing-tourmaline-mozambique-laurs), [SSEF — Paraiba tourmaline](https://www.ssef.ch/reports/paraiba-tourmaline/), [LMHC Information Sheet #6 — Paraiba](https://gcslab.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2021/12/Paraiba-Tourmaline.pdf), [Pala — Paraiba](http://www.palagems.com/paraiba)).
- **Indicolite + rubellite + watermelon** — from various Tanzanian pegmatites in Mtwara, Morogoro, Manyara.

### D–G. Standard pegmatite mining: pocket mining + tunnelling + hand-pick. Heating is common for blue/green stabilisation; paraíba is sometimes heated to remove brownish/reddish overtones.

### H. Trade-name protection

**"Paraíba tourmaline"** is a **trade name protected by LMHC and CIBJO** for blue-to-green Cu- and Mn-bearing elbaite of **any geographic origin** — Brazil (original Mina da Batalha), Mozambique, Tanzania, Nigeria. The chemistry (Cu detectable + Mn) is the gating definition, not the location. **Tanzania-origin paraíba is real and command Mozambique-tier pricing if neon saturation matches.**

## 10. Opal — SiO₂·nH₂O (brief, minor in Tanzania)

### A. Identity

- Formula: SiO₂·nH₂O (3–10% water) — **amorphous, not crystalline**.
- Hardness: 5.5–6.5 (soft, fracture-prone, dehydration crazing risk).
- RI: 1.37–1.52.
- SG: 1.98–2.20.
- Play of colour from **ordered silica microsphere stacking** diffracting visible light (Bragg-like).

### B. Geology — sedimentary (Australia Coober Pedy, Lightning Ridge, Mintabie — 95% world supply), hydrothermal (Mexico fire opal, Ethiopia Welo).

### C. Tanzania — minor occurrences in Mara/Singida regions, common opal (potch) only; no gem-grade precious opal at commercial scale.

## 13. Boji house decision-logic library — gem operations

**a. Origin certification policy**
- All gemstones > **USD 5,000/ct** must have one of: GIA, SSEF, Gübelin, Lotus, GRS, AGL report — with origin opinion if available.
- All tanzanite, regardless of price, must carry Tanzania Certificate of Origin (only origin in the world).

**b. Treatment disclosure**
- LMHC-aligned standard ([LMHC](https://www.lmhc-gemmology.org/)). Every Boji invoice records: species, variety, treatment level (LMHC code), and date of any post-mine processing.
- Default treatment status by species:
  - Tanzanite — heated (95%+). Encode as default.
  - Ruby/sapphire — heated (90%+). Encode as default.
  - Emerald — oiled (90%+). Encode minor/moderate/significant.
  - Garnet — NEVER treated. Encode as default "none".
  - Spinel — NONE commercial. Encode as "none" but flag for verification.
  - Alexandrite — NONE.
  - Tourmaline — heat sometimes (paraíba in particular). Encode per-stone.

**c. Lab routing decision tree**
- Origin opinion needed → SSEF or Gübelin (Switzerland) — top tier.
- Bangkok-proximity (ruby/sapphire/spinel) → Lotus Gemology (Bangkok).
- Diamond → GIA NY or Antwerp.
- Speed/cost → AGL (US) or GRS.
- Local Tanzania grading → Tanzanite Foundation + Mining Commission GMO.

**d. Auction channel decision**
- Tanzanite rough > USD 20k parcel → Sky Associates / TanzaniteOne auction; or Bonas Geneva; or TMX e-auction.
- Ruby/sapphire/spinel rough > USD 50k parcel → Bonhams or Christie's coloured-stone tenders; Tucson Feb show; Hong Kong Mar/Sep.
- Diamond rough > 1 ct → KP Certificate + Antwerp/Dubai tender; large stones → De Beers GSS or Sotheby's.
- Cut & polished retail → Geneva auctions Nov, NY auctions May.

**e. Smuggling / theft controls**
- Mererani Wall enforced; ASM tanzanite must exit via single gate at MTC.
- Brink's / Malca-Amit / Loomis / Ferrari Group for all > USD 50k parcels — insured DAR/JRO → destination.
- Biometric chain-of-custody at MTC (introduced 2019, expanded 2024) — Boji's Custody Agent records every hand-off.

**f. Heat-treat / value-uplift modelling**
- Tanzanite brown rough > 5 ct: EV(heat-blue) ≈ 3× rough price × P(clean heat ~80%) − cost(~USD 5–20/ct) − risk(~5% catastrophic loss).
- Sapphire blue rough with silk: EV(heat) ≈ 2× rough price × P(silk dissolves ~85%) − cost.
- Ruby Songea: EV(heat) ≈ 1.5–2× × P(~90%).

**g. Origin defence dataset**
Boji must maintain an internal **trace-element database** of every Tanzanian gem-producing locality:
- Tanzanite: Mererani Block A/B/C/D V/Cr/Sr ratios.
- Ruby: Songea (high Mg, high Fe), Winza (high Fe, low V, low Ga, hollow-tube inclusions), Mahenge (rare ruby).
- Sapphire: Tunduru (basalt-related), Umba (metamorphic).
- Emerald: Manyara (low Fe, Cr-dominant, phlogopite inclusions), Sumbawanga.
- Garnet: Komolo/Lemshuko tsavorite (V/Cr), Loliondo spessartine (Mn).
- Spinel: Mahenge (Cr-dominant red).
- Alexandrite: Manyara (low Cr 0.03%), Tunduru (alluvial mix).
- Tourmaline: Komolo/Komatte (Cu±Mn — paraíba candidate).
