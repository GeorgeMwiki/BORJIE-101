## 0. Why industrial minerals matter to Boji

Industrial minerals are the **silent giant** of Tanzania's extractive economy. Gold, gemstones, graphite and nickel dominate headlines and FX inflows, but **limestone, gypsum, salt, phosphate, pozzolana and kaolin underpin the cement, fertiliser, food, construction and ceramic value chains that every Tanzanian touches daily.** Two facts shape Boji's product strategy here:

1. **Volume × low margin.** A 2 Mt/yr cement plant moves more rock than any gold mine; an artisanal salt cooperative serves 500 villages. Boji's economic surface area is wider in industrials than in precious metals.
2. **Decision triggers, not assays.** A limestone owner does not need a 43-101; they need a cement-grade test (CaCO₃, MgO, SiO₂, alkali). A salt cooperative needs iodisation compliance. A vermiculite prospect must be asbestos-screened before extraction is ever recommended.

Boji's role is to **lower the cost of a correct decision** at every stage from outcrop to FOB. The structure below is uniform per mineral; the depth scales with Tanzania relevance.

## 1. Gypsum (CaSO₄·2H₂O)

### A. Identity
| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Formula | CaSO₄·2H₂O (selenite, alabaster, satin spar varieties); anhydrite is CaSO₄ |
| Mohs hardness | 2 (scratched by fingernail) |
| Density | 2.31–2.33 g/cm³ |
| Crystal habit | Tabular monoclinic, fibrous, massive |
| Solubility | 2.4 g/L in cold water (sparingly soluble) |
| Common associates | Anhydrite, halite, dolomite, native sulphur, clay |

### B. Geology of formation
Evaporite. Sea-water or playa-lake brine concentrates past gypsum saturation (~3.6× normal sea-water salinity), and CaSO₄·2H₂O precipitates in stratiform beds. Deeper burial dehydrates to anhydrite; near-surface re-hydration restores gypsum. Secondary gypsum forms from oxidation of sulphides (acid-mine drainage) or weathering of marls.

### C. Tanzania / East Africa relevance
| Deposit | Location | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Pindilo & Mbane | Kilwa, Lindi | ~90% purity; principal Twiga Cement feed; high-quality decades-long resource |
| Itigi | Singida region | ~75% purity; on the central rail line — strong rail logistics |
| Makanya | Same region | Sandy/earthy gypsite; artisan-controlled; lower purity |
| Msagali | Dodoma | Smaller deposits |

**~90% of national output is exported, predominantly to Kenya** (Bamburi, Mombasa Cement, Savannah). Dodoma Cement and Dangote Tanzania also pull domestic gypsum for cement set-time control.

### D. Exploration & determination
- Pitting + shallow drilling on bedded gypsite; tape-and-compass mapping is often sufficient for outcropping lenses.
- pXRF for Ca + S (with caution: light elements at LOD limit); **definitive test is loss-on-ignition (LOI ~20.9% theoretical) + chemical CaSO₄·2H₂O assay**.
- Purity proxy: insolubles after hot-water digestion; <5% insolubles is cement-grade.
- Cement-spec gypsum: SO₃ ≥ 35%, free moisture < 1%, free silica < 5%.

### E. Mining methods
Open-pit quarry, bench-and-flitch. Light drill-and-blast in harder gypsites, ripper-and-dozer in soft selenite. Hand-cobbing for purity sorting at small operations.

### F. Beneficiation & processing
- **Run-of-mine → primary jaw → secondary impactor → screen.** That is the whole story for cement-set-retarder gypsum.
- **Plaster of Paris (CaSO₄·½H₂O hemihydrate):** calcine ROM gypsum at 150 °C in kettles or rotary calciners to drive off 1.5 H₂O; further heating to 300 °C yields anhydrite.
- **Gypsum board:** slurry plaster + foam + paper-sandwich; sets via rehydration back to dihydrate.
- **Agricultural gypsum:** simple crush + screen to −2 mm; sold as soil conditioner for sodic soils.

### G. Final-product specifications
| Grade | Spec |
|---|---|
| Cement retarder | SO₃ ≥ 35%, free H₂O < 1%, NaCl < 0.2% |
| Plaster of Paris | ≥ 80% hemihydrate, setting time 8–15 min |
| Gypsum board core | ≥ 99% gypsum after rehydration; bulk density 650–750 kg/m³ |
| Agricultural | Ca ≥ 18%, S ≥ 14%, <5% insolubles |

### H. Movement, packaging, hazard
Bulk 25–40 t trucks; coastal bulk vessels for Mombasa/Dar export. Big bags (1 t) for plaster export. **Hazard profile is benign** — nuisance dust at worst; no silicosis risk; biodegradable.

### I. Pricing & markets
- Raw gypsum FOB East Africa: **USD 8–25/t** (purity-dependent).
- Cement-grade delivered Mombasa: USD 28–45/t.
- Plaster of Paris: USD 25–60/t bag.
- USGS-tracked US average mine value ~USD 9–11/t.

### J. EHS
Quarry-wall stability is the main risk (soft strata); silica content negligible; no respirable hazard at extraction. Calciner stage requires dust capture (nuisance + minor irritant).

### K. Decision triggers Boji should encode
- "If purity > 85% CaSO₄·2H₂O: route to Kenya cement export — adds USD 8–15/t vs domestic."
- "If purity 70–85%: domestic cement market (Twiga, Tanga, Mbeya, Dangote)."
- "If purity 50–70% or earthy: agricultural-gypsum lane only; price ceiling ~USD 12/t."
- "Anhydrite layers: blend with dihydrate; pure anhydrite is unsuitable as cement retarder alone."

- "Tanzania: Nation Boasts of Best Gypsum Deposits Globally" — allAfrica: https://allafrica.com/stories/201107260979.html
- "Gypsum available in plenty — Tanzania" — The Exchange: https://theexchange.africa/industry-and-trade/extractive-and-energy/gypsum-available-in-plenty-tanzania/

## 2. Limestone (CaCO₃)

### A. Identity
| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Formula | CaCO₃ (calcite); high-Mg variant is dolomitic, transitions to dolomite at CaMg(CO₃)₂ |
| Mohs hardness | 3 |
| Density | 2.71 g/cm³ pure calcite |
| Crystal habit | Massive sedimentary / micritic; coarse crystalline marbles |
| Associates | Dolomite, chert nodules, clay (marl), pyrite, phosphate, organic matter |

### B. Geology of formation
- **Sedimentary marine** (overwhelming majority): biogenic carbonate accumulation on shallow shelves; consolidates to limestone.
- **Metamorphic:** recrystallises to marble (Morogoro, Tanga karst).
- **Lacustrine** travertine, tufa (Lake Manyara region, Kilimanjaro foothills).
- **Reefal** (East African coastal — coral limestones, Pemba, Zanzibar, Mafia, Kilwa).

### C. Tanzania / East Africa relevance
Limestone is **the single largest tonnage industrial mineral in Tanzania**, anchored by four integrated cement plants:

| Plant | Owner | Capacity | Location |
|---|---|---|---|
| Wazo Hill (TPCC/Twiga) | Heidelberg Materials | ~2.0 Mt/yr cement, 3 dry kilns | Dar es Salaam |
| Tanga Cement (Simba) | AfriSam → recent local consolidation; Amsons-linked expansion | 1.3 Mt/yr; new 5,000 tpd clinker line in EPC with Sinoma (signed Dec 2025) | Tanga |
| Mbeya Cement | Amsons Group (acquired from LafargeHolcim) | Expanding from 1,000 → 5,000 tpd clinker (USD 130m) | Mbeya |
| Dangote Tanzania (Mtwara) | Dangote Cement | 3.0 Mt/yr design | Mtwara |
| Fortune White Cement | Local | New plant inaugurated Pwani region | Pwani |

Additional non-cement use cases: agricultural lime (acid-soil correction), lime kiln burnt-lime (CaO) for sugar refining at Kilombero/Kagera/TPC, steel flux, paper, glass, and aggregate.

### D. Exploration & determination
- Strip-and-trench mapping on outcropping plateaus; **borehole density 50–100 m grid** is typical for a 30-year quarry plan.
- pXRF screens for SiO₂, Fe₂O₃, MgO; final cement-grade decision needs **XRF whole-rock + chemical CaCO₃ titration + LOI**.
- Cement-grade limestone targets:

| Parameter | Cement spec |
|---|---|
| CaCO₃ | ≥ 75–78% (higher better) |
| MgO | < 5% (excessive Mg → magnesia expansion / unsoundness) |
| SiO₂ | < 14% (raw mix adjusted with clay/shale) |
| Al₂O₃ | 1–3% |
| Fe₂O₃ | 0.5–3% |
| Alkalis (Na₂O + K₂O) | < 0.6% for low-alkali cement (ASR-sensitive aggregate) |
| Cl⁻ | < 0.015% (corrosion of rebar) |
| SO₃ in raw | < 1.5% |
| Free silica (quartz grains) | minimise — increases kiln energy |

Aggregate-grade stone has its own tests: **LA (Los Angeles) abrasion < 35%, Aggregate Crushing Value (ACV) < 30%, water absorption < 2%**.

### E. Mining methods
Bench drilling (ANFO blasting), primary impactor or jaw, dump-truck haul to crusher. Selective mining of low-Mg, low-SiO₂ pockets via grade-control mapping. **Marl/clay corrective quarry** typically co-located within 5–10 km.

### F. Beneficiation & processing — cement (the canonical flow)
1. Quarry → primary jaw / impactor → screen.
2. **Pre-blend stockpile** for chemical uniformity (covered storage in TZ to reduce moisture variation).
3. Raw mill (vertical roller mill) grinds limestone + clay/shale + Fe-corrective (laterite/iron ore) + SiO₂-corrective (silica sand) → kiln feed at fineness 12–15% R-90 µm.
4. **Pre-heater tower (4–6 stage cyclone)** preheats kiln feed to 800–900 °C.
5. **Rotary kiln @ 1450 °C** — calcines CaCO₃ → CaO + CO₂, then forms clinker minerals via Bogue phase formation:
   - C₃S (alite, 50–70%) — early strength
   - C₂S (belite, 15–30%) — long-term strength
   - C₃A (aluminate, 5–10%) — set time
   - C₄AF (ferrite, 5–15%) — colour, sulphate response
6. Air-quench cooler → clinker silo.
7. **Cement mill (ball mill or VRM)** grinds clinker + 5% gypsum + optional pozzolana/fly-ash/limestone-filler → final cement (CEM I, II, IV per EN 197-1).

### G. Final-product specifications
| Product | Spec |
|---|---|
| CEM I 42.5N (OPC) | Compressive strength ≥ 42.5 MPa @ 28 d; clinker ≥ 95% |
| CEM I 52.5N | ≥ 52.5 MPa @ 28 d; higher C₃S, finer grind |
| CEM II/A-P (pozzolanic) | 6–20% pozzolana / fly-ash |
| CEM II/B-P | 21–35% pozzolana |
| CEM IV/B | 36–55% pozzolana — popular in coastal/Mbeya markets |
| Hydrated lime | Ca(OH)₂ ≥ 90%, fineness 90% < 90 µm |
| Burnt lime / quicklime | CaO ≥ 90%, reactivity ≥ 30 °C/2 min (slaking test) |

### H. Movement, packaging, hazard
Bulk pneumatic tanker (cement); 50-kg bags (retail); 1-t bulk bags. Clinker exported by bulk vessel from Dar / Mtwara / Tanga. **Hazard: cement (CaO/Ca(OH)₂) is caustic — pH 12–13 — causes chemical burns; respirable crystalline silica risk during quarrying and grinding (silicosis).**

### I. Pricing & markets
- Aggregate stone ex-quarry TZ: USD 5–10/t.
- Cement-grade limestone (transferred internally): USD 8–15/t notional.
- Bulk cement EAC retail: **USD 90–120/t (USD 4–6/50-kg bag at the till)**.
- Clinker export FOB East African coast: USD 45–70/t.
- USGS reports US lime average value ~USD 175–195/t in 2024 (calcined product, not raw stone).

### J. EHS
- **Silicosis** from quartz-bearing limestones during drilling.
- **Caustic exposure** — CaO + skin moisture → Ca(OH)₂; eye burns are the highest-severity injury at cement plants.
- **NOₓ / SO₂ / CO₂** kiln emissions; Tanzania cement plants now use SO₂ scrubbers + bag-house dust capture.
- **Quarry-wall geotech** failures.

### K. Decision triggers Boji should encode
- "If CaCO₃ ≥ 75% AND MgO < 5% AND SiO₂ < 14%: cement-grade — value 3–5× aggregate."
- "If MgO 5–15%: dolomitic limestone — flux + dimension stone lane, not cement raw mix."
- "If CaCO₃ 50–70%: agricultural lime + aggregate lane only."
- "Coastal coral limestone (Pemba/Mafia/Kilwa): high SO₃ + Cl⁻ — block from cement; suitable as aggregate or burnt lime if chloride < 0.05% post-wash."
- "Aggregate destined for ready-mix in Dar must pass LA < 35% AND ACV < 30% — push to Boji-network testing lab before contract."

- USGS MCS 2025 — Lime: https://pubs.usgs.gov/periodicals/mcs2025/mcs2025-lime.pdf

## 3. Salt (NaCl)

### A. Identity
| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Formula | NaCl (halite); rock salt = mineral form |
| Mohs hardness | 2.5 |
| Density | 2.17 g/cm³ |
| Crystal habit | Cubic; hopper crystals from rapid evaporation |
| Solubility | 360 g/L cold water |
| Associates | Gypsum, anhydrite, sylvite (KCl), polyhalite, carnallite |

### B. Geology of formation
Pure evaporite. Three styles relevant to TZ:
1. **Brine spring (Uvinza-type)**: ancient evaporite at depth dissolved by groundwater; brine rises along faults; surface or shallow-well capture.
2. **Solar saltern (Bagamoyo-coastal type)**: sea-water concentrated through pans.
3. **Bedded rock salt** (Wieliczka, Kansas, Texas style): not yet commercially exploited in TZ; potential exists in Permian–Karoo evaporite basins.

### C. Tanzania / East Africa relevance
| Operation | Type | Region |
|---|---|---|
| Uvinza Salt Works (Nyanza Salt Mines) | Brine-spring + solar evaporation along Malagarasi River; >30 saline springs; ~34,000 t/yr | Kigoma |
| Bagamoyo coastal solar salterns | Solar pans; large / medium / small producers | Pwani |
| Lindi, Mtwara, Tanga coastal pans | Solar | Coastal |
| Singida, Dodoma, Manyara inland | Small-scale brine | Central plateau |
| Maere | Inland brine | — |

Tanzania is **largely self-sufficient and a net exporter to land-locked neighbours** (Burundi, DRC, Rwanda). Inland Uvinza is strategically positioned to serve Eastern DRC, where Boji should anticipate a regional FX-denominated trade.

### D. Exploration & determination
- Brine conductivity / specific gravity (Baumé hydrometer); >24 °Bé brine is harvestable.
- Chloride titration (Mohr method, AgNO₃).
- Trace elements: Mg²⁺ (bitterness/hygroscopicity), Ca²⁺, SO₄²⁻, K⁺ (low-Na adulterant).
- Iodisation verification: spot test (rapid kit) gives qualitative; iodometric titration gives mg KIO₃/kg.

### E. Mining methods
- **Solar evaporation pans** — gravity-fed sea-water or pumped brine, multi-stage pans, crystallisers at final density.
- **Brine extraction + pan evaporation** (Uvinza).
- **Solution mining** (not used in TZ but globally common): inject hot water into rock-salt bed, recover brine, evaporate in vacuum pans.
- **Conventional underground room-and-pillar** (Wieliczka, Kansas) — not in TZ.

### F. Beneficiation & processing
- Brine → settle / clarify → graduated evaporator pans → harvest crystals → wash → centrifuge → dry → screen → iodise.
- **Iodisation:** dose KIO₃ at 40–80 mg/kg using knapsack sprayer or in-line dosing during drying. Bagamoyo studies achieved 60.9 ± 7.4 ppm (within WHO range) using improved knapsack-spray methods; older mixing methods averaged only 10.6 ppm.
- Anti-caking agent (sodium ferrocyanide, calcium silicate) for free-flowing salt.
- Food-grade upgrade: re-crystallisation; vacuum pan refining.

### G. Final-product specifications
| Grade | Spec |
|---|---|
| Food-grade iodised | NaCl ≥ 97%, KIO₃ 30–80 ppm, moisture < 0.5%, no visible insolubles |
| Coarse rock / industrial | NaCl ≥ 95% |
| Refined vacuum | NaCl ≥ 99.5%, Mg²⁺ < 0.05% |
| Cattle salt blocks | NaCl ≥ 96%, trace minerals added |
| De-icing / road salt | NaCl ≥ 92%, coarse |

### H. Movement, packaging, hazard
500 g – 1 kg consumer packs; 25 kg / 50 kg bags trade; bulk truck inland. Hygroscopic — needs PE-lined bags + dry warehouse. **Hazard nil to handler**; high concentrations corrode steel (storage/handling design implication).

### I. Pricing & markets
- Crude solar salt FOB TZ: **USD 25–60/t**.
- Iodised retail in TZ: TZS 600–1,000/kg consumer.
- Refined food-grade export: USD 80–150/t.
- US bulk solar / rock salt: USD 50–80/t mine value; PVD evaporated USD 200–280/t.

### J. EHS
Pan workers face UV, dehydration, foot abrasions from salt crystals (footwear discipline). Mg-bittern disposal must avoid coastal mangrove damage. No respiratory hazard.

### K. Decision triggers Boji should encode
- **"Uniodised salt for human consumption is illegal in Tanzania."** Any cooperative selling table salt must produce TBS-compliant iodised product (KIO₃ 30–80 ppm). Refuse to onboard non-compliant inventory.
- "If brine SG < 1.20 (≈ 24 °Bé) at harvesting pan: re-route to higher concentrator; current pan yield is sub-economic."
- "If destination is DRC overland: confirm phytosanitary + food-import permits; price premium 20–35% justifies compliance cost."
- "Mangrove-loss flag on coastal salt expansion: NEMC EIA mandatory; refuse to recommend new pans into intact mangrove without ESIA."

- Uvinza Salt Works — Wikipedia: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uvinza_Salt_Works
- Sutton & Roberts — Uvinza and Its Salt Industry: https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/Uvinza-and-its-Salt-Industry-Sutton-Roberts/74703d33f03612641d8d1f9b94473ca69d3b4c74

## 4. Soda ash (Na₂CO₃)

### A. Identity
| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Formula | Na₂CO₃ (anhydrous); decahydrate Na₂CO₃·10H₂O = washing soda; trona = Na₃(CO₃)(HCO₃)·2H₂O |
| Mohs hardness | 2.5–3 (trona) |
| Density | 2.53 g/cm³ anhydrous |
| Solubility | Highly soluble; pH ~11 in solution |
| Associates | Nahcolite (NaHCO₃), halite, gaylussite, pirssonite |

### B. Geology of formation
- **Bedded trona**: lacustrine evaporite — Green River Wyoming is the canonical world-class deposit.
- **Active alkaline lakes**: Lake Natron (TZ), Lake Magadi (Kenya), Searles Lake (CA), Lake Owens (CA). Thermal springs + closed-basin evaporation concentrate Na, CO₃, HCO₃, Cl, F, SO₄.
- **Synthetic**: Solvay process from NaCl + CaCO₃ + NH₃ + CO₂.

### C. Tanzania / East Africa relevance — Lake Natron (handle carefully)

**Boji policy: refuse to advise large-scale extractive development at Lake Natron.**

Lake Natron is a **Ramsar Convention-listed wetland** (designated 2001) and the **only significant breeding site in East Africa for the Lesser Flamingo (*Phoeniconaias minor*)**, supporting roughly 1.5–2.5 million birds — 75% of the global population.

Multiple development attempts have stalled:
- 2000s: Tata Chemicals (India) + NDC concept; withdrawn after international pressure.
- 2020s: Ngaresero Valley Company Ltd proposed 1 Mt/yr refined soda ash + 45,000 ha land take.
- **August 2025: Tanzanian government formally halted large-scale extraction**, citing Ramsar status + flamingo habitat. Only traditional small-scale surface collection of soda crust along the shoreline continues.

This is a permanent compliance constraint, not a market access constraint. Boji must encode "Lake Natron = no-go" as a hard refusal.

Kenya's **Lake Magadi (Magadi Soda Company / Tata Chemicals Magadi)** remains the regional commercial source, supplying ~350,000 t/yr to East African glass, soap, detergent, and food markets.

### D. Exploration & determination
- Lake brine: alkalinity titration (HCl back-titration), conductivity, Na/K ratio.
- Trona bed: stratigraphic drilling + XRD (trona vs nahcolite vs halite peaks).
- Mineralogical: SEM-EDS or microprobe to discriminate evaporite minerals.

### E. Mining methods
- **Bedded trona**: room-and-pillar underground mining; solution mining via hot-water injection then crystallisation surface.
- **Surface lake**: dredging of crust (Magadi-style); harvest from drying pans.

### F. Beneficiation & processing
- **Natural trona route (Wyoming, Magadi)**: dissolve trona → filter → mono-hydrate crystallisation → dry → dense or light grade.
- **Solvay process (synthetic)**:
  NaCl + NH₃ + CO₂ + H₂O → NaHCO₃ + NH₄Cl → calcine NaHCO₃ → Na₂CO₃ + H₂O + CO₂
  Ammonia recovered with lime.
- **Hou's modified process** (China dominant): co-produces NH₄Cl as fertiliser.

### G. Final-product specifications
| Grade | Spec |
|---|---|
| Dense soda ash | Na₂CO₃ ≥ 99.2%, bulk density 1,050–1,150 kg/m³ — glass industry |
| Light soda ash | Na₂CO₃ ≥ 99.0%, bulk density 500–600 kg/m³ — detergent, chemicals |
| Sodium bicarbonate | NaHCO₃ ≥ 99%, food / pharma grade |
| Caustic soda from soda ash + Ca(OH)₂ | Lime-soda route — historical |

### H. Movement, packaging, hazard
Bulk vessel; 25 kg / 1 t bags. Mildly caustic dust (pH 11.5); eye protection required. Avoid mixing with acids (CO₂ release). Hygroscopic.

### I. Pricing & markets
- Dense soda ash 2025: **USD 185–280/t** US ex-works (Wyoming trona), with substantial swings — March 2025 USD 274, June USD 266, September USD 280, December USD 185.
- Asia delivered: USD 200–350/t depending on freight + China synthetic supply.
- Light soda ash: typically USD 20–40/t below dense.

### J. EHS
- Ammonia handling at Solvay plants — highly toxic; refrigerated storage + leak detection mandatory.
- Dust caustic to mucosa; minor.
- Flamingo + Ramsar — environmental sensitivity at lake sources (see C).

### K. Decision triggers Boji should encode
- **"Lake Natron extraction (any scale beyond traditional surface collection): refuse to advise; cite Ramsar 2001 + Aug-2025 government decision."**
- "Regional soda ash sourcing: route to Tata Magadi (Kenya) or Wyoming imports via Dar es Salaam."
- "Glass plant feed (Kioo Ltd Dar): dense soda ash > 99.2%; reject light grade."
- "Detergent / soap industry: light grade acceptable; price floor USD 200/t CIF Dar."

- BirdLife International — "Controversial mining project in Tanzania's Lake Natron halted" (Aug 2025): https://www.birdlife.org/news/2025/08/22/controversial-mining-project-in-tanzanias-lake-natron-halted/

## 5. Phosphate rock (apatite — Minjingu)

### A. Identity
| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Formula | Ca₅(PO₄)₃(F, Cl, OH) — fluorapatite, chlorapatite, hydroxyapatite |
| Mohs hardness | 5 |
| Density | 3.16–3.22 g/cm³ |
| Crystal habit | Hexagonal prisms (igneous); cryptocrystalline (sedimentary francolite) |
| Associates | Calcite, dolomite, quartz, glauconite, pyrite, organic matter, REEs, uranium |

### B. Geology of formation
- **Sedimentary phosphorite** (Morocco, Western Sahara, Florida, Tunisia, Minjingu): upwelling on continental shelves precipitates carbonate-fluorapatite (francolite).
- **Igneous (carbonatite + alkaline)**: Phalaborwa (S Africa), Kovdor (Russia), Brazil. Tanzania has carbonatite at Panda Hill (niobium-focused, with phosphate).
- **Guano** — bird/bat droppings concentrated by leaching; rare commercial scale.

### C. Tanzania / East Africa relevance — Minjingu

**Minjingu Mines & Fertiliser Ltd** (Manyara region, near Lake Manyara) is **East Africa's only operating phosphate mine**. Origin is unusual — partially fossilised guano on a Plio-Pleistocene phosphatic-sediment cap. Significant features:

- P₂O₅ grade: **20–35% raw**, upgradable to 30% by dry screening; products marketed at **29% P₂O₅ as Minjingu Organic Hyperphosphate**.
- Expansion plan: **100,000 → 300,000 t/yr by 2024/25 cycle**; granulator plant adds 30,000 t/yr blended-fertiliser capacity.
- Contains naturally elevated REEs + uranium — noted in recent literature; below regulatory thresholds for fertiliser use but flagged for future stewardship.
- Direct-application acidic-soil suitability (high citric-soluble P₂O₅) is unusual and a regional advantage — works on Tanzanian, Kenyan, Ugandan, Rwandan acid soils without acidulation.

Customers / blender partners: Yara, Mbeya Fertiliser, ETG Inputs, regional cooperatives.

### D. Exploration & determination
- Mapping of phosphatic horizons; auger and core drilling.
- pXRF for P (LOD ~0.05% P₂O₅) — quick screen; **definitive: gravimetric quinolinium phosphomolybdate or vanadomolybdate spectrophotometry**.
- BPL = bone phosphate of lime = P₂O₅ × 2.185 — legacy spec common in trade.
- Trace U, Cd, REE assays for export markets (EU REACH).

### E. Mining methods
Open-pit, ripper-and-dozer in soft phosphorite; shallow blasting for harder caps. Selective benching to manage grade.

### F. Beneficiation & processing
- **Minjingu route**: crush → screen → dry → bag as ground rock phosphate, OR granulate (with binder + limestone filler) to coated prills.
- **Single super-phosphate (SSP)**: rock + H₂SO₄ → 16–20% P₂O₅ water-soluble. Energy-light; ideal where sulphur soils.
- **Triple super-phosphate (TSP)**: rock + H₃PO₄ → 44–48% P₂O₅.
- **DAP/MAP**: rock → wet-process phosphoric acid → ammoniate with NH₃ → di-ammonium / mono-ammonium phosphate. Capital-intensive; not in TZ today.

### G. Final-product specifications
| Product | Spec |
|---|---|
| Direct-application rock phosphate (Minjingu) | 28–30% P₂O₅ total, 8–12% citric-soluble |
| Granulated rock phosphate | 28% P₂O₅, granule size 2–4 mm, dust < 2% |
| SSP | 16–20% P₂O₅, water-soluble ≥ 90% |
| TSP | 44–48% P₂O₅ |
| DAP | 18% N + 46% P₂O₅ |
| Compound blends | e.g. NPK 10-18-24 (with CAN) — Minjingu blends |

### H. Movement, packaging, hazard
50 kg bags; 1 t big bags; bulk truck/rail. **Hazard: dust nuisance; F⁻ leaching from SSP processing (HF effluent control); uranium content low but should be reported.**

### I. Pricing & markets
- World Bank Pink Sheet phosphate rock (Morocco BPL 70%): USD 230–340/t in 2024–2025 cycle.
- Minjingu localised: typically **USD 180–280/t bagged direct-application**, with substantial cooperative discounts.
- Pricing sensitive to NPK retail caps and government subsidy schemes (Tanzania Fertiliser Bulk Procurement System has direct impact).

### J. EHS
- Dust + fluoride from SSP plants.
- Tailings: phosphogypsum (CaSO₄ + radium traces) — careful pond design + cover.
- REE/U enrichment — monitor in product labelling for export.

### K. Decision triggers Boji should encode

- "Acid soil flag (Mbeya, Rukwa, Iringa, Kagera): direct-application rock outperforms granular NPK on first-cycle P-availability."

## 6. Kaolin (Al₂Si₂O₅(OH)₄)

### A. Identity
| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Formula | Al₂Si₂O₅(OH)₄ (kaolinite, dickite, nacrite, halloysite polymorphs) |
| Mohs hardness | 2–2.5 |
| Density | 2.6 g/cm³ |
| Crystal habit | Plate-like 1 µm hexagonal flakes |
| Associates | Quartz, K-feldspar (parent), micas, Fe-oxides (TiO₂ in some) |

### B. Geology of formation
- **Primary (residual)**: in situ weathering of granite / feldspathic rock — Cornwall (UK), Pugu Hills (TZ).
- **Secondary (sedimentary)**: reworked kaolin transported and re-deposited — Georgia USA Tertiary belt (world-class), Brazil Amazon.
- **Hydrothermal**: minor; near-surface acid-sulphate alteration.

### C. Tanzania / East Africa relevance
**Pugu Hills, Kisarawe District, Pwani Region**, ~30 km west of Dar es Salaam. Reserves are considered among the largest globally, but under-developed. STAMICO geological reports note high brightness suitable for paper coating. Iron-oxide content (~1.43%) is the principal grade concern for whiteware translucency — beneficiation (magnetic + flotation) can correct.

Other TZ kaolin: Kalalani (Tanga), Itigi (Singida) lesser-developed.

Comparative world peers: **Cornwall (UK — Imerys English China Clays)**, **Georgia USA (Imerys, KaMin, BASF — Tertiary Buffalo Creek belt)**, **Brazil Amazon (CADAM, IRCC)**, **Czech Republic (Sedlec)**.

### D. Exploration & determination
- Auger and shallow core; clay typically < 30 m deep.
- pXRF / lab XRF for Al, Si, Fe, Ti, K, Na (kaolin signature: high Al + Si, low K + Na = feldspar gone).
- **ISO brightness** (R457 reflectance) — paper grade ≥ 85 ISO, premium ≥ 88; coating grade ≥ 90 ISO after beneficiation.
- Particle size: 80% < 2 µm for coating; Brookfield viscosity at 70% solids.
- Hegman / Hercules dispersion for paint.

### E. Mining methods
Open-pit; minimal blasting; clay extracted with backhoe / dragline; slurried at face for hydraulic transport in large operations.

### F. Beneficiation & processing
1. Crude kaolin slurried with water + dispersant (sodium hexametaphosphate).
2. **Centrifuge / hydrocyclone classification** to separate ≤ 2 µm coating fraction from > 2 µm filler fraction.
3. **High-intensity wet magnetic separation (HIWMS, 20,000 gauss)** removes Fe oxides → brightness gain 3–8 ISO points.
4. **Reductive bleaching** (sodium dithionite Na₂S₂O₄, pH 3) reduces Fe³⁺ → Fe²⁺ soluble → wash out.
5. **Flotation** (oleate / amine collectors) for residual TiO₂ stain.
6. Filter press → spray dry or rotary dry → pulverise.
7. **Calcination** at 650 °C (meta-kaolin, dehydroxylated) or 1000 °C (fully calcined for ultra-white paper / fibreglass / paint).

### G. Final-product specifications
| Grade | Spec |
|---|---|
| Paper filler | ISO 80–85, 60% < 2 µm |
| Paper coating | ISO 87–90, 80% < 2 µm, Brookfield < 500 cP @ 70% solids |
| Calcined kaolin (paper opacifier) | ISO 90+, 1.5 µm median |
| Ceramic (sanitary ware, tile) | Brightness less critical; rheology key |
| Paint extender | Oil absorption 30–50 g/100 g; brightness 85+ |
| Refractory | Al₂O₃ ≥ 38%, Fe₂O₃ < 1.5% |
| Fibreglass / catalyst | Calcined, high purity |

### H. Movement, packaging, hazard
Bulk powder (pneumatic tanker), 1 t big bags, 25 kg bags; high-end slurry shipped in lined ISO tanks. Nuisance dust only. **Major hazard absent.**

### I. Pricing & markets
- Filler-grade kaolin FOB: **USD 120–200/t**.
- Paper coating grade: **USD 200–400/t**.
- Premium coating slurry: USD 250–380/t (bulk contracts).
- **Calcined kaolin: USD 300–800/t; specialty ultra-white > USD 1,500/t.**

### J. EHS
Crystalline silica content in some kaolins (Pugu has quartz inter-bedded) — respirable dust risk in unbeneficiated product. Bleaching effluent (sodium dithionite oxidised) requires neutralisation.

### K. Decision triggers Boji should encode
- "Pugu kaolin: pre-test ISO brightness AND Fe₂O₃. If Fe₂O₃ > 1.5%: route to sanitary-ware / refractory lane (not paper)."
- "If Fe₂O₃ < 1% AND brightness > 85 ISO post-beneficiation: paper-coating lane — value 3× filler."
- "Calcination CAPEX (rotary calciner): justify only with off-take agreement; 800 °C kiln + 1 µm milling is non-trivial."
- "Domestic ceramics demand (Goodwill, Keramika, sanitary-ware imports): unmet — local Pugu offtake play is undervalued."

## 7. Mica group

### A. Identity
| Species | Formula | Hardness | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Muscovite** | KAl₂(AlSi₃O₁₀)(OH)₂ | 2.0–2.5 | Electronics, capacitors, pearlescent pigments |
| **Phlogopite** | KMg₃(AlSi₃O₁₀)(F,OH)₂ | 2.5–3.0 | High-T electrical insulation (stable to 900 °C) |
| **Biotite** | K(Mg,Fe)₃(AlSi₃O₁₀)(OH)₂ | 2.5–3.0 | Limited commercial; filler |
| **Lepidolite (Li-mica)** | K(Li,Al)₃(AlSi₃O₁₀)(OH,F)₂ | 2.5–3.0 | **Lithium source** — see ferro-alloys / battery minerals workstream |

All sheet silicates with perfect basal cleavage; pseudo-hexagonal monoclinic.

### B. Geology of formation
- **Granitic pegmatites** (muscovite, lepidolite) — TZ Morogoro–Tanga–Iringa pegmatite belts.
- **Metamorphic schists & gneisses** (biotite, muscovite) — Mozambique Belt across central TZ.
- **Carbonatites + ultramafics** (phlogopite) — Tanzania has phlogopite in some kimberlitic / carbonatite settings.

### C. Tanzania / East Africa relevance
- **Morogoro Region — Lukwengule (Uluguru Mountains)**: muscovite pegmatites worked since early 20th century (Otto Schwartz era, 1900s).
- **Iringa Region — Udzungwa escarpment**: muscovite-rich pegmatites and biotite-rich aggregate (problematic for concrete — mica weakens cement bond).
- **Tanga Region**: pegmatite belt with mica + tourmaline + beryl.
- Madagascar (across the channel) is the regional dominant producer of high-quality phlogopite.
- India and China dominate global ground-mica supply.

### D. Exploration & determination
- Field: cleavage, lustre, colour (muscovite colourless–silver, phlogopite brown, biotite black).
- Visual sheet sizing (book / cobbing standards — 1×1 inch, 2×2 inch, etc., for sheet mica).
- XRD distinguishes polytypes (2M₁, 1M, 3T).
- ICP-MS for trace Li, Be, Rb in lepidolite.

### E. Mining methods
- **Sheet mica**: artisanal hand-cobbing from pegmatite outcrops; pit + adit mining. Capital-light, labour-intense.
- **Scrap / flake mica**: by-product of feldspar / spodumene / Li mining or stand-alone weathered pegmatite open-pit.
- **Ground mica**: from scrap, milled in roller / hammer mills.

### F. Beneficiation & processing
- Hand selection ("cobbing") for sheet grade.
- **Wet grinding** preserves flake aspect ratio (cosmetic and pearl pigment grade).
- **Dry grinding** for paint, joint compound, well-drilling lost-circulation material.
- Flotation (amine collectors, pH 2–3) for fine flake from pegmatite tailings.
- Magnetic separation to remove biotite (Fe) for premium muscovite.

### G. Final-product specifications
| Grade | Spec |
|---|---|
| Sheet muscovite (electronic) | Block area > 4 in², splittable, dielectric strength > 4 kV/mil |
| Wet-ground mica (cosmetic) | High aspect ratio, ISO brightness 70–80, particle 25–150 µm |
| Dry-ground mica | Lower brightness, used in joint compound (50–60% < 200 mesh) |
| Phlogopite high-T | Stable to 900 °C, dielectric strength comparable to muscovite |

### H. Movement, packaging, hazard
25 kg bags / 1 t bulk bags. Sheet mica in custom wood crates. **Hazard: silicosis if pegmatite host contains free quartz; child-labour and human-rights flag in artisanal supply chains (India Jharkhand scandal applies).**

### I. Pricing & markets
- Ground mica industrial: **USD 200–500/t**.
- Wet-ground cosmetic: **USD 500–1,500/t**.
- Sheet mica (high-grade, large-format): up to USD 5,000–20,000/t depending on sheet size and clarity.

### J. EHS
- Pegmatite quarry silicosis.
- Pigment-grade calcining (for synthetic pearl effects) — minor SO₂ if Fe-mica present.
- Human rights: artisanal mica supply chain is a known child-labour risk (mostly India). Boji should require chain-of-custody attestation before recommending offtake.

### K. Decision triggers Boji should encode
- "Lukwengule / Uluguru pegmatite: if sheet > 1×1 inch and biotite < 5%: electronic-sheet route — value 100× ground."
- "Mica in concrete aggregate > 0.5%: REJECT as structural aggregate; downgrade to base / sub-base."
- "Artisanal supply: require chain-of-custody attestation; refuse cosmetic export route without traceability."
- "Lepidolite suspected: route to Boji battery-minerals workstream — Li-mica is a critical-minerals asset, not a sheet-mica asset."

## 8. Talc (Mg₃Si₄O₁₀(OH)₂)

### A. Identity
| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Formula | Mg₃Si₄O₁₀(OH)₂ — hydrated magnesium silicate |
| Mohs hardness | 1 (softest mineral; defines Mohs floor) |
| Density | 2.7–2.8 g/cm³ |
| Crystal habit | Platy, foliated; massive talc = soapstone |
| Associates | Chlorite, dolomite, magnesite, **tremolite-actinolite (asbestos risk)**, serpentine |

### B. Geology of formation
- **Metamorphism of ultramafic rocks** (dunite, serpentinite + SiO₂ + H₂O → talc): NY-Vermont talc belt (US), Liaoning (China), Trimouns (France).
- **Metamorphism of dolomite + silica**: clean, low-Fe, low-asbestos talc — Italian Val Chisone, Montana steatite.
- **Hydrothermal alteration**: smaller deposits.

### C. Tanzania / East Africa relevance
Limited commercial extraction. Potential in ultramafic belts of the Mozambique Belt (Iringa, Mara). Madagascar produces talc commercially. Boji can encode talc as a watch-list industrial mineral with no current active TZ mine of scale.

### D. Exploration & determination
- Talc soapstone in outcrop — soapy/slippery feel, very soft.
- XRD: talc peak at 9.35 Å (001).
- **Mandatory asbestos screening (PLM + TEM)** — historic Vermont talc contamination scandal (J&J baby powder litigation) makes any new talc deposit a regulated entry.
- Brightness, particle size, oil absorption per ISO/ASTM.

### E. Mining methods
Open-pit selective; underground for premium low-asbestos deposits. Hand-sorting at the face.

### F. Beneficiation & processing
- Hand sorting / optical sorting at conveyor.
- Crush + dry grind (Raymond, ACM, jet mill) for D₅₀ down to 2–10 µm.
- Flotation (fatty-acid collectors) to remove carbonate/Fe gangue.
- Magnetic separation for residual Fe-oxide.

### G. Final-product specifications
| Grade | Spec |
|---|---|
| Cosmetic (USP) | Mg₃Si₄O₁₀(OH)₂ ≥ 95%, asbestos NOT DETECTED (TEM), Pb < 5 ppm |
| Paint | Brightness 85+, D₅₀ 5–10 µm |
| Plastics (polypropylene reinforcement) | High aspect ratio, low moisture |
| Ceramics | Specific MgO ratio |
| Paper filler | Brightness 80+, D₅₀ < 5 µm |

### H. Movement, packaging, hazard
25 kg / 1 t bags. **Hazard: asbestos contamination is the defining industry risk** — never permit cosmetic-grade sales without per-lot asbestos testing.

### I. Pricing & markets
- Industrial talc: **USD 200–400/t**.
- Cosmetic / pharma grade: **USD 500–1,500/t**.
- USGS 2025 reports US talc industry value ~USD 170 million; super-fine talc market USD 425m (2024) → USD 563m projected (2032).

### J. EHS
- Asbestos contamination — silicate sister minerals tremolite, anthophyllite, actinolite occur in same metamorphic settings.
- J&J litigation backdrop: insurance premiums for talc producers rose 40–60% in 2025; aggregate limits tightened.

### K. Decision triggers Boji should encode
- **"New talc prospect: mandatory PLM + TEM asbestos screening before any beneficiation CAPEX. No exceptions."**
- "Carbonate-hosted (dolomite + silica metamorphic) talc has lowest asbestos risk; ultramafic-hosted talc is highest risk."
- "Cosmetic-grade sale: per-lot certificate of analysis with TEM is mandatory for export."
- "Soapstone artisanal carving (Tanzania has cultural soapstone use): sale to tourists low-risk; do not extrapolate to talc industrial path."

## 9. Fluorspar (CaF₂)

### A. Identity
| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Formula | CaF₂ |
| Mohs hardness | 4 |
| Density | 3.18 g/cm³ |
| Crystal habit | Cubic; octahedral cleavage; fluorescent (UV) |
| Associates | Quartz, barite, calcite, galena, sphalerite, sulphides |

### B. Geology of formation
- **Mississippi Valley Type (MVT)**: Illinois–Kentucky district (USA); low-T basinal-brine.
- **Hydrothermal vein** — South Africa (Vergenoeg, Witkop), Mexico (Las Cuevas), Mongolia, China.
- **Carbonatite-hosted**: rare; e.g., Vergenoeg (S Africa) is a magnetite-fluorite carbonatite.

### C. Tanzania / East Africa relevance
Minor occurrences in carbonatites (Panda Hill, Mbeya region — Nb-focused), and minor hydrothermal showings. No commercial fluorspar production in Tanzania at scale. **South Africa is the regional dominant supplier.**

### D. Exploration & determination
- UV-fluorescence in field (blue / purple under SW UV is diagnostic).
- pXRF for Ca + F (F at LOD of pXRF; chemistry preferred).
- **Pyrohydrolysis F⁻ chemistry, ISE F-electrode** for grade.
- Flotation amenability testing on representative composite.

### E. Mining methods
Open-pit for shallow MVT and carbonatite-hosted; underground vein mining (Mexico, China).

### F. Beneficiation & processing
- Crush → grind to flotation feed (P₈₀ ~100 µm).
- **Froth flotation with fatty-acid collectors (oleate), depressants (sodium silicate for silica gangue, quebracho for calcite), at pH 8–9.5.**
- Multi-stage cleaning to acid-grade.
- Filter, dry, screen.

### G. Final-product specifications
| Grade | Spec |
|---|---|
| Acid grade (acidspar) | CaF₂ ≥ 97%, SiO₂ ≤ 1%, CaCO₃ ≤ 1.5%, S < 0.1% — feed for HF acid manufacture |
| Ceramic grade | CaF₂ ≥ 95%, Fe₂O₃ < 0.4%, low Pb |
| Metallurgical grade (metspar) | CaF₂ ≥ 80% — steel flux |

### H. Movement, packaging, hazard
Bulk vessel (acidspar), 1 t bags (metspar). **Hazard: fluoride leaching → HF formation in acid environments; HF is highly toxic, causes deep tissue / bone necrosis.** Workers handling acidspar / HF plant: calcium-gluconate first-aid mandatory.

### I. Pricing & markets
- **Acid-grade fluorspar (97% CaF₂) 2025: USD 401–484/t US/China; up to USD 538/t Brazil/Japan delivered.** (Fastmarkets CIF Rotterdam reference: USD 450–500/t through 2025).
- Met-grade: USD 220–340/t.
- Ceramic grade: USD 350–550/t.

### J. EHS
- HF exposure at downstream consumers (refrigerants, semiconductors, aluminium electrolysis cells).
- Fluoride emissions to air/water at smelters and HF plants — strict permit.

### K. Decision triggers Boji should encode
- "Panda Hill carbonatite: fluorspar is a co-product flag — primary value is Nb; routing to acidspar flotation requires dedicated circuit + offtake."
- "Suspected hydrothermal vein in TZ: stage-gate feasibility before any CAPEX — South Africa freight + global oversupply make small TZ deposits marginal unless acid-grade premium captured."
- "Critical-minerals classification: fluorspar is on US/EU/UK critical-minerals lists — opens DFC / EU strategic finance routes."

## 10. Magnesite (MgCO₃)

### A. Identity
| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Formula | MgCO₃ |
| Mohs hardness | 3.5–4.5 |
| Density | 3.0 g/cm³ |
| Crystal habit | Rhombohedral; cryptocrystalline / massive (sedimentary); macrocrystalline (metamorphic) |
| Associates | Talc, serpentine, chlorite, dolomite, brucite |

### B. Geology of formation
- **Sedimentary lacustrine / playa** (cryptocrystalline): Turkey, Greece, Australia.
- **Metasomatic of ultramafic** (macrocrystalline, "spathic"): China Liaoning (dominant world supply), Russia, Brazil.
- **Hydrothermal vein**: minor.

### C. Tanzania / East Africa relevance
Minor occurrences in ultramafic belts; **not commercially developed**. Boji watchlist only. Madagascar and Kenya (small) supply EAC.

### D. Exploration & determination
- Effervescence in warm dilute HCl (vs limestone in cold HCl — distinguishes).
- XRD (rhombohedral MgCO₃ peak at 2.74 Å).
- Chemistry: MgO + CaO + SiO₂ + Fe₂O₃ majors; ICP for trace.
- Calcining test: weight loss → CO₂ release; product MgO purity.

### E. Mining methods
Open-pit; selective in massive bodies. Hand-sorting to remove serpentine / talc envelope.

### F. Beneficiation & processing
- Crush → hand / optical sort → calcine.
- **Caustic-calcined magnesia (CCM)**: calcine at 700–1000 °C — reactive MgO for environmental, agriculture, animal feed.
- **Dead-burned magnesia (DBM)**: calcine at 1700–2200 °C — refractory MgO, dense, low reactivity. Refractory brick for steel, cement, glass kilns.
- **Fused magnesia (FM)**: electric arc furnace — premium refractories.

### G. Final-product specifications
| Grade | MgO % | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Calcined CCM | 88–95 | Animal feed, water treatment, fertiliser |
| DBM 90 | 90 | Cement-kiln basic brick |
| DBM 95 | 95 | Steel ladle refractory |
| DBM 97 | 97 | EAF refractory |
| Fused magnesia | 97–99 | Premium electrical insulation, ultra-high-T |

### H. Movement, packaging, hazard
Bulk vessel (DBM), 1 t bags. Refractory bricks shipped on pallets. **Hazard low**; high-T calcining is energy-intensive (~3.5 GJ/t DBM).

### I. Pricing & markets
- CCM: USD 250–400/t.
- DBM 90: USD 320–450/t.
- DBM 97: USD 600–900/t.
- Fused magnesia: USD 800–1,500/t.

### J. EHS
Energy-intensive calcining → CO₂ emissions (similar to lime). Worker exposure to hot MgO dust.

### K. Decision triggers Boji should encode
- "TZ magnesite prospect: confirm metamorphic crystalline vs sedimentary cryptocrystalline — process route differs."

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Formula | CaMg(CO₃)₂ |
| Mohs hardness | 3.5–4 |
| Density | 2.85 g/cm³ |
| Crystal habit | Rhombohedral; saddle-shape; massive |
| Associates | Calcite, ankerite, gypsum, halite, sulphides |

### B. Geology of formation
- **Sedimentary dolostone**: replacement of limestone by Mg-rich brines (dolomitisation), or direct precipitation.

### C. Tanzania / East Africa relevance
- Significant dolomitic horizons in coastal Karoo/Jurassic sediments; metamorphic dolomite in Mozambique Belt around Tanga, Morogoro.

### D. Exploration & determination

- Cement / glass / steel routes need chemistry: MgO 18–22% (theoretical for stoichiometric dolomite is 21.9%).

### E. Mining methods
Open-pit quarry; bench drill-and-blast or sawn block for dimension stone.

### F. Beneficiation & processing
Crush + screen for aggregate; calcine for dolomitic lime (CaO·MgO); selective for high-Mg blocks for glass/refractory.

### G. Final-product specifications
| Use | Spec |
|---|---|

| Steel flux | CaO + MgO ≥ 50%, low S |
| Refractory dolomite (basic brick) | MgO ≥ 35%, low SiO₂ |
| Agricultural lime (Mg) | MgO ≥ 15%, fine grind |
| Dimension stone | Aesthetics + strength tests |

### H. Movement, packaging, hazard
Bulk truck / rail; dimension stone palletised slabs. Low hazard.

### I. Pricing & markets
- Aggregate: USD 5–15/t.
- Glass-grade: USD 25–60/t.
- Refractory dolomite: USD 80–180/t.
- Dimension stone (slabs): USD 30–250/m² retail.

### J. EHS
Silicosis if quartz contamination; standard quarry geotech. Otherwise benign.

### K. Decision triggers Boji should encode

## 12. Silica sand / quartz (SiO₂)

### A. Identity
| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Formula | SiO₂ — α-quartz at surface temperatures |
| Mohs hardness | 7 |
| Density | 2.65 g/cm³ |
| Crystal habit | Hexagonal prisms; massive vein quartz; rounded grains in sand |
| Associates | Feldspar, mica, Fe oxides, clay |

### B. Geology of formation
- **Sedimentary**: aeolian and fluvial quartz sand; cemented as sandstone; high-purity from multi-cycle reworking (St. Peter, Ottawa sand).
- **Hydrothermal vein quartz**: high-purity, low-Fe, often Brazilian / Madagascan source for quartz crystal.
- **Pegmatitic quartz**: ultra-high purity for semiconductor.

### C. Tanzania / East Africa relevance
- Coastal sand belts (Pwani, Lindi, Tanga).
- Pugu sandstones (associated with kaolin).
- Lake Victoria shore sands (some used for construction).
- Vein quartz scattered through Lukoma Hills, Geita greenstone.

Current uses: glass at **Kioo Ltd** (Dar); foundry sand; construction; minor frac-sand interest. **No semiconductor-grade purification capability in TZ.**

### D. Exploration & determination
- Visual + petrography (grain shape, angularity, sphericity).
- pXRF for Fe, Ti, Al, K (the four sub-1% impurities that disqualify glass-grade).
- **AFS Grain Fineness Number (GFN)** for foundry sand.
- LECO total sulphur / chlorine for special-glass grades.
- Clay content (AFS test) for foundry binder compatibility.
- Acid-leach tests for Fe removal feasibility.

### E. Mining methods
Open-pit / quarry; dredging of river / coastal sand; hydraulic mining of friable sandstone (Pugu).

### F. Beneficiation & processing
1. Wash + screen.
2. **Attrition scrubbing** removes clay films, breaks Fe-coatings.
3. **Spiral / hydrocyclone concentrator** for size + Fe-mineral pre-separation.
4. **High-intensity magnetic separation** — Fe oxides.
5. **Flotation** (anionic / cationic) — final Fe + Al removal for glass premium.
6. **Acid leaching** (HCl, oxalic acid) — for high-purity ≥ 99.9% SiO₂.
7. **Chlorination + zone refining** — semiconductor grade ≥ 99.999% (5N).

### G. Final-product specifications
| Grade | SiO₂ | Fe₂O₃ | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Construction sand | ≥ 90% | n/a | Concrete |
| Foundry sand | ≥ 95% | < 0.5% | AFS 50–70 GFN |
| Container glass | ≥ 98.5% | < 0.04% | Bottles |
| Flat glass | ≥ 99.0% | < 0.025% | Float glass |
| **Clear glass (low-iron solar)** | **≥ 99.5%** | **< 0.01%** | **Photovoltaic cover glass** |
| Optical / display | ≥ 99.99% | < 0.005% | LCD substrate |
| Semiconductor | ≥ 99.999% (5N–6N) | < 1 ppm | Wafers, fused quartz |

### H. Movement, packaging, hazard
Bulk truck / vessel; 25 kg bags; 1 t bags. **Hazard: respirable crystalline silica (RCS) → silicosis. PPE + dust suppression mandatory.**

### I. Pricing & markets
- Construction sand: USD 8–20/t.
- Foundry sand: USD 50–120/t.
- Glass-grade: USD 60–180/t.
- Solar-glass-grade (low-Fe): USD 200–400/t.
- Frac sand (Northern White / fines): USD 30–80/t.
- **Semiconductor / fused-quartz feedstock: USD 1,500–8,000/t**.

### J. EHS
- Silicosis — single biggest occupational hazard in industrial minerals globally.
- River-dredging environmental damage (sand mining bans in India, parts of Kenya).
- Coastal mangrove + dune ecosystem impact.

### K. Decision triggers Boji should encode
- "Coastal dredging > 100,000 t/yr: NEMC EIA + coastal-management consent mandatory."
- "Glass-grade screening: pXRF Fe < 200 ppm passes first filter; confirm with XRF Fe₂O₃ < 0.04% on washed/attritioned product."

## 13. Bentonite (smectite-rich clay)

### A. Identity
| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Formula | Predominantly Na/Ca-montmorillonite (Al,Mg)₂Si₄O₁₀(OH)₂·nH₂O |
| Mohs hardness | 1–2 |
| Density | 2–3 g/cm³ |
| Crystal habit | Sub-µm platelets; expanding 2:1 layer silicate |
| Associates | Volcanic glass, zeolite, cristobalite, feldspar, gypsum |

### B. Geology of formation
- **Diagenetic alteration of volcanic ash** in marine / lacustrine basins — Wyoming Mowry Shale + Cretaceous bentonites; classic Na-bentonite.
- **Hydrothermal alteration of tuffs** — Sardinia, Greece (Milos), Iran.
- **Ca-bentonite** worldwide more common; Na-bentonite premium and rarer.

### C. Tanzania / East Africa relevance
Occurrences reported in Kilosa, Singida, and Dodoma altered tuffs; **no commercial scale Na-bentonite production**. Boji watchlist with Na-activation potential (Ca → Na by soda-ash exchange).

Kenya has some bentonite resource near Athi River; Ethiopia produces Ca-bentonite.

### D. Exploration & determination
- **Methylene Blue Value (MBV)** — quick screen for smectite content.
- Swelling index in distilled water (Na-bentonite swells 15×; Ca-bentonite ~5×).
- **API 13A drilling-mud testing**: yield (bbl/t — barrels of 15-cP mud per ton; min 90 bbl/t for API-spec).
- XRD: smectite 001 peak at ~12–15 Å, expanding to 17 Å with ethylene glycol — diagnostic.
- CEC (cation exchange capacity) — 80–120 meq/100 g typical for Na-bentonite.

### E. Mining methods
Open-pit; selective benching of high-MBV layers; covered storage (smectites absorb humidity).

### F. Beneficiation & processing
- Sun-dry / kiln-dry.
- **Na-activation** for Ca-bentonite: add soda ash (~3–5% by mass), extrude, age → Ca²⁺ exchanged for Na⁺ → swelling index multiplies. Industry standard for non-Wyoming sources.
- Grind to API 200-mesh (74 µm); 90% passing.
- Acid-activation for bleaching-earth grades.
- Organo-bentonite (quaternary-amine treatment) — paint, drilling-fluid, cosmetics.

### G. Final-product specifications
| Grade | Spec |
|---|---|
| API 13A drilling mud | Yield ≥ 90 bbl/t; YP/PV ≤ 3; PV ≥ 15; ⌀200 mesh ≥ 96% |
| OCMA / European drilling | Yield ≥ 90 bbl/t |
| Foundry green-sand binder | Methylene blue test, working bond strength |
| Pet litter (clumping) | Coarse Na-bentonite; absorption ≥ 250% |
| Civil engineering (slurry walls) | Marsh funnel viscosity, filtrate loss |
| Iron-ore pelletising binder | Smectite content > 80%; CEC > 75 meq/100 g |

### H. Movement, packaging, hazard
Bulk truck (where dry), 25 kg / 1 t bags. **Hazard: crystalline silica (cristobalite) in some volcanic-tuff bentonites — RCS risk; respirator + dust-suppression.**

### I. Pricing & markets
- Untreated Ca-bentonite: **USD 50–120/t**.
- Na-activated / API-grade Wyoming: **USD 120–250/t bulk**, USD 300–500/t bagged.
- Organo-bentonite (specialty): USD 1,500–3,500/t.

### J. EHS
- Cristobalite content can be respirable-silica hazard.
- Soda-ash for activation: caustic dust handling.
- Tailings dewatering — bentonite holds water; large pond footprint.

### K. Decision triggers Boji should encode
- "TZ tuff prospect: pXRF + MBV first; if MBV > 200 mg/100 g: bentonite candidate."
- "Ca-bentonite + accessible soda ash (Kenya Magadi import): Na-activation viable; CAPEX modest."
- "If oilfield drilling-mud market: certify API 13A via TBS or external lab; uncertified material capped at foundry/pet/civil markets."
- "TZ drilling-mud import substitution: currently US/India-imported; local production economics favourable above 10,000 t/yr."

## 14. Pozzolana (volcanic ash, cement extender)

### A. Identity
| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Composition | Glassy volcanic ash / tuff / pumice; reactive SiO₂ + Al₂O₃ |
| Crystallinity | Predominantly amorphous (key to reactivity) |
| Density | 2.2–2.6 g/cm³ (bulk 600–900 kg/m³) |
| Associates | Zeolites, opal, cristobalite, plagioclase, mafic minerals |

### B. Geology of formation
Explosive volcanism deposits ash flows / tuffs. **Reactive (high glass) pozzolana = young geology (Quaternary)** — Tanzanian deposits at Mbeya (Rungwe volcanics) and Kilimanjaro region are textbook.

Older, devitrified volcanic ash loses reactivity → only fillers, not pozzolans.

### C. Tanzania / East Africa relevance
- **Mbeya pozzolana (Rungwe volcanic field)**: actively used by Mbeya Cement; LafargeHolcim R&D has documented use of **"processed and energetically modified pozzolana"** as fly-ash substitute. With Tanzania having no large coal-fired plants, pozzolana is the **primary supplementary cementitious material (SCM)** for the local industry.
- **Kilimanjaro / Arusha (Meru) volcanic ash**: Tanga Cement, Twiga have explored.
- Strong driver for **CEM IV / CEM II-P** market in East Africa (cement that meets sulphate-resistance + lower CO₂ per tonne thanks to clinker reduction).

### D. Exploration & determination
- **Frattini test (EN 196-5)**: 28-day chemical activity; sample fails if [Ca²⁺] curve sits above lime solubility isotherm → not pozzolanic.
- **Strength Activity Index (SAI, ASTM C311)**: 75% of control mortar strength @ 28d for class N natural pozzolan.
- **Chapelle test** — quick lime-fixation indicator.
- **Particle fineness** (Blaine surface area): > 350 m²/kg desired.
- XRD glass-content (amorphous halo at 22–28° 2θ).

### E. Mining methods
Quarry / scoria-cone mining; minimal blasting; loose pyroclastic.

### F. Beneficiation & processing
- Crush + screen.
- Dry to < 1% moisture.
- Optional grinding to Blaine 400+ m²/kg.
- **Energetic modification** (LafargeHolcim Mbeya): high-energy milling alters surface reactivity; an industrial-research-grade route worth monitoring for IP.

### G. Final-product specifications
| Grade | Spec |
|---|---|
| Natural pozzolan class N (ASTM C618) | SiO₂ + Al₂O₃ + Fe₂O₃ ≥ 70%, SO₃ < 4%, LOI < 10% |
| Calcined natural pozzolan | Class N qualifying via SAI ≥ 75% |
| Cement-blend grade | Direct substitution at 10–35% of clinker per CEM II/B-P or IV |

### H. Movement, packaging, hazard
Bulk truck / bulk vessel; 1 t big bags. Low hazard; nuisance dust.

### I. Pricing & markets
- Bulk pozzolana ex-quarry: **USD 5–25/t**.
- Processed / Blaine-controlled: USD 15–40/t.
- Net economic value comes from **clinker displacement at USD 70+/t** — pozzolana's commercial logic is the spread.

### J. EHS
Some volcanic ashes contain respirable cristobalite (Mbeya, parts of Kilimanjaro) — RCS testing important; dust controls in milling.

### K. Decision triggers Boji should encode
- **"Pre-sell test: Frattini + SAI before any pozzolana offtake. Non-reactive material is filler, priced as filler."**
- "Mbeya pozzolana with > 75% SAI: route to Mbeya Cement / Dangote / Twiga as Class N SCM; price = aggregate × 2–3."
- "Cristobalite > 5% in pozzolana: RCS hazard — milling circuit must have local exhaust + medical surveillance."
- "Carbon-credit upside: clinker displacement = direct CO₂ avoidance — quantify per CDM / VCS for emerging carbon markets."

### B. Geology of formation
Lacustrine and marine biogenic accumulation of diatom frustules. Lompoc CA (USA) and Lake Myvatn (Iceland) are textbook lacustrine; California Monterey is marine.

### C. Tanzania / East Africa relevance

Open-pit / shallow strip; ripper or backhoe.

- Air-classifier dry mill.

### G. Final-product specifications

| Calcined | SiO₂ ≥ 88%, < 2% Fe₂O₃ | Beer/wine filtration |
| Flux-calcined | SiO₂ ≥ 88%, low organics | Pharma, sugar refining |

### H. Movement, packaging, hazard
25 kg bags; bulk-pneumatic for filter aids. **Hazard: calcined / flux-calcined diatomite contains cristobalite — Group 1 IARC respirable carcinogen. Respirator + workplace monitoring mandatory.**

### I. Pricing & markets

- East Africa offtake: limited domestic processing — most filter-aid is imported (Imerys, Showa, EaglePicher).

## 16. Vermiculite (hydrated Mg-Al-Fe layer silicate)

### A. Identity
| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Formula | (Mg,Fe,Al)₃(Al,Si)₄O₁₀(OH)₂·4H₂O |
| Mohs hardness | 1.5 |
| Density | 2.4–2.7 unexpanded; 60–160 kg/m³ exfoliated |
| Crystal habit | Pseudo-hexagonal platelets; trioctahedral 2:1 layer silicate |
| Associates | Phlogopite, biotite, talc, serpentine, pyroxene, **tremolite-actinolite asbestos (Libby risk)** |

### B. Geology of formation

### C. Tanzania / East Africa relevance
Minor occurrences in ultramafic complexes; Kenya has historical small production. Boji watchlist.

### D. Exploration & determination
- Field exfoliation test (small flake + flame → expansion 8–20×).

### E. Mining methods
Open-pit ultramafic mining; selective ore-grade extraction.

### F. Beneficiation & processing
- Crush + size + wet beneficiation (flotation, attrition, hydroclassification).

### G. Final-product specifications

| Medium | Insulation, lightweight concrete |
| Fine | Friction linings, gaskets, paint |
| Micronised | Coatings, fire-resistant board |

### H. Movement, packaging, hazard
Exfoliated product is bulky-low-density — shipped in big bags; cube-out before weight-out.

**The hazard is asbestos contamination.** The Libby, Montana scandal — **W. R. Grace operated the mine from 1963 knowing the vermiculite was contaminated with tremolite-actinolite (Libby Amphibole asbestos); 694 deaths from asbestos-related disease 1979–2011, 1 in 10 residents of Libby town (3,200 pop) has asbestos disease; EPA declared first-ever Public Health Emergency 2009; site on NPL Superfund**. ### I. Pricing & markets
- Crude concentrate: USD 350–600/t.
- Exfoliated: USD 800–1,500/t.

### J. EHS
- **Asbestos exposure — fatal, latent (30–40 year latency for mesothelioma).**
- Friable amphibole dust at mine, mill, exfoliation furnace.

### K. Decision triggers Boji should encode
- **"Vermiculite prospect: MANDATORY PLM + TEM asbestos screening on N ≥ 30 samples covering full deposit before ANY extraction recommendation. If positive at any sample: refuse to advise extraction. Cite Libby."**
- "Even if negative: chain-of-custody asbestos certification PER LOT for all sold material — buyer-mandated standard since Libby."

- "Horticultural / consumer-product market liability is uninsurable without per-lot certification."

## 18. Barite (BaSO₄)

### A. Identity
| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Formula | BaSO₄ |
| Mohs hardness | 3–3.5 |
| Density | **4.48 g/cm³** — distinctive heft |
| Crystal habit | Tabular orthorhombic; "rose" rosettes; massive |
| Associates | Fluorite, galena, sphalerite, calcite, quartz, sulphides |

### B. Geology of formation
- **Bedded sedimentary** (China, Nevada): high-grade massive bedded.
- **Vein hydrothermal** (Morocco, UK Pennines): premium API-grade single-mineral veins.
- **Residual / weathered** (India): supergene concentration over carbonate host.
- **Mississippi Valley Type**: associated with Pb–Zn ± fluorite.

### C. Tanzania / East Africa relevance
Minor occurrences (Mbeya, Mara, around Lake Victoria greenstones); **no commercial production**. Imported (China, Morocco, India) for any drilling-fluid use in TZ — and TZ has limited oil/gas drilling demand. Mozambique offshore gas (Rovuma) is the regional drilling-fluid market.

### D. Exploration & determination
- Field: heft test (extreme density for non-metallic mineral).
- pXRF: Ba is detectable but better via XRF or wet chemistry.
- **API SG ≥ 4.20** is the key oilfield specification.
- Heavy-metals chemistry: Pb < 250 ppm, Cd < 5 ppm, Hg < 1 ppm (API 13A).

### E. Mining methods
Open-pit for bedded; underground for vein. Hand-sorting common at small-scale.

### F. Beneficiation & processing
- Crush + jig / spiral / heavy-media for gravity upgrade.
- Flotation for fine-grained or mixed sulphide ores.
- Wet-grinding to API mesh.
- Drying + bagging.

### G. Final-product specifications
| Grade | Spec |
|---|---|
| **API 13A drilling** | SG ≥ 4.20, water-soluble alkaline earth metals (as Ca) < 250 mg/kg, water-soluble carbonates < 0.1% |
| Chemical (BaSO₄ pigment / glass) | SG 4.10–4.20, BaSO₄ ≥ 92% |
| Industrial filler | SG 4.10–4.20, white, low Fe |
| Radiation shielding | High SG concrete aggregate |

### H. Movement, packaging, hazard
Bulk vessel for drilling barite (FOB China is benchmark). **Hazard: barium is toxic if soluble (BaCl₂); BaSO₄ is insoluble and inert. Respirable barite dust is a controlled occupational exposure (NIOSH REL).**

### I. Pricing & markets
- API drilling barite 2025: **USD 160–200/t US, USD 200–375/t global delivered**.
- Industrial / paint / filler: USD 120–250/t.
- USGS critical mineral list since 2018.

### J. EHS
Soluble barium compounds toxic; BaSO₄ inert. NORM (radium co-precipitation) possible at some deposits — screening recommended.

### K. Decision triggers Boji should encode
- "TZ barite: only viable if Rovuma / EACOP drilling demand materialises AND deposit is API-spec achievable at < 100,000 t mine."
- "Imported barite remains cheaper for current TZ demand — entry strategy is geological resource confirmation, not near-term mine build."
- "If SG < 4.10: not API; downgrade to filler / pigment lane."

## 20. Cross-cutting decision rules for Boji's industrial-minerals reasoner

These are encoded as standing rules in the Boji policy layer.

1. **No asbestos = no shortcut.** Vermiculite + talc require PLM + TEM screening per lot. Talc cosmetic export without TEM cert: refuse.
2. **Ramsar / RAMSAR-equivalent designated wetland**: refuse to advise extractive development. Specifically Lake Natron, Lake Manyara, Lake Eyasi alkaline lakes are ecologically sensitive.
3. **Iodised salt only** for human consumption (TZ law, codex). KIO₃ 30–80 ppm.
4. **Cement-grade limestone**: CaCO₃ ≥ 75%, MgO < 5%, SiO₂ < 14%; if any threshold fails, downgrade.
5. **Frattini + SAI before any pozzolana offtake** — non-reactive ash is filler.
6. **API 13A certification** for any drilling-mud bentonite or barite product. Uncertified material routes to alternate markets only.
7. **Critical-minerals overlap**: fluorspar, lepidolite (lithium), graphite (separate workstream), niobium, REEs — opens DFC / EXIM / EU CRMA strategic-finance routes.
8. **Carbon-credit overlay**: pozzolana + calcined-clay clinker substitution + DBM displacement of imports each have measurable CO₂ reduction credentials — route to Boji's carbon-finance module.
9. **Silicosis = #1 occupational hazard** across silica sand, limestone, pozzolana, diatomite, bentonite, mica. PPE + medical surveillance + wet-dust controls non-negotiable.
10. **Mwananchi cooperative pathway**: salt, gypsum, pozzolana, agricultural lime, building stone — all are viable artisanal-scale Boji onboarding pathways with low entry CAPEX. Limestone-for-aggregate is the highest-volume EAC opportunity.
