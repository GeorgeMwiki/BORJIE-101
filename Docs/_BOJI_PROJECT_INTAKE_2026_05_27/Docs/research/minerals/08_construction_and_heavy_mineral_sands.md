## 1. Aggregates — crushed stone, gravel, sand

### 1.A Identity

Aggregates are the **size-graded mineral particulate** that forms the bulk
of concrete, asphalt, road base, ballast and engineered fill. They are
classified by:

- **NMAS** (Nominal Maximum Aggregate Size) — the sieve through which 95%
  passes. Common NMAS: 40 mm (sub-base), 20 mm (concrete), 14 mm (asphalt
  wearing course), 10 mm (concrete fines), 6 mm (chippings), <5 mm
  (sand fraction).
- **Gradation curve** — particle-size distribution plotted on a Fuller /
  Talbot curve. Continuous gradation = strong packing; gap-graded = drainage.
- **Source mineralogy** — granite (high strength, low absorption), basalt
  (very high crushing strength, dark colour), dolerite (preferred for road
  base), limestone (cheap, weaker, alkali-prone), quartzite (excellent but
  hard on crushers).
- **Shape & angularity** — crushed faces (>2 faces) preferred for asphalt
  interlock; rounded river gravel preferred for high-flow concrete.

| Test | Spec target (road base / structural concrete) | Failure consequence |
|------|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------|
| LA Abrasion (ASTM C131) | < 30 % (road base); < 40 % (concrete) | Pavement ravelling |
| ACV (Aggregate Crushing Value, BS 812) | < 25 % | Structural failure under load |
| AIV (Aggregate Impact Value) | < 25 % | Cracking under dynamic load |
| 10% fines (BS 812) | > 100 kN | Surface course unsuitable |
| Polished-stone-value (PSV) | > 50 for skid-resistant courses | Wet-skid accidents |
| Mg-sulphate soundness | < 18 % loss after 5 cycles | Frost / wet-dry decay |
| Water absorption | < 2 % (concrete); < 1.5 % (asphalt) | Freeze-thaw spalling |
| ASR (alkali-silica reactivity) | Innocuous per ASTM C1260 (<0.10 %) | Concrete expansion / map-cracking |

### 1.B Geology & deposit types

- **Hard-rock crushing** — granite plutons (Mwanza, Geita, Tabora), basalt
  flows (Ngozi-Mbeya, Arusha rift volcanics), dolerite dykes, limestone
  (Wazo Hill, Tanga, Mtwara), quartzite. Hard-rock yields predictable
  spec but capital-heavy (drill-blast-crush plant).
- **Alluvial gravel terraces** — river-cut benches with rounded pebble fill
  (Pangani, Wami, Ruvu, Ruaha valleys). Cheap to extract but variable in
  spec; often used for sub-base only.
- **Glacial outwash** — irrelevant to East Africa (no Pleistocene glaciers
  south of Kilimanjaro summit). Significant in northern hemisphere markets.
- **Marine aggregate** — offshore dredged gravel, used in Europe / Japan;
  generally banned for structural use in Tanzania due to chloride
  contamination.

### 1.C Tanzania & East Africa relevance

Every major Tanzanian urban centre has a captive aggregate ring:

- **Dar es Salaam** — Mlandizi & Kibaha granite/basalt quarries (Pwani
  Region); Wazo Hill limestone (also Twiga Cement source); Kunduchi /
  Bunju coast gravel pits.
- **Arusha / Moshi** — Olympic-area basalt and lava-flow scoria, Meru
  volcanic aggregate.
- **Mwanza** — Sukuma granite domes (Sasco Trading, Kleemann-plant quarry
  near Lake Victoria).
- **Mbeya** — Ngozi caldera basalt + Mbozi granite.
- **Mtwara / Lindi** — Cretaceous limestone (also feeds Dangote Mtwara
  cement plant) plus coastal river gravel.

Pricing (Mining Commission indicative, Q2 2025, TZS-denominated)
translates roughly to USD 6 – 14 / t at the quarry gate for crushed stone
and USD 8 – 18 / t for graded concrete sand, with the **economic radius
hard-capped near 50 km** because hauling > 50 km on TZS 350-400 / t·km
truck tariffs eats half the gross margin.

### 1.D Exploration & determination

1. **Reconnaissance** — geological mapping + rock-mass classification
   (RMR, Q-system) to confirm a competent face > 60 m long, > 15 m high.
2. **Drilling** — diamond core (HQ size, 63.5 mm) on 25-50 m grid to test
   continuity and find weathering profile.
3. **Aggregate test battery** — LA abrasion, ACV, AIV, 10% fines, water
   absorption, soundness, ASR, petrography.
4. **Pre-quarry pit design** — bench geometry, blast-design, drainage,
   plant location, overburden ratio (target < 0.3:1 for greenfield).

### 1.E Mining methods

Drill-and-blast, 89-152 mm blastholes, 5-10 m benches, ANFO + emulsion
boosters. Primary jaw crusher (typically 1200 × 900 mm jaw) → secondary
cone (CS440 class) → tertiary cone (CH660) → screens producing the 40 /
20 / 14 / 10 / 6 / dust fractions. Wash plant for sand fraction. Stockpile
on graded pad with bay separation.

### 1.F Beneficiation

- Dry classification by **inclined screens** (4-deck typical).
- Wet washing through a **hydrocyclone** to strip clay and silt fines.
- **Attrition scrubbing** for sticky overburden contamination.
- **VSI (vertical shaft impactor)** for manufactured-sand finishing —
  cubical particle shape, AFS fineness 60-80.

### 1.G Final product specs

- **Sub-base** — graded crushed rock 0-40 mm, CBR > 80 %, plasticity index
  < 6.
- **Base course** — 0-20 mm, CBR > 100 %, ACV < 25 %.
- **Concrete coarse** — 20 mm or 10 mm single-size, ASTM C33.
- **Asphalt aggregate** — 14 mm / 10 mm / 6 mm chippings, PSV > 50, AAV
  (aggregate abrasion value) < 14.

### 1.H Movement, packaging

- Short-haul tipper-truck (10 m³ / 16 m³ Howo / FAW).
- Captive haul-trucks (Belaz / CAT 770G) inside the quarry.
- Bulk transfer to a **cement plant** by conveyor (Wazo, Tanga, Mtwara
  Dangote, Mbeya / Tanga Twiga).

### 1.I Pricing & markets

| Product | Quarry gate USD/t | Delivered USD/t @ 50 km |
|---------|-------------------|--------------------------|
| 0-40 mm sub-base | 5 – 9 | 18 – 25 |
| 20 mm concrete | 8 – 14 | 22 – 32 |
| 10 mm asphalt | 10 – 16 | 25 – 35 |
| Crusher dust | 3 – 6 | 10 – 14 |
| Manufactured sand | 9 – 15 | 22 – 30 |

### 1.J EHS

- **Silicosis** — exposure to crystalline silica dust > 0.025 mg/m³ over
  8 h triggers OSHA / TBS action level. Wet drilling + dust suppression
  mandatory.
- **Blast injuries** — flyrock, mis-fires; OAS / NIOSH protocols.
- **Pit-wall collapse** — leading fatality category; bench angles must
  honour rock-mass strength.
- **Noise & vibration** — community complaints, PPV limit < 50 mm/s.

### 1.K Boji decision triggers

- *"Economic radius < 50 km from market. Beyond that, transport eats
  margin — flag the deal."*
- *"If LA abrasion > 30 % → not road-base grade; restrict to fill /
  sub-base."*
- *"ASR-positive aggregate (ASTM C1260 > 0.10 %) must be flagged for
  concrete; downgrade to asphalt or fill only."*
- *"Bench height > 15 m without geotech sign-off → block listing until
  RMR documented."*

## 2. Sand (manufactured, river, sea, frac)

### 2.A Identity

Sand is the 0.075 – 4.75 mm fraction. **AFS Grain Fineness Number** (GFN)
classifies it: GFN 35-40 (coarse), 45-60 (medium, preferred for concrete),
60-80 (fine, manufactured), > 100 (foundry-grade fines). Concrete sand
must be **silica-rich, low chloride, low clay**.

| Sand type | Source | Typical use | Chloride | Clay+silt |
|-----------|--------|-------------|----------|-----------|
| River sand | Bed-load Pangani, Ruvu, Wami | Concrete, mortar | < 0.02 % | < 3 % |
| Aeolian dune | Saharan / Sahel desert | Generally **rejected** (too round, low cohesion) | low | low |
| Beach / marine | Coastal Bagamoyo, Mafia | Generally **rejected** (chloride) | > 0.5 % | varies |
| Manufactured (M-sand) | Crusher fines, VSI-shaped | Concrete (preferred 2025+) | < 0.02 % | < 3 % |
| Frac sand | High-purity quartzite, Permian | Oil & gas well completion | n/a | n/a |
| Foundry | Quartz dune / Lake-sand | Metal casting moulds | < 0.05 % | < 0.5 % |

### 2.B Geology & deposit types

- **River bedload** is the gold standard — water-washed, low clay,
  rounded-to-subangular grains, good packing.
- **Aeolian dune** — too rounded ("ball-bearing" effect ruins concrete
  cohesion); occasionally suitable for plaster.
- **Beach** — chloride contamination (NaCl > 0.04 % triggers rebar
  corrosion in reinforced concrete; ASTM C1218 limit).
- **Manufactured** — produced by crushing igneous rock (granite, basalt)
  and VSI-shaping to cubical grains. Now mandatory in many Indian states;
  Tanzania trending the same direction post-2024.

### 2.C Tanzania & East Africa relevance

- **Pangani River** sand: heavily mined, especially Korogwe-Hale stretch.
  Pangani River Basin Office has imposed extraction quotas; full-basin
  moratoria periodically declared after flood damage.
- **Ruvu River** (Coast Region): main feeder for Dar concrete; NEMC has
  flagged riverbank-collapse at Mlandizi.
- **Wami River**: similar pressure; Wami-Ruvu Basin Water Office
  regulates abstraction & sand permits.
- **Marine sand**: Kunduchi, Kawe and Bagamoyo coast — multiple district
  bans after measurable coastal erosion (NEMC 2018+ enforcement actions).
- **M-sand**: growing — every major quarry in Kibaha/Mlandizi now sells
  M-sand alongside crushed stone.

### 2.D Exploration & determination

- **AFS GFN** (sieve analysis).
- **Chloride content** by AgNO₃ titration or ion chromatograph.
- **Sulphate** content (limit 0.4 %).
- **Organic content** (NaOH colour test — Tanco / Mn standard).
- **Methylene-blue value** for clay activity.
- **Bulk density**, **silt content**, **soundness**.

### 2.E Mining methods

- **Hydraulic dredging** — barge-mounted suction dredge, used offshore
  & in deep river pools.
- **Mechanical (dry mining)** — excavator into truck; favoured in
  shallow river bars (most TZ extraction).
- **Suction-pump shore extraction** — gravel-pump on river bank.
- **Manufactured sand**: produced at the parent quarry, dry/wet.

### 2.F Beneficiation

Washing (cyclone + dewatering screen) → attrition scrubbing → spiral
classifier → stockpile.

### 2.G Final product specs

- **ASTM C33 zones 1-4** for concrete fine aggregate.
- **EN 12620** in EAC procurement (post-2021 harmonisation).
- **Mortar sand**: finer fraction, no oversize.
- **Frac sand (API RP 19C)**: 40/70 mesh or 30/50 mesh, 95% sphericity,
  crush resistance > 6,000 psi at the design closure stress.

### 2.H Movement & packaging

Bulk tipper haul; no packaging. Frac sand is uniquely shipped in 1-tonne
super-sacks by rail (BNSF Permian basin).

### 2.I Pricing

| Sand | USD/t at source | Notes |
|------|-----------------|-------|
| River sand (TZ) | 8 – 18 | Permit-tight basins, see § 8 |
| M-sand (TZ) | 9 – 15 | Co-produced at quarry |
| Beach sand | n/a | Generally illegal for structural use |
| Foundry sand (silica) | 35 – 80 | Specialist market |
| Frac sand (US FOB rail) | 30 – 90 | Negligible TZ market |

### 2.J EHS

- River-bank collapse, fish-habitat destruction, salination.
- Coastal erosion from marine extraction.
- Vehicle accidents on un-graded haul tracks.
- Sand-mafia / informal-economy criminality (documented globally; Boji
  must flag KYC red-flag on un-permitted suppliers).

### 2.K Boji decision triggers

- *"Marine sand requires chloride < 0.04 %. Reject by default — fail
  rate ~70 % for direct marine sand to reinforced concrete."*
- *"River sand near Pangani / Ruvu / Wami: check basin-office permit
  status before recommending; flag NEMC moratorium if active."*
- *"M-sand should be the default recommendation for new procurement
  contracts > 5,000 t — supply security + spec stability."*
- *"Frac sand: not relevant to TZ given negligible shale-gas market."*

## 3. Dimension stone (granite, marble, slate, basalt, sandstone)

### 3.A Identity

Dimension stone is **rock cut to specified geometries** — blocks, slabs,
tiles, kerbs — for architectural, monumental and street-furniture use.
Classified by:

- **Mineralogy & rock type** — granite, marble, slate, basalt, sandstone,
  limestone, quartzite, gabbro ("black granite").
- **Hardness** (Mohs / Knoop).
- **Finish** — polished, honed, flamed, sandblasted, bush-hammered,
  leathered.
- **Block size** — typically marketed as 3 m³ (8 t) blocks; > 5 m³
  premium; < 1.5 m³ scrap.

### 3.B Geology

- **Granite / gabbro** ("black granite") — Precambrian Tanzania Craton
  intrusives. Tanzania's gabbro/diabase is heavily exported as
  "African Black" / "Tanzania Black" through Mwanza and Dar es Salaam.
- **Marble** — metamorphosed limestone; Tanzania has some Mozambique-belt
  marble (Kongwa, Singida) but volume is small vs Carrara, Macael.
- **Slate** — fine-grained metamorphic; minor TZ occurrences.
- **Sandstone & limestone** — Karoo / coastal sediments.
- **Quartzite** — Bukoba quartzite has been quarried as flagstone.

### 3.C Tanzania & East Africa relevance

- **Tanzania Black granite** — premium export to Italy, China, India
  (raw blocks; cut and re-exported globally as polished slab).
- **Mwanza & Geita** — granite belt for both aggregate AND dimension
  stone; some operators (e.g., Marmo e Granito, Jitegemee Holdings) cut
  domestic slab.
- **Bukoba quartzite** — flagstone & cladding (lower volume).
- **Dodoma-Singida marble** — boutique scale.

### 3.D Exploration & determination

- Surface mapping for joint-spacing — joints > 3 m apart needed for
  saleable blocks.
- **Trial-blast / trial-cut** to test recovery (% of saleable block per
  bench).
- **Petrographic & physical tests** — compressive strength > 130 MPa
  (granite), water absorption < 0.4 %, abrasion (Böhme) < 18 cm³/50 cm².
- **Colour & finish trial** on polished test-slab — colour-fastness,
  iron-staining behaviour.

### 3.E Mining methods

- **Diamond-wire saw** — primary cutting method, 7 mm wire bead, water-
  cooled, ~ 4 m²/h cutting rate in granite.
- **Chain-saw** — soft stones (marble, soft limestone).
- **Splitting wedges & jet-burning** — for very hard rock where wire
  is uneconomic.
- **Hydraulic bag-splitter** — to break blocks free.
- Minimal blasting (controlled detonator-cord only) to preserve block
  integrity.

### 3.F Processing

- **Block-sawing** (gang-saw with diamond blades, or single-blade saw).
- **Polishing line** — successive grit heads (60, 120, 220, 400, 800,
  1500, 3000) + brilliantatura final pass.
- **Edging, sizing, calibration**.
- **Cratering** & resin-filling for void-prone slabs.

### 3.G Final products

- **Raw blocks** — 2.5-3 m³, exported FCL or break-bulk to Italian /
  Chinese / Indian cutters.
- **Slabs** — typically 2.7 × 1.8 m × 20 mm or 30 mm.
- **Tiles** — 30 × 30, 30 × 60, 60 × 60 cm.
- **Kerbs / setts / monumental work**.

### 3.H Movement, packaging, hazard

- Blocks shipped in **20' FCL** (weight-limited to ~ 22 t per container).
- Slabs in **A-frames** in 40' FCL, foam-padded.
- Hazards: silica dust, heavy-lift handling injuries, crane / spreader-bar
  failure.

### 3.I Pricing

| Stone | USD/m³ FOB Dar | Notes |
|-------|----------------|-------|
| Common grey granite | 200 – 400 | Volume export |
| Tanzania Black / gabbro | 800 – 1,500 | Premium colour |
| Boutique reds / blues | 1,500 – 3,000+ | Rare colour, low yield |
| Marble (boutique) | 500 – 1,200 | Low TZ volume |
| Slate | 150 – 350 | Small market |
| Bukoba quartzite | 250 – 500 | Flagstone |

### 3.J EHS

- Silicosis (granite > 30 % free silica).
- Slipping / crush injuries during block handling.
- Diesel particulate from wire-saw rigs.

### 3.K Boji decision triggers

- *"Block size matters. Recovery < 1.5 m³ blocks = scrap (sells only as
  aggregate). >3 m³ blocks command premium."*
- *"Wire-saw cut-rate < 2 m²/h on a granite face → quarry geology is
  too jointed; question economic viability."*
- *"Tanzania Black gabbro: protect FOB price discipline — Indian buyers
  routinely undercut by 30 % via informal trade."*

## 4. Pumice & scoria (volcanic light aggregates)

### 4.A Identity

- **Pumice** — highly vesicular silicic volcanic glass; density 0.25-0.9
  g/cm³; floats on water. Used as lightweight concrete aggregate, abrasive,
  horticultural medium, pozzolan.
- **Scoria** — basaltic equivalent; denser (0.9-1.5 g/cm³), darker,
  excellent thermal mass; used in roadbase, septic-field, lightweight
  concrete blocks.

### 4.B Geology

Pyroclastic deposits at volcanic centres. East Africa's Rift gives
Tanzania abundant young volcanic supply:

- **Kilimanjaro / Meru** — scoria cones, lapilli aprons.
- **Ngozi caldera (Mbeya)** — pumice and ignimbrite.
- **Oldonyo Lengai / Engaruka plains** — natrocarbonatite + scoria.

### 4.C Tanzania relevance

- Mbeya / Songwe pumice is **exported to East African cement plants**
  as pozzolan (clinker substitute) and to local lightweight-concrete
  block makers.
- Arusha / Kilimanjaro scoria feeds road sub-base and septic-leaching
  beds.

### 4.D Determination

- Bulk density.
- Particle-size distribution.
- Pozzolanic activity (ASTM C311 strength activity index > 75 % at 28
  days).
- LOI < 10 %, SiO₂ + Al₂O₃ + Fe₂O₃ > 70 % for pozzolan-grade.

### 4.E Mining methods

Open-pit, ripper-and-loader (no blasting needed); screen on-site to size
fractions.

### 4.F Processing

Minimal — screen, dry, sometimes lightly crushed for blocks.

### 4.G Specs

- **ASTM C332** lightweight aggregate for structural concrete.
- **ASTM C618 Class N** natural pozzolan.

### 4.H Pricing

- USD 25 – 80 / t FOB; pozzolan-grade fetches higher when cement plants
  bid for clinker substitution to cut CO₂ footprint.

### 4.I EHS

- Dust + fine glass shards (eye / lung hazard).
- Rim-collapse on un-buttressed pit walls.

### 4.J Boji triggers

- *"Pozzolan activity < 75 % → not cement-grade; downgrade to
  lightweight-block use."*
- *"Mbeya pumice ex-Tunduma: check Zambian onward route — Lafarge Ndola
  is a buyer."*

## 5. Cement raw materials — aggregate dimension recap

> Detailed treatment is in dossier **04 — Industrial Minerals**. Here we
> recap only the *aggregate* dimension.

Cement-plant inputs:

| Input | Source | Role |
|-------|--------|------|
| Limestone (CaCO₃) | Wazo Hill, Mtwara, Tanga, Mbeya | 75-80 % of raw mix |
| Clay / shale | Quarried close to plant | Silica + alumina |
| Iron ore / laterite | Imported or local | Fe₂O₃ flux |
| Bauxite | Often imported | Alumina trim |
| Gypsum | Imported (Oman) | Set retarder |
| Pozzolan (pumice, fly-ash, scoria) | Mbeya pumice, regional fly-ash | Clinker substitute (CO₂ reduction) |

Tanzanian cement-aggregate dynamics:

- **Wazo Hill / Twiga Cement** — Pwani limestone, integrated quarry.
- **Tanga Cement (Simba)** — coastal limestone + pozzolan from Mbeya.
- **Dangote Mtwara** — Cretaceous limestone, captive quarry.
- **Mbeya Cement (Lafarge)** — Songwe limestone.

Boji must encode the **pozzolan-substitution premium**: pumice/scoria
that meets ASTM C618 sells for USD 35-55/t to cement plants, vs USD 15/t
as plain lightweight aggregate.

## 6. Heavy-Mineral Sands (HMS / mineral sands)

> This is the **highest-margin** chapter of this dossier. HMS is where
> "construction-style coastal sand mining" meets "critical-minerals
> economics".

### 6.A Identity

HMS deposits contain a suite of **dense (SG > 4.0) detrital minerals**
hosted in beach / aeolian / palaeo-strandline sands. The valuable
fractions:

| Mineral | Formula | SG | Magnetic | Conductor | Market role |
|---------|---------|----|----------|-----------|-------------|
| Ilmenite | FeTiO₃ | 4.5-5.0 | strongly | yes | Sulphate-process TiO₂ pigment |
| Rutile | TiO₂ | 4.2 | non | yes | Chloride-process TiO₂; welding fluxes |
| Leucoxene | altered ilmenite (60-90 % TiO₂) | 3.8-4.3 | weakly | yes | Premium pigment feed |
| Zircon | ZrSiO₄ | 4.6 | non | non | Ceramic opacifier, refractory, nuclear |
| Monazite | (Ce,La,Nd,Th)PO₄ | 4.8-5.5 | weakly | non | REE + thorium feedstock |
| Garnet | (Mg,Fe,Mn,Ca)₃Al₂Si₃O₁₂ | 3.5-4.3 | weakly | non | Abrasive (waterjet, blasting) |
| Sillimanite / kyanite | Al₂SiO₅ | 3.2-3.6 | non | non | Refractory |
| Staurolite | (Fe,Mg)₂Al₉Si₄O₂₃(OH) | 3.7 | weakly | non | Foundry sand |

A typical "total heavy mineral" (THM) head-grade is reported as **% THM**
in the host sand. **3-5 % THM** is economic for greenfield projects;
> 8 % is exceptional. Within THM, the **valuable heavy-mineral (VHM)
assemblage** is what drives revenue — VHM = ilmenite + rutile + leucoxene
+ zircon + monazite.

### 6.B Geology of formation & deposit types

HMS forms by **gravity-sorting of denser detrital grains** along beach
foreshore, swash-zone and aeolian back-beach environments. Three
sub-types:

1. **Modern beach / dune deposits** — currently active strandlines.
   Western Australia (Eneabba, Cooljarloo), Mozambique (Moma, Chibuto),
   Senegal (Grande Côte / Diogo), Madagascar (QMM / Fort Dauphin), South
   Africa (Richards Bay, Tormin), Tanzania (Pwani / Pangani / Mtwara
   coast).
2. **Palaeo-strandline deposits** — uplifted ancient beaches now inland.
   Murray Basin (Australia: Ginkgo, Pooncarie, Snapper); Indonesia
   palaeo-strands.
3. **Alluvial / placer HMS** — re-worked from older deposits into river
   systems; smaller scale.

Tropical / coastal climates with strong long-shore drift and a hinterland
of weathered Precambrian crystalline rocks (TiO₂-bearing) are ideal — and
Tanzania's southern and Pwani coast match that template.

### 6.C Tanzania & East Africa relevance

Tanzania is on the cusp of becoming an HMS producer. Confirmed projects
(2025-2026):

- **Fungoni** (Strandline Resources, 25 km south of Dar) — 12.3 Mt @
  3.9 % THM (upper zone 10.8 Mt @ 4.4 %). Assemblage: **42 % ilmenite,
  18 % zircon, 4.5 % rutile, 1.2 % leucoxene**. LOM saleable output:
  ~197 kt ilmenite, ~83 kt zircon, ~16 kt rutile, ~7 kt monazite.
- **Tajiri / Pangani** (Strandline / Nyati Mineral Sands) — Tanga region,
  ~ 268 Mt total HMS resource estimate; ~74 Mt proven; ilmenite-zircon-
  rutile-rich. Long-dated, large-scale.
- **Madimba (Mtwara)** — discovered by reconnaissance sampling; two
  zones (Madimba & Madimba East), up to 3 km × 1.5 km mineralisation
  footprints; zircon + rutile component locally reaches 16 % of THM.
- **Bagamoyo / Mkuranga corridor** — exploration play; smaller scale.

The Indicative Mineral Prices issued by the Tanzania Mining Commission
(Tume ya Madini) for Q2 2025 list:

- Ilmenite ~ **USD 705 / t**.
- Rutile ~ **USD 2,305 / t**.
- Zircon sand ~ **USD 1,400 – 1,500 / t**, FOB Dar.

These are royalty-base prices, not market clears; Boji must reconcile to
Fastmarkets / TZMI quotes.

### 6.D Exploration & determination

1. **Reconnaissance surface sampling** — 0.5-1.5 m hand-auger / push-core
   on regular grid (Madimba used reconnaissance surface sampling +
   follow-up drilling on > 3 % THM anomalies).
2. **Aircore / sonic drilling** — 100 × 50 m → 50 × 25 m → infill 25 × 25
   m grids as the project advances from inferred to indicated to measured
   resource.
3. **Heavy-liquid separation** (LST or TBE at SG 2.85-2.96) to isolate
   THM in the lab.
4. **Mineral split**: magnetic separator (Frantz / induced-roll) +
   electrostatic separator + heavy-liquid + sieve sizing to give the
   "in-lab mineral assemblage": % ilmenite / % rutile / % leucoxene / %
   zircon / % monazite / % heavy gangue (silicate slimes).
5. **QEMSCAN / TIMA-X automated mineralogy** — confirms liberation,
   intergrowth, alteration (e.g., ilmenite-to-leucoxene weathering).
6. **TiO₂ grade in ilmenite** — XRF; key for whether ilmenite is
   sulphate-process feed (50-55 % TiO₂) or upgrade-process feed (54-65 %
   TiO₂).

### 6.E Mining methods

Two main routes:

- **Dry mining** — dozer-truck-feed to a mobile mining unit (MMU) with
  trommel + wet screen, then pipe slurry to a wet concentrator plant.
  Used at Fungoni-style coastal projects.
- **Wet (dredge) mining** — floating cutter-suction dredge feeds a
  floating wet concentrator; suited to high-water-table, large-tonnage
  operations (Iluka Jacinth-Ambrosia, Rio Tinto RBM).

### 6.F Beneficiation & processing — the HMS flowsheet

This is the **core technical exam** Boji must master:

**Stage 1 — Wet concentrator plant (WCP)**

- Trommel + scrubber to liberate clay from sand.
- Desliming hydrocyclones (cut at ~ 38 µm).
- **Gravity spirals** (Reichert / Mineral Technologies HG / WW / MG):
  three-stage rougher-cleaner-recleaner.
- Output: **Heavy-mineral concentrate (HMC)** at **90-95 % heavy
  minerals**.
- Tailings: silica sand returned to mining void for rehabilitation.

**Stage 2 — Dry mineral separation plant (MSP)**

The dry plant uses three principles — magnetic susceptibility, electrical
conductivity, and density — to split HMC into final products:

1. **Heat dry** the HMC.
2. **High-intensity magnetic separation** (induced-roll, rare-earth-roll,
   or wet high-intensity drum): pulls **ilmenite + monazite** (magnetic)
   from **rutile + zircon + leucoxene** (non-magnetic).
3. **Electrostatic separation** (high-tension roll): separates
   **conductors (rutile, ilmenite)** from **non-conductors (zircon,
   monazite, silicates)**.
4. **Gravity tables / spirals** for residual cleanup.
5. **Optical / X-ray sorting** for high-purity final clean-up.

The output streams:

- **Ilmenite final**: 52-65 % TiO₂.
- **Rutile final**: 94-96 % TiO₂.
- **Zircon final**: ZrO₂ + HfO₂ + SiO₂ ≥ 98 %.
- **Leucoxene final**: 70-90 % TiO₂ (premium upgrade feed).
- **Monazite stream**: 50-60 % REO + 5-10 % ThO₂ (NORM-managed).

**Stage 3 — Ilmenite upgrading (optional, off-site)**

- **Sorel slag** / **QIT-Fer-Titane** electric-arc smelt to 80-87 % TiO₂
  slag + pig iron.
- **Becher process**: oxidation + reduction + aeration + leach to give
  **synthetic rutile** at ~ 93 % TiO₂.
- **Benilite / Murso**: HCl leach to remove Fe.

Tanzania's projects today aim to **export HMC or split products** rather
than build domestic upgrade plant (capex too high; sub-economic at
current scale).

| Product | TiO₂ / ZrO₂ | Use | Buyer profile |
|---------|--------------|-----|----------------|
| Ilmenite sulphate-grade | 50-55 % TiO₂ | Sulphate TiO₂ pigment plant | Chinese pigment majors |
| Ilmenite chloride-grade | 58-65 % TiO₂ | Chloride TiO₂ pigment plant | Tronox, Chemours, Venator |

### 6.H Movement, packaging, hazard

- Ilmenite + rutile + zircon: **bulk vessel** (handysize 28-40 kt) FOB
  Dar / Mtwara.
- Smaller shipments: **20' FCL** with 1-tonne FIBC super-sacks (especially
  zircon and monazite).
- Monazite is a **NORM (Naturally Occurring Radioactive Material)** —
  IAEA Safety Standards GSR Part 3 + Tanzania's Atomic Energy Commission
  (TAEC) require licence for handling, transport, export. Surface dose
  rates typically < 2 μSv/h on bagged monazite but bulk piles can hit
  10-50 μSv/h.
- Hazards: silicosis (silica gangue), beryllium / thorium dust at the
  MSP, fire/static at electrostatic separators, dredge / barge maritime
  risk.

## 7. Asphalt / bitumen-bearing sands & construction binders (brief)

Tanzania does not host a commercial oil-sand province. Construction-binder
materials of interest:

- **Bitumen / asphalt** — imported (Middle East-sourced), used for

## 8. Beach / river sand mining environmental governance

This is one of Tanzania's most politically sensitive extractive segments
because it (a) involves tens of thousands of artisanal participants, (b)
intersects with riverine fisheries, water supply and bridge / road
foundations, and (c) cuts across multiple regulators.

### 8.1 Regulator map

- **NEMC (National Environment Management Council)** — EIA approvals,
  enforcement of moratoria, environmental compliance audits.
- **Basin Water Boards** — Wami-Ruvu, Pangani, Rufiji, Lake Victoria
  basins. Each issues sand-extraction permits with quotas and seasonal
  windows.
- **District councils** — site-level permits and revenue collection.
- **TARURA / TANROADS** — concerns about bridge-pier scour caused by
  upstream sand extraction.
- **Tume ya Madini (Mining Commission)** — royalty collection (industrial
  minerals chapter).
- **TFS (Tanzania Forest Service)** — where extraction is in or adjacent
  to forest reserves.
- **TAEC** — only where HMS / NORM is involved.

### 8.2 Recurring controversies

- **Pangani River** — Korogwe / Hale stretch: periodic basin-wide
  moratoria after flood damage exacerbated by over-extraction.
- **Ruvu River** — Mlandizi sand-bars: bank-collapse and water-treatment
  intake siltation.
- **Wami River** — Chalinze corridor; competition between sand miners
  and Wami-Mbiki conservation area neighbours.
- **Marine sand bans** — Kunduchi, Kawe, Mbweni, Bagamoyo: multiple
  enforcement actions after measurable beach retreat.
- **Bridge-foundation undermining** — TANROADS has lobbied for buffer
  zones (typically 500 m upstream / downstream of bridges).

### 8.3 Compliance checklist (Boji must encode for any sand-mining deal)

| Item | Required | Source |
|------|----------|--------|
| NEMC EIA certificate | Yes (Category B+ usually) | EMA 2004 + regulations |
| Basin Water Board permit | Yes | Water Resources Management Act 2009 |
| District council licence | Yes | LGA Finances Act |
| Bridge / road buffer respected | Yes (typ. 500 m) | TANROADS / TARURA standing orders |
| Royalty paid to Mining Commission | Yes | Mining Act 2010 (amended 2017) |
| Local Content Plan | Yes (post-2025 amendments) | Mining (Local Content) Regs 2025 |
| Community grievance log | Strongly advised | NEMC compliance audits |
| Seasonal extraction window | Yes (often dry-season only) | Basin Office condition |

### 8.4 Boji triggers — sand governance

- *"No active Basin Office permit → block deal."*
- *"Within 500 m of a TANROADS bridge → block deal until buffer
  cleared."*
- *"Marine sand for structural use → block by default (chloride)."*
- *"Active NEMC moratorium on this basin → freeze prospective listings
  and notify."*

## 9. Brick clay & fire-clay

### 9.A Identity

- **Brick clay** — plastic clay (kaolinitic / illitic / montmorillonitic
  blends) suitable for moulding and firing at 950-1,100 °C.
- **Fire-clay** — refractory clay with kaolinite dominance, Al₂O₃
  ≥ 30 %, low alkali; can withstand > 1,500 °C.

Key tests: **Atterberg limits** (liquid limit, plastic limit, plasticity
index), **firing shrinkage** (target 6-10 %), **drying shrinkage**
(target < 8 %), **PCE** (pyrometric cone equivalent) for refractory
grade.

### 9.B Geology

- **Residual clays** — kaolin in weathering profile of feldspathic rocks
  (Pugu, Tanzania).
- **Sedimentary clays** — alluvial / lacustrine deposits in river
  floodplains (Wami, Mara, Ruvu basins).
- **Volcanic ash-derived clays** — Mbeya region.

### 9.C Tanzania relevance

- **Pugu kaolin / clay deposit** (Pwani Region) — strategic kaolin
  resource; some grades used for tile and refractory.
- **Wazo Hill clays** — input to Twiga Cement raw mix.
- **Mwanza & Mara brickworks** — alluvial-clay-fired bricks.
- **Singida & Dodoma brickyards** — vernacular sun-dried + low-fired
  bricks.

### 9.D Determination

- Atterberg limits, particle-size, XRD mineralogy, firing-curve trial
  (drying loss, shrinkage, water absorption of fired brick), compressive
  strength (target > 7 MPa for class B brick, > 17.5 MPa for class A).

### 9.E Mining methods

- Open pit (shallow), excavator + tipper.
- Overburden often < 1 m.
- Pit-soaking and wedging traditional in artisanal yards.

### 9.F Processing

Extrusion (modern) or hand-moulding (artisanal) → drying yard → kiln
(beehive, Hoffman, tunnel) → grading.

### 9.G Products

- Solid brick, hollow block, paving brick, fire-brick, roof tile.

### 9.H Pricing

- USD 0.10 – 0.30 per brick at factory.
- Specialty fire-brick: USD 1 – 3 per brick.

### 9.I EHS

- **Wood-fired kilns** — Tanzanian artisanal kilns consume large
  quantities of fuelwood, driving local deforestation. TFS / NEMC have
  pushed for biomass-pellet / efficient-tunnel kiln adoption.
- Heat / burn injuries at kiln stage.
- Silicosis on clay-dryer floors.

### 9.J Boji triggers

- *"Wood-fired kiln brickworks → flag for biomass-pellet conversion
  opportunity; ESG-risk for institutional financing."*
- *"Pugu kaolin: check whether deal is brick-grade or tile/refractory-
  grade — pricing differential is 5-15×."*

## 10. Gravel (river vs glacial vs marine)

Gravel = 4.75-75 mm rounded-to-subangular detrital fragments.

### 10.A Types

- **River gravel** — water-rounded, well-sorted, low fines; preferred for
  concrete coarse and drainage. Tanzania: Pangani / Wami / Ruaha terraces.
- **Glacial outwash gravel** — Pleistocene meltwater fans; **not relevant
  in Tanzania** outside ice-cap remnants on Kilimanjaro.
- **Marine gravel** — offshore dredged; mostly relevant to North Sea and
  Japanese markets. Not consented in Tanzania.
- **Crushed gravel** — manufactured gravel sourced from quarry crushing;
  often blended with river gravel for spec compliance.

### 10.B Specs

- ASTM C33 coarse aggregate.
- Soundness < 18 % loss.
- Crushed particle content > 50 % for road structural layers.

### 10.C Tanzania

- Pangani / Wami / Ruvu terraces: same regulatory regime as river sand
  (Basin Office permits + NEMC EIA).
- **Glacial gravel** is purely an academic mention for Tanzania.
- **Marine gravel** is essentially unconsented.

### 10.D Boji trigger

- *"Glacial-origin gravel is irrelevant in TZ — flag the term in supplier
  documentation as a quality-assurance / fraud sniff."*

## 11. Cross-cutting Boji decision triggers (master list)

The following triggers consolidate the per-mineral cuts into the
explicit rules Boji's deal-screening engine should encode for the
construction & HMS family:

### 11.1 Aggregate / sand / gravel triggers

1. Economic radius hard-cap: **flag any aggregate / sand deal where
   delivered distance > 50 km from end-user without rail or barge
   alternative.**
2. **LA abrasion > 30 %** ⇒ block road-base use; allow only sub-base /
   fill.
3. **ASR positive (ASTM C1260 > 0.10 %)** ⇒ block concrete-coarse use.
4. **Marine sand / chloride > 0.04 %** ⇒ block structural use.
5. **Active Basin Water Board moratorium** ⇒ freeze listing.
6. **Within 500 m of TANROADS / TARURA bridge** ⇒ block extraction
   listing.
7. **No EIA + Basin permit + District licence trio** ⇒ block listing.
8. **Wood-fired brick kiln** ⇒ ESG flag.
9. **Glacial gravel claim in TZ supplier docs** ⇒ document-integrity
   flag.

### 11.2 Dimension stone triggers

10. **Recovery < 1.5 m³ per saleable block** ⇒ downgrade to aggregate;
    re-price.
11. **Wire-saw cut rate < 2 m²/h on granite** ⇒ jointed geology;
    economic risk.
12. **Slab water absorption > 0.4 %** ⇒ exterior-cladding ineligible.
13. **Asbestos serpentine cross-contamination** ⇒ block listing pending
    petrography.

### 11.3 HMS triggers

14. **THM head-grade < 2 %** ⇒ marginal; >3 % greenfield-economic; >5 %
    bankable.
15. **VHM / THM < 20 %** ⇒ low-value (heavy silicates dominate).
16. **Ilmenite TiO₂ < 50 %** ⇒ sulphate-only feed (Chinese buyers).
17. **Ilmenite TiO₂ ≥ 58 %** ⇒ chloride-grade premium (Tronox / Chemours
    / Venator eligible).
18. **Zircon premium**: require ZrO₂+HfO₂+SiO₂ ≥ 98 % and U+Th < 500 ppm.
19. **Monazite fraction > 0.5 % of HMC** ⇒ NORM licence (TAEC + IAEA GSR
    Part 3) mandatory; specialist REE buyer route; otherwise engineered-
    containment stockpile.
20. **No TAEC NORM licence in monazite-bearing project** ⇒ block listing.
21. **Coastal HMS without progressive rehabilitation plan** ⇒ block
    listing; ESG-tier 1 risk.

### 11.4 Pozzolan triggers

22. **Pozzolan activity index < 75 %** ⇒ not cement-grade; downgrade.
23. **Pumice LOI > 10 %** ⇒ degraded glass; not pozzolanic.

### 11.5 Cross-mineral KYC / compliance triggers

24. **Cash-only buyer for > USD 50,000 lot** ⇒ AML flag.
25. **No Mining Commission royalty receipt** ⇒ block listing.
26. **No Local Content Plan (post-2025 regulations)** ⇒ block listing.
27. **Royalty rate paid below Tume ya Madini indicative price** ⇒ price-
    integrity flag.
