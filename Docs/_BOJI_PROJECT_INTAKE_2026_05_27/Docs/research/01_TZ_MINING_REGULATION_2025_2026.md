## 1. Licence Types and Lifecycle

Tanzania's licensing regime is anchored in the **Mining Act 2010 (Cap 123)**, materially restructured by the *Written Laws (Miscellaneous Amendments) Act No. 7 of 2017*, the *Mining (Local Content) Regulations 2018*, the *Mining (State Participation) Regulations 2022*, the *Mining (CSR) Regulations 2023*, the *Finance Act 2024*, and the *Mining (Local Content) (Amendment) Regulations 2025* (GN 563). Mineral rights are issued by the **Mining Commission** ([Tume ya Madini — Application Procedures](https://www.tumemadini.go.tz/pages/applicationprocedure/)).

### 1.1 Prospecting Licence (PL)
- **Who can apply:** Any individual, body corporate, or partnership (foreign-owned permitted).
- **Area:** Up to **2,000 km²** per PL ([Rive & Co — PL Procedures](https://www.rive.co.tz/application-for-prospecting-licence-pl-procedures-in-tanzania/)).
- **Term:** Initial **4 years**, renewal periods of **3 years then 2 years** (max 9 years total) ([Tume ya Madini — Types of Licences](https://www.tumemadini.go.tz/pages/applicationprocedure/)).
- **At each renewal**, holder must **relinquish 50%** of the area (statutory area reduction).
- **Obligations:** Approved work programme + minimum expenditure schedule; annual reports; pay annual rent per km² (rate set in *Mining (Mineral Rights) Regulations*).
- **Cancellation triggers:** Failure to commence work within 90 days; non-payment of rent; failure to lodge work-programme reports; breach of EPP; declared dormant after 18 months of inactivity ([TanzaniaInvest — 8,501 licences issued, 14 revoked, 95 warned](https://www.tanzaniainvest.com/mining/mining-licences-2024-2025-issued-revoked-warnings) — 2025-05).

### 1.2 Primary Mining Licence (PML)
- **Who can apply:** **Tanzanian citizens only**, individual or 100% Tanzanian-owned company ([Tume ya Madini](https://www.tumemadini.go.tz/pages/applicationprocedure/)).
- **Area:** Maximum **10 hectares** per PML ([GERPAT Solutions — Mining Licences Guide](https://gerpatsolutions.co.tz/mining-licences-acquired-in-tanzania-the-comprehensive-guide/)).
- **Capital threshold:** Investment **≤ USD 5 million** ([Tume ya Madini — Application Procedures](https://www.tumemadini.go.tz/pages/applicationprocedure/)).
- **Term:** **7 years**, renewable.
- **Obligations:** **Environmental Protection Plan (EPP)** certified by NEMC; mine-closure and rehabilitation liability; royalty + 1% inspection fee on sales.
- **Cancellation:** Non-payment of royalty/rent; failure to lodge EPP; dormancy; transfer to non-citizen.

### 1.3 Mining Licence (ML)
- **Who can apply:** Companies (foreign permitted) with capital **> USD 100,000** but **≤ USD 100 million** (medium-scale band) ([Lexology — First-step Analysis: Mining in Tanzania](https://www.lexology.com/library/detail.aspx?g=450aa156-1f27-426d-92df-4d6aa95c6e8b)).
- **Area:** Defined by mineable reserve in the feasibility study (typically ≤ 10 km²).
- **Term:** **10 years**, renewable.
- **Obligations:** EIA approved by NEMC; feasibility study; CSR plan agreed with LGA; 16% state free-carried interest; local-content plan; programme of mining operations.

### 1.4 Special Mining Licence (SML)
- **Who can apply:** Large-scale projects, capital **> USD 100 million**, issued by the Mining Commission **on Cabinet approval** ([Tume ya Madini](https://www.tumemadini.go.tz/pages/applicationprocedure/)).
- **Area:** No statutory cap — set by the ore-body life-of-mine.
- **Term:** Up to **estimated life of ore body** (per s.43 of Mining Act, Cap 123).
- **Obligations:** Framework Agreement with Government; **16% non-dilutable free-carried interest**; tax-for-equity scaling up to 50% ([Bowmans — State Participation Regulations 2022](https://bowmanslaw.com/insights/tanzania-mining-state-participation-regulations-2022-published/)); CSR plan; full EIA; mine-closure bond; local content; **20% mandatory domestic gold trade** if gold producer ([FB Attorneys — BoT Gold Programme Notice](https://fbattorneys.co.tz/bot-issues-notice-on-gold-purchase-program-in-tanzania/) — 2024-10-08).
- **Cancellation:** Material breach of Framework Agreement; default on royalty; failure to commence operations within 18 months.

### 1.5 Processing, Smelting, Refining
- Separate licences for **Processing**, **Smelting**, and **Refining** issued by the Commission; mandatory for any in-country value addition; aligned with the **2025 directive that no ML/SML will be issued without a local value-addition plan** ([TanzaniaInvest — 227 applications revoked / value addition mandate](https://www.tanzaniainvest.com/mining/ministry-revokes-mining-license-applications-and-declares-no-new-licenses-without-local-value-addition) — 2024-05).
- Designated refineries for the BoT Gold Programme: **Geita Gold Refinery Ltd, Mwanza Precious Metals Refinery Co. Ltd, Eyes of Africa Ltd**.

### 1.6 Dealer Licence
- Permits the holder to **buy, sell, and otherwise deal in minerals within Tanzania** ([Eden Law Chambers — Mineral Dealer Licence](https://www.edenlawchambers.co.tz/2020/04/01/mineral-license-in-tanzania/)).
- **Term:** **1 year**, renewable annually.
- **Obligations:** Secure premises, AML compliance, records of all source mines/licence holders, mandatory through-Mineral-Trading-Centre transactions, statutory fees and inspection fees on every consignment.

### 1.7 Broker Licence
- Acts as intermediary at a Buying Centre; **may not take physical possession**; cannot export ([Cymbell Attorneys — Mineral Trading & Export](https://tz.cmadvocates.com/the-legal-and-procedural-requirement-for-establishing-mineral-trading-and-export-operations-in-tanzania/)).
- **Term:** 1 year, renewable.

### 1.8 Mineral Trading Centres (MTCs) and Buying Stations
- Established under the *Mining (Mineral Trading) Regulations* and amendments. Trading of **gold, tin, diamond, tanzanite, and coloured gemstones** must happen through MTCs and Buying Centres across Tanzania mainland ([Tume ya Madini — Mineral Royalties Page](https://www.tumemadini.go.tz/pages/mineral-royalties-and-inspection-fees-rates/)).

### 1.9 Licensing throughput, FY2024/25
[TanzaniaInvest — 2024/25 Licence Statistics](https://www.tanzaniainvest.com/mining/mining-licences-2024-2025-issued-revoked-warnings) (2025-05): **8,501 licences** issued — 1 SML, 25 ML, 396 PL, 6,227 PML, 548 Dealer, 1,290 Broker, 1 Refining, 13 Processing. **14 revoked** + 95 warned + 118 PL default notices + 41 ML default notices.

## 2. Mining Commission, Cadastre, Payment Rails, and 2025 Reforms

### 2.1 Mining Commission (Tume ya Madini)
- Established under the *Mining Act* as amended by the *Written Laws (Misc. Amendments) Act No. 7 of 2017* ([Tume ya Madini — Functions](https://www.tumemadini.go.tz/about-us/functions/)).
- Departments include **Mineral Licensing and Information Systems**, which runs the **Mining Cadastre Section** and **Information Systems Management Section**.
- Operates the **online cadastre portal** at `portal.tumemadini.go.tz/portal/` (formerly `portal.madini.go.tz/map/`) ([Madini Portal](https://portal.madini.go.tz/) and [Spatial Dimension — TZ Online Cadastre Launched](https://www.spatialdimension.com/articles/tanzania-online-mining-cadastre-portal-launched)).
- The portal supports: license portfolio view, online applications for **new tenements, renewals, extensions, relinquishments, surrenders, and online payments**. Holders must complete one-time in-person registration at MEM Head Office (Division A/B) or Zonal/Resident Mines Offices (Division C/D).

### 2.2 Payment rails — GePG / Control Numbers
- All royalties, rents, inspection fees, and clearance fees are paid via the **Government Electronic Payment Gateway (GePG)** with a unique **Control Number** acting as the payment reference; channels include M-Pesa, Tigo Pesa, Airtel Money, HaloPesa, and bank counters ([GePG Tanzania](https://epay.gepg.go.tz/), [ClickPesa — Understanding GePG](https://clickpesa.com/understanding-gepg-in-tanzania-what-it-is-how-it-works-and-why-it-matters/)).

### 2.3 Regional architecture
- **Zonal Mines Offices** and **Resident Mines Offices** handle PML, Dealer, Broker (Division C/D); Government Mining Officers (GMOs) inspect and value at MTCs and at the mine gate.

### 2.4 Sister institutions
- **GST (Geological Survey of Tanzania)** — geoscientific data custodian; launched 2nd edition Industrial Minerals Book Nov 2024 ([TanzaniaInvest — GST Industrial Minerals Book](https://www.tanzaniainvest.com/mining/geological-survey-industrial-minerals-book-launch)).
- **STAMICO (State Mining Corporation)** — partners with GST to provide geological data to derisk miner lending ([STAMICO — Geological Services](https://stamico.co.tz/pages/geological-services)).
- **NEMC (National Environment Management Council)** — EIA approval, EPP audit ([NEMC EIA guidelines](https://www.nemc.or.tz/uploads/publications/sw-1576238321-EIA%20Training%20Manual%20Version%204.pdf)).
- **OSHA**, **TRA** (collects royalty via GePG), and **TEITI** (transparency reporting).
- Note: **TMAA was absorbed** when the Mining Commission was created in 2017; its inspection/valuation function moved inside the Commission.

### 2.5 Sector revenue
- FY2023/24: Ministry collected **TZS 753.82 billion** (85.45% of TZS 882.1B target); royalties = TZS 582.9B; inspection fees = TZS 106.2B; target FY2024/25 = **near TZS 1 trillion** ([TanzaniaInvest — Mining Sector Revenue 2023/24](https://www.tanzaniainvest.com/mining/mining-sector-revenue-2023-2024)).

## 3. Royalties, Inspection, Clearance, and Local Levies

### 3.1 Mining Act statutory royalty schedule
Calculated on **gross value** (market value as determined by GMO valuation) ([Tume ya Madini — Royalty and Inspection Fees](https://www.tumemadini.go.tz/pages/mineral-royalties-and-inspection-fees-rates/), [Lexology — Mining duties, royalties and taxes in TZ](https://www.lexology.com/library/detail.aspx?g=398c68a3-74b3-441f-8888-169a3ffee73a)):

| Mineral | Royalty |
|---|---|
| Uranium | 5% |
| Diamonds, uncut gemstones, tanzanite (rough) | 5% |
| Gemstones (cut/processed including tanzanite cut) | 1% (gems) |
| Gold, copper, silver, platinum group metals | **6%** raw export / **4%** if sold to in-country refinery |
| Other metallic minerals (nickel, cobalt, iron ore, rare earths, graphite as metallic) | 6% |
| Coal | 1% if sold to domestic industry / 3% if exported |
| Industrial minerals (limestone, gypsum, salt, soda ash, phosphate, sand, dimension stone, building materials) | **3%** |
| Salt produced renewably with environmental levy paid | **Exempt** |
| Gem | 1% |

Refer to the official rates page: [Tume ya Madini — Royalties and Inspection Fees Rates](https://www.tumemadini.go.tz/pages/mineral-royalties-and-inspection-fees-rates/).

### 3.2 Inspection / clearance fee
- **1% of gross value** as a clearance/inspection fee on every consignment cleared for domestic use or export ([TanzaniaInvest — 1% Clearing Fee on Mineral Exports](https://www.tanzaniainvest.com/mining/1-percenyt-clearing-fee-mineral-export)).
- This is the "**6% royalty + 1% inspection**" gold framework many owners refer to.

### 3.3 Effective burden on unrefined gold export (illustrative)
≈ **9.4%** — 6% royalty + 2% WHT + 1% inspection + 0.3% service levy + 0.1% HIV levy ([Lexology — Mining duties, royalties and taxes](https://www.lexology.com/library/detail.aspx?g=398c68a3-74b3-441f-8888-169a3ffee73a)).

### 3.4 Local Government Service Levy
- Up to **0.3% of turnover** payable to the district/municipal council where the mine sits; separate from royalty and income tax.

### 3.5 BoT Domestic Gold Purchase incentives (effective 1 July 2024 / notice 8 Oct 2024)
- **Royalty 4%** (reduced from 6%), **inspection 0%**, **VAT 0-rated** ([FB Attorneys — BoT Gold Programme](https://fbattorneys.co.tz/bot-issues-notice-on-gold-purchase-program-in-tanzania/)).

## 4. Mineral Trading and Export Rules

- **Mandatory MTC channel:** Trading of gold, tin, diamond, tanzanite, and coloured gemstones must occur at Mineral Markets and Buying Centres ([Tume ya Madini](https://www.tumemadini.go.tz/pages/mineral-royalties-and-inspection-fees-rates/)).
- **Who can buy:** Licensed Dealers and authorised refineries (Geita Gold Refinery, Mwanza Precious Metals, Eyes of Africa) ([FB Attorneys — BoT Gold Programme](https://fbattorneys.co.tz/bot-issues-notice-on-gold-purchase-program-in-tanzania/)).
- **Who can export:** Mineral right holders + Dealer Licence holders + non-residents (with Special Export Permit) ([Tume ya Madini — Procedures for Exportation of Minerals](https://www.tumemadini.go.tz/pages/procedures-for-exportation-of-minerals/)).
- **Mandatory 20% domestic set-aside for gold** under *Mining (Minerals and Mineral Concentrates Trading) (Amendment) Regulations* (effective 1 July 2024) — failure means **no export permit issued** ([FB Attorneys — BoT Gold Programme](https://fbattorneys.co.tz/bot-issues-notice-on-gold-purchase-program-in-tanzania/), [Bloomberg — TZ wants 20% gold refined/traded locally](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-06-12/tanzania-wants-large-miners-to-refine-trade-20-of-gold-locally) — 2025-06-12).
- **Required documents at exit:** Export Permit; Certificate of Origin (tanzanite); **Tanzania Kimberley Process Certificate** (diamonds); ICGLR Certificate (Sn/Ta/W); GMO valuation receipt; surrender of permit to Customs/Postmaster at exit ([Tume ya Madini — Procedures for Exportation](https://www.tumemadini.go.tz/pages/procedures-for-exportation-of-minerals/)).
- **Note on "biometric/fingerprint":** The specific biometric step is implemented at MTC valuation registration (linked to KYC of the seller/broker) — codified in MTC operating procedures rather than the primary Act; treat as a workflow detail Boji should mirror in its MTC pre-flight tool.

## 5. Foreign Exchange Rules (Critical for Mining Owners)

The single biggest 2024-2025 shift for in-country pricing.

- **Legal basis:** *Finance Act 2024* amended the **Bank of Tanzania Act** by inserting **section 26(2)**, making it an offence to transact in any currency other than TZS in Tanzania ([Breakthrough Attorneys — Foreign Currency Ban](https://www.breakthroughattorneys.com/foreign-currency-ban-what-tanzanian-businesses-need-to-know/), [PwC TZ — Press Room](https://www.pwc.co.tz/press-room/regulations-on-foreign-currency.html)).
- **GN 198 of 2025** (effective **28 March 2025**) — *Foreign Currency Usage Regulations 2025* ([Clyde & Co — New regulations on foreign currency use](https://www.clydeco.com/en/insights/2025/03/new-regulations-on-foreign-currency-use-tanzania) — 2025-03; [Auditax International](https://auditaxinternational.co.tz/tanzania-issues-foreign-currency-usage-regulations-2025-gn-no-198-of-2025/)):
  - **All pricing, invoicing, and payment for goods/services within Tanzania must be in TZS.**
  - A seller cannot refuse TZS for any goods/services.
  - **Existing FX-denominated contracts** must be renegotiated into TZS within **12 months** of the effective date — otherwise they **become void**.
  - **Permitted FX uses (schedule):** Government payments to regional bodies, diplomatic missions/IOs, foreign-currency loans from local banks/FIs, duty-free shop purchases, certain export transactions and offshore obligations.
- **Implication for mining owner:** Cannot legally invoice a TZ dealer in USD even though LBMA gold price is USD; must convert via published BoT rate at invoice date and account for FX risk. **Exports** can still settle in USD with offshore counterparties; export-retention permitted in foreign currency in CRDB/NMB/Stanbic FCY accounts.
- **BoT also published the Foreign Exchange Regulations 2022/2023** amendments tightening reporting on Bureau de Change ([Clyde & Co — New amendments 2023](https://www.clydeco.com/en/insights/2023/09/banking-in-tanzania-new-amendments-to-the-foreign)).

## 6. Environmental Protection Plan (EPP) and EIA

- **EPP** is the lighter-touch instrument for **PML holders** — application for PML must be accompanied by a baseline environmental investigation and an EPP to mitigate impacts ([World Bank — TZ SMMRP EPP Guide](https://documents1.worldbank.org/curated/en/277301468113344662/pdf/E47700SMMRP0P100Box385436B00PUBLIC0.pdf)).
- The Mining Act 2010 **exempts PML small-scale miners from the full EIA** route ([Lexology — Environmental regulations for mining in TZ](https://www.lexology.com/library/detail.aspx?g=6bc13cfe-92e6-4592-a8e5-b5c4e87824d0)).
- **EIA** is mandatory for **ML and SML** — submitted to and approved by **NEMC** under the *Environmental Management Act 2004* and *EIA & Audit Regulations 2005* (updated 2024) ([Clyde & Co — Environment Management Regulations 2024](https://www.clydeco.com/en/insights/2024/07/environment-management-regulations) — 2024-07).
- **Rehabilitation Bond:** **Regulation 207** of the *Mining (Environmental Protection for Small-Scale Mining) Regulations* requires mineral right holders to deposit a Rehabilitation Bond with the Minister for Minerals to cover mine-closure costs ([Mwebesa Law — Competing Bonds](https://mwebesalaw.co.tz/a-legal-quagmire-of-competing-bonds-prioritize-environmental-security-or-regulatory-efficiency/)).
- **Known gap:** NEMC's enforcement framework is calibrated to EIAs; EPPs sit awkwardly outside the Environmental Management Act regime — Boji should flag this as a compliance grey zone for PML clients.

## 7. Village/District Interaction, Surface Rights, CSR

- **Land Act 1999 + Village Land Act 1999** govern surface tenure; the **Mining Act 2010** governs subsurface mineral rights — and **the Mining Act takes precedence** once minerals are found ([Rights and Resources — Land Factor in Gold Mining](https://rightsandresources.org/blog/ipp-media-the-land-factor-in-gold-mining-reserves-in-tanzania/)).
- **Compensation:** Holder pays "fair and reasonable compensation" to the lawful occupier for surface improvements (not for the land itself) before entry ([Lexology — Mining rights and title in TZ](https://www.lexology.com/library/detail.aspx?g=65e7d403-d1fa-4b19-bbba-a556170f9064)).
- **Village minutes** are required as evidence the village council was consulted: minutes naming the project, area, compensation schedule, and CSR commitments, signed by the village chairman and VEO.
- **CSR under s.105 Mining Act + Mining (CSR) Regulations 2023** ([Bowmans — Mining CSR Regulations 2023](https://bowmanslaw.com/insights/tanzania-the-mining-corporate-social-responsibility-regulations-2023/)):
  - CSR Plan must be **jointly approved with the LGA** where the operations sit.
  - Original allocation: **40% to village/street, 60% to district/town/municipal council**.
  - **High Court (March 2026)** invalidated Reg 4(4)(a) and (b) as ultra vires the Mining Act ([African Mining Market — TZ CSR Allocation Invalidated](https://africanminingmarket.com/tanzania-high-court-invalidates-corporate-social-responsibility-allocation-framework-under-mining-regulations/25116/) — 2026-03). The CSR plan obligation remains; the fixed 40/60 split is now negotiable.
- **CSR plan annual reporting** to the Mining Commission with budget vs. actuals.

## 8. Local Content Regulations (2018, amended 2019, 2022, 2025)

### 8.1 Definitions
- **Indigenous Tanzanian Company (ITC):** as amended by **GN 563 of 2025** — a company incorporated under the Companies Act with at least 20% equity held by Tanzanian citizens, **80% of senior management** Tanzanian, and **100% of non-managerial staff** Tanzanian ([Clyde & Co — Amendment GN 563 of 2025](https://www.clydeco.com/en/insights/2025/09/amendment-to-the-mining-local-content-regulations) — 2025-09-12; [Dentons — Reserved goods and services](https://www.dentons.com/en/insights/alerts/2025/november/27/mandatory-reservation-of-goods-and-services-in-the-mining-sector-for-indigenous-tanzanian-companies)).
- **Reserved-list ITC:** Some goods/services must be supplied **only by ITCs that are 100% Tanzanian-owned** (Regulation 13A introduced in 2025) ([ALN — Local Content 2025 Key Changes](https://aln.africa/insight/mining-local-content-amendment-regulations-2025-key-changes-and-implications/)).

### 8.2 Operational obligations
- Mandatory **JV with an ITC** for non-indigenous suppliers — ITC must hold **minimum 20%** equity in the JV ([Mondaq — TZ Local Content JV Rules](https://www.mondaq.com/mining/1762932/tanzania-mining-local-content-regulations-2025-what-foreign-investors-must-know-about-the-new-jv-rules)).
- For reserved goods/services, **only 100% ITCs** — no JV cures non-indigenous participation.
- **Commission must pre-approve JV agreements** before mining activities commence (new in GN 563 of 2025).
- **Sole-sourced contracts > USD 10,000 equivalent** must be notified to the Commission.
- **Procurement Plan:** Annual (was quarterly) projection of contracts.
- **Pre-tender disclosure** at EOI stage, with **minimum 7-day** submission window.
- **Local-content plan**: deemed approved after **50 working days** if Commission silent.
- **At least 5% ITC equity** in a mining company itself.

### 8.3 Employment, training, technology transfer
- Succession plan for non-Tanzanian roles; mandatory training plan filed annually.
- Employment quotas as per Local Content Regs 2018 (still in force, subject to GN 563 overlays): targets ramp from 80% → 100% Tanzanian for non-managerial within stipulated phases.

## 9. Transfers, JVs, State Participation, Listing

- **Transfers of mineral rights** require **prior written consent of the Minister** (Mining Act ss.8-12); a transfer to a non-citizen of a PML is void.
- **Inheritance:** PML can pass to a Tanzanian heir on death but must be re-registered.
- **JV / farm-out:** Permitted but the JV agreement must be filed with the Commission and is now (2025) subject to pre-approval under local content rules.
- **State Participation (GN 939 of 2022):** *Mining (State Participation) Regulations 2022* ([Bowmans — State Participation Regulations 2022](https://bowmanslaw.com/insights/tanzania-mining-state-participation-regulations-2022-published/), [Clyde & Co — Free Carried Interest TZ](https://www.clydeco.com/en/insights/2024/02/the-concept-of-free-carried-interest-tanzania) — 2024-02):
  - **16% non-dilutable free-carried interest** for the Government in every ML/SML.
  - Government may scale up to **50%** via reversionary value, tax-for-equity swaps, or negotiation.
  - Holders of ML/SML had **90 days** from 2022 publication to notify the Commission and start JV/Framework Agreement negotiations.
  - Governance rights: state nominees on board, info rights, dividend rights.
- **Framework Agreement** is the master contract between Government and Investor for SMLs ([Clyde & Co — Framework Agreement TZ](https://www.clydeco.com/en/insights/2024/11/tanzania-mining-framework-agreement) — 2024-11).
- **No mandatory DSE listing**, but Government may negotiate listing requirements within the Framework Agreement.

- **73 additional licences revoked** Nov 2025 to reallocate to youth mining programmes ([The Citizen — 73 Mining Licences Revoked](https://www.thecitizen.co.tz/tanzania/news/national/tanzania-revokes-73-mining-licences-in-renewed-crackdown-on-dormant-operators-5276816), [BizLens — 73 Licences Cancelled](https://thebizlens.co.tz/2025/11/25/tanzania-cancels-73-inactive-mineral-licences-to-boost-youth-mining-programmes/) — 2025-11-25).
- **227 mining licence applications revoked** (May 2024) for non-payment of fees and missing documentation; new mandate: **no ML/SML without local value-addition plan** ([TanzaniaInvest — 227 revoked + local value addition](https://www.tanzaniainvest.com/mining/ministry-revokes-mining-license-applications-and-declares-no-new-licenses-without-local-value-addition)).

- **Illegal mining penalties** under Mining Act + AML Act + Penal Code; smuggling carries seizure + imprisonment.

## 11. Cross-Border Comparison (Snapshot)

- **Ghana — GoldBod (Act 1140 of 2025):** sole authority for ASM gold licensing, aggregation, assay, and export; effective **1 May 2025**, operating outside is criminal ([Clinton Consultancy — GoldBod Update](https://clintonconsultancy.com/2025/04/16/clintonconsultancy-com-goldbod-regulatory-update-2025/), [Lawyard — Ghana Gold Board Act 2025](https://www.lawyard.org/blog-articles/ghana-gold-board-act-2025-a-bold-step-to-transform-the-nations-gold-sector/)). Ghana also pursuing a **three-tier rights regime** (large/medium/ASM) with shorter licence terms.
- **DRC:** Tightening compliance, local-ownership and employment quotas; ASGM formalisation push, target export of **15-18 tonnes gold in 2026**; provincial-level ASM cooperatives via SAEMAPE.
- **Kenya:** Mining Act 2016 + Mining (ASM) Regulations 2017 — Cooperative-based ASM Permits; State Department for Mining now using an online cadastre (similar pattern to TZ).
- **South Africa:** **Mineral Resources Development Bill (2025)** released for public consultation — would replace MPRDA 2002 framework; ASM has long been informal and the Bill creates a more explicit ASM permit regime ([AHK — Africa Mining Regulatory Reform](https://www.ahkgroup.com/africa-mining-regulatory-reform-what-investors-must-know/)).
- **Zambia:** Issued **680 ASM rights in 2024** (up from 304 in 2023); new dedicated ASM department; **Local Content Regulations** drafted for 2025 enactment ([Veridicor — ASM in Zambia](https://veridicor.com/2025/09/09/artisanal-and-small-scale-mining-in-zambia-sector-analysis/)).
- **Common porting hooks for Boji:** Cadastre integration (most are now online), gold-board / single-buyer programmes (Tanzania's BoT, Ghana's GoldBod), CSR plan obligations (TZ s.105, Ghana community mining), local content (TZ 2018/2025, Ghana new bill, Zambia 2025 drafts) — Boji's local-content compliance engine, royalty calculator, and CSR plan generator are highly portable.
